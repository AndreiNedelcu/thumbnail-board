This video documents the design and creation of a portable Mac Mini dock that incorporates an iPad Mini as a secondary display. The creator, building on a previous Mac Studio design, explores improvements to an earlier portable Mac Mini concept by leveraging the Mac Mini's compact size and power efficiency. The project involves 3D printing a custom enclosure that mimics classic Macintosh aesthetics, complete with a handle for portability. A critical component is a 20,800 mAh power bank integrated into the design to run both the Mac Mini and charge the iPad simultaneously. After testing at a coffee shop for three hours of 4K video editing, the battery retained 46% charge, demonstrating Apple Silicon's energy efficiency. However, the creator concludes the portable version is impractical for most users, weighing nearly seven pounds—equivalent to two 14-inch MacBook Pros—making it too heavy for backpack transport and prone to thermal concerns in warm environments. The video targets makers and Apple enthusiasts interested in creative hardware modifications and 3D printing projects, while emphasizing that the stationary Mac Mini dock remains a more practical solution for desk-based workflows.
