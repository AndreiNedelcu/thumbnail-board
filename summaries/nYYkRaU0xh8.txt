This video explores 21 cognitive biases and thinking errors that unconsciously influence human decision-making and judgment. Drawing heavily from Daniel Kahneman's "Thinking Fast and Slow," the content systematically examines phenomena like cognitive dissonance (holding conflicting beliefs), the spotlight effect (overestimating how much others observe us), anchoring bias (being influenced by initial numbers or information), and the halo effect (allowing one positive trait to overshadow other judgments). Additional biases covered include the gambler's fallacy (expecting patterns in random events), confirmation bias (seeking information that supports existing beliefs), the Baader-Meinhof phenomenon (noticing things more after learning about them), and the Zeigarnik effect (remembering incomplete tasks). The video uses accessible examples—from job interviews to romantic relationships to business negotiations—demonstrating how these mental shortcuts work. Targeted at viewers interested in psychology, critical thinking, and self-improvement, the content aims to help audiences recognize when these biases operate in their own minds, making them more rational and thoughtful decision-makers despite being unable to eliminate these hardwired cognitive patterns entirely.
