This video explores winter fashion through the lens of "city boy" style, a Japanese aesthetic term coined by Popeye magazine in the mid-1970s that blends American prep, British workwear, French style, and minimalist influences. The creator provides an in-depth historical analysis of classic winter pieces, beginning with the duffel coat—tracing its origins from Belgium through British military adoption to its popularization by figures like Field Marshal Montgomery and subsequent cultural icons. The discussion extends to Fair Isle sweaters, examining their Scottish heritage and resurgence in contemporary styling, particularly as layering pieces under suiting. Vintage alpine hiking boots from brands like Paraboot, Danner, and Eddie Bauer receive attention for their 1960s aesthetic and current popularity. Throughout, the creator emphasizes how military and workwear garments have transitioned into civilian fashion while maintaining practicality. The video targets fashion enthusiasts interested in menswear history, vintage clothing, and Japanese-influenced style, offering practical recommendations for sourcing these pieces from both new retailers and secondhand markets like eBay, with attention to quality, fabric composition, and price points.
