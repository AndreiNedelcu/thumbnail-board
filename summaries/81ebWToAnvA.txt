The creator builds physical mazes and tests whether water can solve them, comparing the results to a popular online simulation. Water navigates mazes through two distinct mechanisms depending on scale: in smaller mazes, air pressure trapped in dead-end paths prevents water from entering wrong turns, making it solve the maze perfectly; in larger mazes, surface tension becomes negligible and water instead follows a gravity-based algorithm, taking the lowest available path until blocked by geometry, filling more incorrect routes before finding the solution. The creator fabricates both small and large versions from laser-cut acrylic layers, encountering challenges with watertight construction and exploring how surface tension accumulates across multiple points in the maze, eventually halting water flow before complete saturation. The video demonstrates how the same physical system produces different solving behaviors based on physical parameters like scale and surface tension, revealing that the online simulation likely operates under different physical assumptions (possibly without air resistance). This appeals to viewers interested in physics, engineering, creative problem-solving, and hands-on experimental visualization of abstract concepts.
