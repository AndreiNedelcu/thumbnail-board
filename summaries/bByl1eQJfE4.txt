# Corridor Crew VFX Analysis Summary

Professional visual effects artists critically examine the five Oscar-nominated films for Best Visual Effects: Wicked, The Brutalist, Kingdom of the Planet of the Apes, Dune: Part Two, and Alien: Romulus. The episode features detailed breakdowns of each film's signature sequences, with emphasis on how production design and VFX blur together in Wicked's meticulously constructed sets (featuring 9 million real tulips) and practical builds, while discussing the animal animation challenges across multiple ape-centric films and the complexities of wet fur simulation in water scenes. The team explores the distinct technical approaches: Wicked's hybrid practical-digital integration, Kingdom of the Planet of the Apes' photorealistic multi-character work by WETA, Better Man's complete CG body replacement with simulated costumes and challenging lens flare compositing, Dune Part Two's dust atmospherics and explosion design, and Alien: Romulus' particle destruction sequences. The episode targets VFX professionals and enthusiasts interested in industry-standard techniques, software like Houdini, and the decision-making process behind award-winning visual effects. The discussion balances technical admiration with critical assessment of which film deserves the Oscar based on pure visual effects achievement.
