Po-Shen Loh, a mathematician and social entrepreneur at Carnegie Mellon University, argues that education must shift from teaching students to solve known problems toward developing creative, independent thinking skills. As AI tools like ChatGPT become ubiquitous, the critical skill is learning to "grade homework"—evaluating and building upon AI suggestions rather than merely reproducing learned answers. Loh illustrates this through the matchstick puzzle example, demonstrating how innovative problem-solving requires three-dimensional, unconventional thinking. He critiques traditional test preparation industries that prioritize memorization over flexibility, instead advocating for curricula using competition-style problems students haven't encountered before. Loh describes his own scalable ecosystem combining three groups: middle school learners, advanced mathematics students, and drama-trained coaches—creating a win-win model where high school students gain Broadway-quality instruction while drama performers find stable part-time work. His philosophy emphasizes direct engagement with communities through city park talks and school visits to understand real pain points. Ultimately, Loh positions creative thinking and entrepreneurial value-creation as essential skills for thriving in an AI-driven world, targeting educators, parents, and students seeking education beyond conventional academics.
