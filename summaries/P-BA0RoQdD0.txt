This video investigates caffeine content in ready-to-drink canned coffee products from four countries using a specialized caffeine analyzer. The host tests blind samples from the UK, Japan, Korea, and the USA, then attempts to guess caffeine levels through taste alone before revealing measured results. The analyzer measures caffeine in milligrams per 100 milliliters and chlorogenic acid levels, costing approximately five pounds per test. Results show significant variation within and across regions: UK products averaged 33.7 mg per 100ml, Japan averaged 53 mg, Korea 29 mg, and the US notably higher at 66 mg on average. Notable findings include a UK Frappuccino with minimal caffeine compared to concentrated Japanese Boss Coffee requiring dilution, and Korean black coffees showing surprisingly low caffeine despite appearing strong. The video concludes that consumers cannot reliably determine caffeine content through taste alone, highlighting the importance of standardized labeling for caffeinated beverages. The content targets coffee enthusiasts and consumers interested in understanding caffeine consumption and beverage analysis.
