# Summary

This investigation examines claims made by Rabbit, a startup that raised $30 million in venture capital and pre-sold $20 million worth of its R1 "Orange Box" device, which allegedly contains an AI system called Lamb capable of autonomously controlling applications and managing daily tasks. The creator tests whether Lamb actually exists as described or if it's a scam, discovering that the device fundamentally relies on ChatGPT, hardcoded automation scripts using Playwright (a web automation tool), and the Perplexity search engine—not a proprietary foundational AI model. When tested, the device fails basic tasks from its marketing materials, like ordering food or calling rides, and breaks whenever applications update their interfaces. Despite these failures, Rabbit CEO Jesse Lyu initially presented Lamb as an intelligent system that understands and adapts to websites, not rigid scripts. The investigation reveals concerning privacy vulnerabilities in the codebase, poor geolocation tracking, and an anonymous employee's claim that "Lamb was a marketing term." The core critique is that Rabbit built its value proposition on misleading claims about non-existent AI technology, selling consumers an expensive device based on false promises rather than delivering genuine innovation in autonomous app control.
