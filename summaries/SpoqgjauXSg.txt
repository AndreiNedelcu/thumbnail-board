# Summary

This video explores the Western Interior Seaway, a prehistoric shallow ocean that covered much of North America during the Late Cretaceous period, particularly during the Campanian Age (83.6-72.1 million years ago). The Seaway was home to a terrifying array of marine predators that far exceeded modern ocean dangers, earning it the nickname "Hell's Aquarium." The primary focus is on mosasaurs—massive aquatic reptiles including species like Tylosaurus (up to 52 feet long) and Mosasaurus (up to 56 feet), which dominated as apex predators with powerful jaws and specialized hunting tactics. The video also details other nightmarish creatures including Deinosachus, an enormous crocodilian with a bite force exceeding that of a T-Rex; giant cephalopods like Parapuzosia, the largest ammonite ever known; and fearsome sharks such as Oxyhrina, an aggressive hunter of other megafauna. Through detailed descriptions of these creatures' anatomy, hunting behaviors, and dietary preferences based on fossil evidence, the video paints a vivid picture of why this prehistoric seaway was arguably the deadliest ocean environment in Earth's history, targeting audiences interested in paleontology, prehistoric marine life, and natural history.
