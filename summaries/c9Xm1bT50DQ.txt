# WeWork: The Rise and Fall Summary

This video chronicles the spectacular collapse of WeWork, a company that once valued itself at $47 billion but ultimately filed for bankruptcy amid accusations of being a cult-like organization. The narrative begins with founder Adam Neumann's unconventional background—raised in communes and kibbutzim—before following his entrepreneurial journey from failed ventures like collapsible heel shoes and baby knee pads to co-founding Green Desk with Miguel McKelvey in 2008. After discovering the co-working space market gap during the financial crisis, Neumann and McKelvey sold Green Desk and launched WeWork in 2010 with ambitious expansion plans. The video details how Neumann marketed WeWork not as a real estate company but as a "physical social network" and tech platform comparable to Uber or Airbnb, using this framing to attract venture capital. It explores the company's cult-like workplace culture, where underpaid young employees worked extreme hours fueled by promises of stock options and being part of a world-changing movement, while Neumann staged elaborate "activate the space" parties to impress investors. The narrative sets up the analysis of how a simple co-working concept was transformed through aggressive expansion, questionable financial practices, and messianic leadership into a cautionary tale of startup excess.
