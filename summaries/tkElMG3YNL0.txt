The video documents an attempt to climb the world's longest man-made climbing route, a six-pitch progression built by Red Bull at the Verzasca Dam in Switzerland (220 meters tall, famous from the James Bond film Golden Eye) for elite competition climbers. The protagonist and climbing partner Cedar Wright, a renowned expedition and free solo climber, work through increasingly difficult pitches culminating in a 7c+ final crux, encountering technical challenges including jumps, slopers, crimpers, and jamming sections. Throughout the ascent, they employ sport climbing techniques with rope protection, navigate route-specific beta shared by Cedar who commentated the competition, and manage both physical and mental demands of sustained multi-pitch climbing on a deliberately sandbagged route designed for world champions and Olympic winners. The video targets climbing enthusiasts interested in high-level technical climbing, expedition narratives, and the contrast between different climbing disciplines, offering insights into professional-grade route design, climbing strategy, and the psychology of attempting elite-level challenges.
