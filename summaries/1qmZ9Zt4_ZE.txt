The video follows a runner attempting to break the world record for the fastest marathon run in Crocs shoes during his first-ever marathon attempt. Starting with the existing record of three hours and two minutes, the protagonist must maintain a pace of approximately 6 minutes and 51 seconds per mile while wearing budget-friendly footwear that cost only £40, in stark contrast to the high-tech running shoes worn by professional competitors. The video documents the full 26.2-mile journey, highlighting the runner's initial confident pace—so fast he gained a two-minute buffer early on—before hitting the infamous "wall" around mile 20, where severe pain, blisters, and cramping nearly derail his attempt. Throughout the race, he interacts with fellow runners and receives encouragement from spectators and support team members, including conversations with other marathoners and interactions with the crowd. The content appeals to sports enthusiasts, endurance challenge viewers, and those interested in humorous attempts at breaking records through unconventional means, combining athletic determination with comedic underdog storytelling as the runner battles physical agony to achieve his childhood dream of breaking a world record.
