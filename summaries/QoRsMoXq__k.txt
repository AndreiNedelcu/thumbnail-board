A senior developer reviews a frontend engineer's resume with four years of experience, identifying critical mistakes that prevent job callbacks and explaining the mindset needed for effective technical CVs. The reviewer critiques the resume's structure, excessive length (two pages instead of one), vague professional summary filled with buzzwords, irrelevant work experience unrelated to software engineering, and poor formatting of contact information and dates. Key recommendations include condensing the summary to two concise lines highlighting relevant technologies like React, TypeScript, and AWS; expanding the most recent position to showcase concrete achievements with specific technologies used; merging fragmented consulting roles into one larger position; reorganizing sections to prioritize professional experience; and replacing generic language with specific technical details and quantifiable results. The reviewer emphasizes removing non-technical background information, eliminating outdated skills, and restructuring the skills section into languages, technologies/frameworks, and interests. The target audience includes junior and mid-level frontend developers seeking to improve their hiring prospects and understand how recruiters and hiring managers evaluate resumes, with the core message that relevance, specificity, and clear technical accomplishments matter far more than padding or business jargon.
