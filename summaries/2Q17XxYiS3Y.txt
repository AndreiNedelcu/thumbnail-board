# Summary

This video examines the mysterious founder of Mindgeek, the parent company controlling Pornhub and numerous other adult content platforms, tracing an investigative journey to unmask Bernard Bergman, an Austrian businessman who built a multibillion-dollar empire while remaining deliberately invisible to the public. The investigation begins with New York Times and Financial Times exposures in 2020 revealing that Pornhub hosted videos of sexual assault, abuse, and child exploitation, prompting payment processors Visa and Mastercard to cut ties. Despite Mindgeek's dominance—receiving 15 terabytes of daily uploads and generating $460 million in annual revenue—Bergman had orchestrated his complete erasure from public records and search engines. The video traces his rise from a modest Austrian farming background through University of Chicago business school to acquiring Pornhub's parent company, exploring his connections to predecessors like Fabian Thorman who pioneered digital adult content consolidation. Through metadata analysis of his wife's Instagram and social media, investigators located Bergman in a London mansion and confronted him, breaking his years of anonymity. The narrative examines how the platform's inadequate content moderation enabled the hosting of non-consensual and child abuse material while generating enormous profits, illustrating the conflict between Bergman's obsessive privacy and the public exploitation his company enabled, targeting audiences interested in corporate accountability, internet privacy, and investigative journalism.
