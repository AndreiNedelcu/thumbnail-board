A professional climber with minimal gym training experience experiments with creatine supplementation for two months to assess its effects on strength and climbing performance. Starting at 70.2 kg, the athlete documents daily intake of approximately 7-14 grams of creatine monohydrate while tracking measurements and testing progressions in bench press and weighted pull-ups. Over the initial weeks, he gains 2-4 kg primarily through water retention, experiences nausea from the supplement, and initially feels clumsy on the climbing wall due to the added weight. However, by late December, after returning from military training, he reports feeling stronger and more powerful, noting improved explosiveness and muscular sensation. By the two-month mark at 75.5 kg, he demonstrates significant physical gains: biceps increased from 36 cm to 39 cm, chest expanded from 103 cm to 111 cm, and crucially, he achieves a new bench press personal record of 112.5 kilos (surpassing his two-month goal of 110 kg) and improves weighted pull-ups to over 68 kg. He concludes that creatine provides measurable strength benefits for climbers seeking to build complementary gym strength, though acknowledges the initial adjustment period and water weight accumulation. The video targets climbing enthusiasts and fitness enthusiasts interested in supplementation research and strength progression documentation.
