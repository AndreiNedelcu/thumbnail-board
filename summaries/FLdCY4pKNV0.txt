# Video Summary

This video documents the complete journey of designing and manufacturing the Encore keyboard, a custom mechanical keyboard combining wood and metal aesthetics. Matthew Anedin walks through his collaborative process with Mode, a high-end keyboard company, from initial concept exploration using mood boards and sketches through CAD modeling in Fusion 360 to final production. The design draws inspiration from vintage Cherry keyboards while incorporating a minimalist aesthetic, featuring a 65% layout with oak or walnut wood paired with anodized aluminum or copper components. Key challenges addressed include manufacturing constraints around wood chipping, tolerance issues, acoustic resonance in copper bottoms (solved with custom gasket solutions), and side profile simplification through iteration with manufacturer feedback. The video also covers packaging design using debossing and spot UV techniques, and comprehensive marketing strategy involving product photography, press releases, streamer reviews, and keyboard community meetups. Throughout, Anedin shares practical design principles including establishing design language, studying reference materials, sketching extensively, and balancing creative vision with manufacturing pragmatism. The content targets aspiring product designers, mechanical keyboard enthusiasts, and entrepreneurs interested in bringing physical products to market using platforms like Shopify for commerce.
