# Summary

This video examines how two Barcelona presidents systematically dismantled one of football's greatest clubs over a fifteen-year period, leaving it with €1.35 billion in debt. Sandro Rosell initiated the decline starting in 2010 by prioritizing commercialization over sporting values, abandoning the club's youth academy philosophy, and facilitating the controversial Neymar transfer involving hidden payments. His successor Josep Bartomeu catastrophically worsened the situation from 2014-2020 by squandering €222 million from Neymar's sale on failed signings like Ousmane Dembélé (€105 million), Philippe Coutinho (€160 million), and Antoine Griezmann (€120 million), while simultaneously inflating wages to an unsustainable €600 million annually. The club's wage-to-revenue ratio became untenable, with players like Coutinho earning €400,000 weekly while underperforming. Meanwhile, Barcelona suffered humiliating Champions League eliminations against Roma and Liverpool, and an 8-2 defeat to Bayern Munich. COVID-19 compounded financial losses, and the debt ultimately forced Barcelona to sell Lionel Messi in 2021 despite his willingness to take pay cuts. The video targets football enthusiasts and business-minded viewers interested in organizational failure, corporate mismanagement, and how poor leadership destroys institutional excellence.
