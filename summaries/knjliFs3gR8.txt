# Summary

This video provides a minute-by-minute account of Operation Neptune Spear, the May 2011 Navy SEAL Team 6 raid that killed Osama bin Laden in Abbottabad, Pakistan, after nearly a decade of searching. The narrative covers the intelligence gathering phase beginning in 2010, President Obama's decision to authorize the high-risk mission despite skepticism from security advisors, and the tactical execution involving roughly 24 SEALs, supporting personnel, and a Belgian Malinois military dog named Cairo. The video details the unexpected helicopter crash during insertion, the team's progression through the three-story compound encountering resistance and civilians, conflicting accounts of bin Laden's final moments, and the rapid 15-minute operation from landing to neutralization. It also covers post-mission procedures including the destruction of the damaged helicopter, bin Laden's body being transported to the USS Carl Vinson, his burial at sea according to Islamic rites, and President Obama's late-night announcement to the American public. The content targets military history enthusiasts, those interested in special operations tactics, and viewers seeking detailed procedural breakdowns of significant historical events.
