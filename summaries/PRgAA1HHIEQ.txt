This documentary investigates the conflict between mining corporations and Native American communities over a massive copper deposit in Arizona's Oak Flat, set against the broader context of green energy transition. The video examines how Resolution Copper, a joint venture between multinational giants BHP and Rio Tinto, obtained rights to extract 40 billion pounds of copper through a controversial 2014 land swap secretly embedded in military funding legislation by senators with corporate ties. The story contrasts the promises of 1,500 jobs and economic revival in the struggling town of Superior with the harsh realities of boom-and-bust mining cycles, environmental devastation including 1.2 billion tons of toxic tailings and massive water consumption during a severe drought, and the displacement of the San Carlos Apache tribe, for whom Oak Flat holds deep spiritual and cultural significance. The video targets viewers concerned with environmental justice, indigenous rights, corporate accountability, and the hidden costs of green energy infrastructure, drawing parallels between mining communities across America left impoverished and polluted after resource extraction. It frames the conflict as fundamentally about who profits from economic development and who bears the true costs.
