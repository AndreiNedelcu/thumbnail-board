# Summary

This episode discusses Microsoft's troubling gaming division developments, particularly rumors of potential Xbox studio closures and divestments amid strategic restructuring. The hosts examine why Microsoft spent billions acquiring gaming studios like Double Fine, Ninja Theory, and Compulsion Games only to shut them down, questioning the business logic behind this pattern—noting that Embracer Group follows a similar acquisition-and-closure strategy. The conversation centers on Double Fine's recent releases, analyzing Steam player metrics showing Kiln peaked at 193 concurrent players despite launching as an Xbox exclusive less than two months after years of development, and Keeper's similarly poor performance. They contrast these with Psychonauts 2's more robust engagement, discussing how Game Pass subscriptions may harm visibility on other platforms like Steam and reduce emotional investment compared to purchased games. The hosts express frustration that new Xbox leadership initially promised revitalization before rumors emerged suggesting the opposite—potential divestment or spin-off rather than meaningful investment. They debate whether poor studio performance justifies closures versus questioning management's overall strategy, highlighting the challenging economics of modern game development where most titles fail financially while a handful achieve massive success.
