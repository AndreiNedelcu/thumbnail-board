This video examines the new Aeropress Clear brewer and what it signals about the company's future direction. The Clear model, made from Triton material to address previous durability issues, costs $50 compared to the original Aeropress's historical $30 price point. The creator conducts stain resistance tests by leaving coffee in the brewer for extended periods and subjecting it to heat, confirming the material resists discoloration and passes durability drop tests. However, he notes concerning changes under new ownership: the box now contains only 100 paper filters instead of 350, the funnel is no longer included, and pricing on accessories like the flow control filter cap ($25) appears aggressive compared to competing products. Looking ahead, Aeropress is releasing an XL model—twice the original volume—and a premium glass-and-metal version, both departing from founder Alan Adler's original philosophy of accessibility and simplicity. The video targets coffee enthusiasts and Aeropress users, offering practical assessment while raising questions about whether these product extensions align with the community that embraced the brewer for its affordability and excellent coffee-making capability.
