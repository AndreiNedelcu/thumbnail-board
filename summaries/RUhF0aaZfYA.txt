# Summary

This video explores whether the iPad Pro remains relevant in 2022, addressing how it compares to the newly released MacBook Pro. The creator, a longtime iPad Pro user, argues that despite owning both devices, they serve distinct purposes and remain complementary rather than redundant. The iPad Pro excels for drawing, design work with Procreate, photo editing in Lightroom and Affinity apps, note-taking with Apple Pencil, and media consumption. The MacBook Pro is superior for video editing in Final Cut Pro, advanced Photoshop work, and external display integration. The video features interviews with other content creators discussing their iPad workflows, revealing diverse use cases from ADHD-friendly single-app focus to creative professional work. A central concern emerges around iPadOS's untapped potential—despite powerful M-series hardware, the software hasn't evolved significantly to justify yearly upgrades or fully leverage the device's capabilities. The creator expresses hope that iPadOS 16 will introduce pro features like Final Cut Pro or enhanced multitasking, though acknowledges this seems unlikely. Ultimately, the iPad Pro remains his preferred daily device despite its limitations, valued for its unique capabilities, portability, and the enjoyable creative experience it provides.
