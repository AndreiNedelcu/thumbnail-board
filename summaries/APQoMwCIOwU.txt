# Video Summary

This video presents a Japanese urban legend from the 1970s about a train driver named Tekashi Itito and his assistant working a cargo train through a desolate, snowy region of Hokkaido. While traveling through brutal winter conditions at night, the two men strike a woman standing on the tracks, unable to stop the train in time. Upon investigating, they discover her body split in two, with her upper half positioned on the snow with eyes open and an expression that Tekashi interprets as accusatory. After the assistant departs to find help, Tekashi remains alone with the heating vent open, attempting to rest despite traumatic intrusive thoughts. He then hears an unexplained grinding metallic sound growing progressively louder and closer to the train, causing him to scream when something enters through the door. When police and the assistant return an hour later, Tekashi has vanished from the train with no explanation. The video targets fans of horror folklore and mysterious narrative storytelling, presenting this story as a cautionary tale from Japanese oral tradition with multiple interpretations of truth. The narrator creates atmospheric tension through detailed scene-setting and builds psychological dread leading to an unexplained supernatural element.
