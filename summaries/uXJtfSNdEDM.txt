# Video Summary

A music producer discovers that a platinum-certified song by Mexican OT titled "Johnny Dang" used a drum loop from his free "Nightmare" drum kit without permission, credit, or payment. After learning the track garnered over 125 million streams and went platinum, he decides to take action—not through legal confrontation but by contacting the artist's team in good faith. Through negotiation calls with Mexican OT's manager Brandon and CEO Greg, he discovers the beat was created spontaneously during a studio session when a producer played instrumentals for OT, who chose that one without realizing a sample pack loop was involved. The video documents the entire resolution process, including conversations about fair compensation and the revelation that Greg himself lost out on five platinum records as a producer. Ultimately, the video concludes with the artist deciding to celebrate the achievement rather than pursue aggressive legal action, launching a collaborative "Platinum Pack" melody pack and inviting fans to submit beats for potential inclusion on Mexican OT's next project. This story appeals to music producers, beat makers, and content creators interested in sample clearance issues and music industry negotiations.
