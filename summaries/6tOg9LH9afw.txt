# Summary

On April 15, 1992, Lisa Ziegert, a 24-year-old teacher's aide and evening shift worker at Britney's Card and Gift Shop in Agawam, Massachusetts, disappeared during her shift. The next morning, police discovered her abandoned car at the shopping plaza, her belongings still inside, and evidence of a struggle with blood on cardboard boxes in the back room. Detective Wayne Macy led the investigation, noting scuff marks suggesting the victim had been dragged out through an unlocked back door. The crime scene offered limited clues—no security cameras, no eyewitnesses, and the cash register remained untouched, ruling out simple robbery. The last register transaction at 8:20 p.m. narrowed the attack window to 40 minutes before closing. Three days later, searchers discovered a woman's body in nearby woods showing signs of sexual assault and multiple stab wounds. Initial investigation focused on people close to Lisa, including her boyfriend Blair and coworker Ed, though interviews revealed no clear motive. The case's geographic and forensic elements—muddy terrain, tire tracks, and potential DNA evidence—suggested the killer was a local with four-wheel-drive vehicle knowledge.
