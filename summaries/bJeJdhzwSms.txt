This video documents a content creator's attempt to master Flame, Autodesk's professional VFX editing software used on Hollywood productions like Avatar and Inception, costing £462 monthly or up to £1,124 for three years. Despite paying significantly more than Adobe Creative Cloud, the creator discovers Flame is designed exclusively for Mac and operates on a node-based system fundamentally different from consumer software like Premiere Pro. Over approximately two weeks, he struggles intensely with basic tasks—importing footage, creating timelines, and particularly green-screen keying—repeatedly hitting performance issues and confusing workflows despite watching comprehensive tutorials. The software's steep learning curve and complex node graphs prove overwhelming; after failing to accomplish his ambitious goal of creating a tribute music video using Flame's effects, he concedes defeat and completes the project in Premiere Pro in just two hours. The video targets aspiring video editors curious about professional-grade tools while serving as a cautionary tale about software complexity, ultimately conveying respect for Hollywood professionals who master such demanding software while reinforcing that extensive training and dedication are essential for tools engineered for experts rather than general users.
