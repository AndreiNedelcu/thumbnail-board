# Summary

This video examines the feasibility of powering the global economy entirely with clean energy by analyzing current energy consumption patterns and technological constraints. The presenter reveals that in 2023, only 8.5% of global primary energy came from low-carbon sources, with fossil fuels dominating electricity generation and all energy sectors combined. He explores the scale of renewable transition needed, calculating that powering the world with wind alone would require approximately 30-60 million turbines depending on storage solutions. However, the analysis becomes more optimistic when accounting for thermal efficiency losses in fossil fuel generation and the massive energy savings from electrifying transport and heating systems. Electric vehicles and heat pumps achieve significantly higher end-use efficiency than combustion-based alternatives, potentially reducing global primary energy demand by 40%. Drawing on UK climate projections, the presenter demonstrates that efficiency gains from electrification outweigh energy demand growth, meaning a fully decarbonized system requires less total primary energy than today. While 90-95% clean energy is achievable, reaching 100% faces technological barriers in aviation, shipping, and industrial processes, requiring strategic deployment of carbon capture and green hydrogen for remaining emissions rather than complete elimination.
