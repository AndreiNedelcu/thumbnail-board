This video documents an ambitious metalcasting project exploring electromagnetic induction through a custom-built copper sphere. The creator fabricates a large truncated icosahedron-shaped copper vessel using ceramic shell casting techniques, melting nearly 100 pounds of copper to create a hollow structure designed to contain a powerful 2-inch N52 neodymium magnet. The core experiment investigates whether the magnet will levitate inside the spinning copper sphere due to eddy currents—electrical currents induced in the copper that create opposing magnetic fields, producing a damping effect similar to magnetic braking. The video demonstrates this phenomenon through slow-motion footage showing the magnet's delayed fall and attempted rotation experiments. The creator details the entire manufacturing process, including 3D printing molds, creating ceramic shells through multiple dipping stages, kiln firing, metal pouring, sandblasting, drilling, filing pentagonal holes for rotation plugs, and polishing copper facets. While the magnet does exhibit levitation at specific rotation speeds, the effect proves difficult to sustain consistently. The target audience includes engineering enthusiasts, physics educators, metalcasting hobbyists, and viewers interested in hands-on demonstrations of electromagnetic principles combined with impressive craftsmanship.
