This video examines Russia's strategic push to establish control over the Arctic region through military expansion, territorial claims, and resource extraction. The analysis covers Russia's 2023 UN-validated claim to 1.7 million square kilometers of Arctic seabed containing vast oil and gas reserves, alongside its dramatic military buildup. Russia has reopened over 50 Soviet-era bases, modernized its Northern Fleet with nuclear-capable submarines and hypersonic missiles, and maintains the world's largest icebreaker fleet, outnumbering NATO bases in the region by three-to-one. The video emphasizes Russia's strategic control of the Northern Sea Route—which it treats as internal waterway despite international usage—and its geopolitical implications for NATO. Climate change accelerating Arctic ice melt will make this passage increasingly viable, intensifying competition for resources and trade routes. The geopolitical context shifts dramatically following Russia's invasion of Ukraine, as Finland and Sweden have joined NATO, surrounding Russia with alliance members and escalating tensions in a region historically characterized by cooperation. The video targets audiences interested in geopolitics, military strategy, resource competition, and contemporary international relations.
