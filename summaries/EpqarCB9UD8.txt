# Summary

This video traces the creation and rise of Minecraft from its 2009 origins as a passion project by Swedish programmer Markus "Notch" Persson. Beginning as "Ruby Dung," the game evolved after Persson discovered Infiniminer, which inspired him to develop a voxel-based sandbox game focused on mining and crafting. After posting an alpha version to the TIG Source indie game forum in May 2009, Minecraft gained rapid community traction through iterative development driven by player feedback. Persson transitioned from hobby development to commercial release with an innovative pricing model rewarding early adopters, eventually founding Mojang with colleagues Jacob Porser and Carl Manet to handle the game's explosive growth. With sound designer C418 joining the team, Minecraft reached 700,000 sales before its official November 2011 release at the first MineCon convention. Microsoft acquired Mojang for $2.5 billion in 2014, making Persson a billionaire. The video addresses both Minecraft's phenomenal success—becoming the best-selling video game ever with over 300 million copies sold—and Persson's controversial legacy following offensive social media posts that led Microsoft to distance itself from him, raising questions about accountability and redemption in gaming culture.
