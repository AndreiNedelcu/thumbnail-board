This video reviews the Makita coffee maker, a battery-powered brewing device manufactured by the power tool company Makita, examining whether its novelty justifies its practical limitations. The reviewer conducts a detailed technical analysis, explaining how the machine's 190-watt power draw rapidly depletes batteries—a standard 22 watt-hour battery only provides seven minutes of brewing for approximately 160 milliliters of coffee. The coffee produced is competent but unremarkable, comparable to budget domestic filter coffee makers, with water temperatures matching those of cheap Mr. Coffee-style machines. The reviewer demonstrates that carrying the necessary battery weight (210 grams per battery) to produce a modest coffee serving makes little practical sense compared to alternatives like an Aeropress or thermos, especially since most building sites have accessible power supplies. The video also features a battery-powered Makita kettle requiring two large batteries simultaneously. The analysis targets coffee enthusiasts, tool users, and viewers curious about absurd product design, ultimately concluding the machine has virtually no genuine use cases where it outperforms simpler, lighter alternatives, though the reviewer acknowledges its entertainment value and invites audience commentary about real-world usage.
