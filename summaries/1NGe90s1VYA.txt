# Summary

The creator redesigns three modern brands—Nutella, Beyond Burger, and 5-Hour Energy—to appear as if they were created between the 1920s and 1950s. For Nutella, he transforms the modern minimalist design into a vintage glass jar label featuring limited color palettes, ornate borders, serif fonts, and aged textures like surface wear and frayed edges. The Beyond Burger becomes a vintage tin can with a charming watercolor-style pea mascot dressed as a rancher, emphasizing the product's plant-based origins through playful imagery. The 5-Hour Energy reimagined as a glass bottle features a clock-like circular logo with lightning bolts and period-appropriate typography. Throughout the process, he applies authentic aging effects using a custom texture toolkit with hundreds of effects including dust, corrosion, chips, and paper deterioration to make the designs look genuinely decades old. The video targets design enthusiasts, branding professionals, and anyone interested in how modern aesthetics compare to historical design conventions, blending humor with legitimate design principles.
