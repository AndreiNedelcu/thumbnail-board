This video introduces three foundational game theory tactics applicable to real-world strategic interactions. It begins by defining game theory as the mathematical study of decision-making under uncertainty when multiple parties with competing interests interact. The presentation uses three key examples to illustrate practical concepts: the Cold War's arms reduction treaties demonstrate how apparently zero-sum competitive situations can yield mutual cooperation through incremental interactions; poker serves as an accessible model for understanding optimal decision-making amid uncertainty and probability assessment; and the sunk cost fallacy highlights a critical cognitive bias where people continue investing in failing endeavors due to past commitments rather than future outcomes. The video emphasizes that breaking large complex interactions into smaller stages can transform negative-sum situations into mutually beneficial ones, a principle applicable to personal relationships and negotiations. The minimax strategy for zero-sum games is introduced as a way to minimize maximum loss against sophisticated opponents. The content targets general audiences seeking practical applications of game theory to everyday decisions, relationships, and professional challenges, making abstract mathematical concepts accessible through relatable examples.
