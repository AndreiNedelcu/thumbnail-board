# Summary

This documentary traces the unconventional career of Nardwuar (John Ruskin), a eccentric Vancouver-based punk rocker and journalist who revolutionized music interviewing through his unique approach and extensive research. Beginning with his first interview at age seventeen and evolving through his work at university radio station CITR, Nardwuar founded the punk band The Evaporators while simultaneously establishing himself as a political reporter at government press conferences. His interviewing style combines theatrical presence, deep biographical research, and personalized gift-giving to create memorable encounters with major artists like James Brown, Snoop Dogg, and others. The video chronicles both his successes and numerous confrontational interviews where guests misunderstood his intentions, including hostile encounters with Blur and Sonic Youth that resulted in property damage. Despite facing skepticism, bullying, and serious health challenges including a brain hemorrhage and stroke, Nardwuar remained committed to his craft. The film positions him as a misunderstood pioneer whose unconventional methods and genuine curiosity eventually gained respect within the music industry, appealing to audiences interested in music history, journalism, and counterculture figures.
