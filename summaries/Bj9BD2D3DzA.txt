This video explores interpretability research into large language models, specifically examining how to understand and observe AI's internal reasoning processes. Rather than treating AI as an inscrutable black box, the researchers developed methods to trace the model's "thoughts" by analyzing internal neural circuits, using poetry generation as a case study. When Claude composes a poem line, researchers can observe it internally considers words like "rabbit" before writing, showing the model plans ahead and explores multiple completion paths. By intervening on these circuits—dampening certain concept activations—they can steer the model toward different outputs, providing evidence that models genuinely reason through problems rather than simply pattern-matching. The work parallels neuroscience's approach to understanding biological brains, aiming to make AI systems more transparent and controllable. This research targets researchers, AI safety engineers, and those interested in understanding AI cognition, emphasizing that better interpretability tools are essential for building more reliable and safer AI systems that behave as intended.
