# Summary

This video documents a 72-hour experiment where the creator uses nasal dilators—both external Breathe Right strips and internal nasal dilators—to explore whether forcibly opening the nostrils can improve breathing, sleep, athletic performance, and overall health. The creator tests two main product types: adhesive strips worn on the outside of the nose and small inserts placed inside the nasal cavity, alternating between them to avoid skin irritation. Throughout the experiment, he monitors heart rate variability (HRV), tracks subjective experiences during workouts and sleep, and notes changes in energy levels, cognitive function, and breathing comfort. The video explores the scientific basis for nasal breathing benefits, including increased nitric oxide production, improved oxygen transport, and enhanced parasympathetic nervous system activation. After 72 hours, the creator removes the dilators and observes whether the changes persist. Key findings include improved sleep quality and workout performance while wearing dilators, but less conclusive long-term benefits afterward, leading him to conclude that occasional use may be beneficial while avoiding potential dependence. The target audience includes health-conscious individuals interested in biohacking, athletic performance optimization, and breathing pattern improvement.
