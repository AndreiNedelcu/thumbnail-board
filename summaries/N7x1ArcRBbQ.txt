# Summary

This video demonstrates graphic design techniques by repackaging items from popular fast food chains to make them appear more premium. The creator redesigns a Taco Bell taco, Subway sandwich, and McDonald's apple pie, showing how strategic use of color, typography, illustration, texture, and layout can dramatically shift consumer perception of value. Rather than relying on stereotypical expensive design elements like gold leaf and ornate fonts, the creator employs cohesive design principles such as well-executed illustrations, natural materials (cutting boards, farm scenes), vintage aesthetics, and careful visual hierarchy. Key concepts discussed include kerning, ambient occlusion, color grading, drop shadows, and how realistic versus painted imagery affects perceived quality. The video targets graphic design enthusiasts and aspiring designers interested in branding and packaging design, while also appealing to general audiences curious about marketing psychology and how design influences purchasing decisions. The creator uses Squarespace to build a portfolio showcasing early design work, illustrating how packaging design has evolved throughout their career.
