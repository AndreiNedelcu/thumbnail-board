This video documents an ambitious engineering challenge to create a drone capable of filming a complete lap of a Formula 1 car at race speed. The project begins with a straightforward speed comparison between a prototype racing drone and Max Verstappen's F1 car, which quickly reveals that sustained high-speed filming around a full circuit presents vastly different challenges than straight-line acceleration. The team iteratively develops and tests increasingly sophisticated FPV drone prototypes, encountering technical hurdles including overheating electronics, signal loss, and aerodynamic instability caused by the car's wake turbulence. After multiple failed attempts and engineering consultations with Red Bull's F1 design team—employing advanced materials and aerodynamic optimization—they successfully capture smooth footage around Silverstone with test driver Liam Lawson, then attempt the ultimate test with three-time world champion Max Verstappen driving the new RB20. The video targets motorsports enthusiasts and engineering-minded audiences, demonstrating how cutting-edge drone technology can reveal new perspectives on Formula 1's incredible speeds and cornering capabilities while showcasing the iterative problem-solving required to push both automotive and aerial technology to their limits.
