This video documents a PC build challenge performed while wearing ASUS ROG XR R1 gaming glasses that display a third-person perspective view on a 171-inch virtual screen. The builder must assemble a gaming PC using an AMD Ryzen 7 7700X processor, B850 motherboard, RTX 5060 Ti graphics card, and Noctua cooler while viewing themselves from an external camera angle, creating a disorienting but gamified experience. The glasses feature a microOLED 240Hz display with electrochromatic lenses and connect via DisplayPort Alt Mode directly from an iPhone 17 Pro, eliminating the need for external converters. Throughout the build, the presenter demonstrates the glasses' functionality, including floating and anchored display modes for gaming, 3D content viewing, and spatial locking capabilities. The video highlights both the technology's impressive display quality and practical limitations, such as latency issues and battery drain from powering the phone, glasses, and 4K recording simultaneously. Targeting tech enthusiasts and gamers interested in AR/VR wearables and PC hardware, it showcases how innovative display technology can transform routine tasks into engaging experiences while evaluating real-world usability for gaming and productivity applications.
