This video documents a designer's journey bringing a physical product to market through a collaboration between his creative studio and Grovemade, a Portland-based desk accessory company. The creator outlines the Double Diamond design methodology, a four-phase iterative process consisting of discover, define, develop, and deliver. During discovery, the team conducted environmental studies and surveys to identify pain points in note-taking workflows. In the define phase, they narrowed insights into a clear design challenge: creating a better system to capture, review, and store notes. The develop phase involved extensive sketching, prototyping with cardboard and 3D printing, and material testing to perfect dimensions and functionality. The deliver phase focused on manufacturing, sourcing, and production logistics. Beyond design, the video covers critical go-to-market activities including product naming, messaging strategy, and marketing execution through existing social media channels and email lists. The resulting product—a note-taking kit comprising a dotted notepad, display rail, and vertical organizer in multiple material finishes—targets creative professionals and productivity-focused individuals seeking organized workspace solutions. This comprehensive overview appeals to entrepreneurs, designers, and content creators interested in product development and business launch strategies.
