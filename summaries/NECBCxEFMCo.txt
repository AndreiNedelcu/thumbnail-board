The video examines whether foldable smartphones, specifically book-style designs that fold in half, have reached their peak potential or remain viable. The reviewer analyzes the Oppo Find N5, the world's thinnest foldable, featuring a Snapdragon 8 Elite processor, 5600mAh battery (using silicon carbide technology), and displays reaching 2,450 nits brightness. The N5 measures just 8.93mm thick when closed, with a 6.6-inch exterior screen that functions like a standard flagship phone and unfolds into an 8.1-inch interior display with more screen area than an iPad Mini. The video targets tech enthusiasts and early adopters interested in foldable technology. While acknowledging the hardware achievements, the reviewer notes foldables still face compromises: thermal limitations from extreme thinness, inferior cameras compared to traditional flagships, square-shaped interior screens less practical for media consumption, and premium pricing. The analysis concludes foldables aren't "cooked" but rather evolving into specialized segments—book-style foldables for multitasking and flip phones for compact form factors. The reviewer identifies price reduction as the next frontier for mainstream adoption.
