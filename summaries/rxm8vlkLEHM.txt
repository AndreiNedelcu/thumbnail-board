# Summary

This video addresses why traditional planning methods fail for chronic procrastinators, particularly those with ADHD, and presents practical solutions. The creator explains that the core problem isn't planning itself but rather following through on schedules, often due to time blindness (difficulty perceiving time accurately) and fluctuating energy levels that standard productivity systems ignore. He introduces the dual-calendar method, where you plan tasks in one calendar and log actual activities in another, creating patterns that reveal realistic time estimates and peak energy periods. The video also tackles decision paralysis caused by overwhelming task lists and presents Amplenote's task-scoring system, which automatically prioritizes tasks based on urgency, importance, frequency of review, and dependencies rather than requiring manual ranking. The creator emphasizes that effective time management requires tracking both time and energy levels, aligning demanding work with personal peak performance windows, and adopting flexible systems that encourage observation rather than perfectionism. Aimed at people struggling with procrastination, ADHD, burnout, and conventional productivity advice, the video positions itself as a practical alternative to discipline-based approaches.
