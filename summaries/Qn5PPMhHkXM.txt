# Summary

This video presents a systematic three-phase training model for scaling a creative agency by teaching others to replicate the founder's expertise and decision-making processes. The speaker outlines the "I do, We do, You do" framework borrowed from educational pedagogy: the "I do" phase involves shadowing and observation where trainees watch the leader work and learn their methods; the "we do" phase transitions responsibility through collaborative client work where the trainee begins taking the lead while the founder remains present; and the "you do" phase grants full autonomy with the trainee as primary point of contact and decision-maker. The key to successful delegation involves identifying candidates with core skills, drive, and communication abilities, then scaffolding their development through increasingly complex responsibilities. The speaker emphasizes that entrepreneurship fundamentally depends on teaching—transferring values, principles, and problem-solving approaches to others—rather than remaining a bottleneck. By systematically removing himself from day-to-day operations and empowering team members like his designer Tom to build direct client relationships, the founder freed himself to focus on strategic growth. This approach targets aspiring entrepreneurs, agency owners, and managers seeking to escape being a "company of one" and build scalable, systems-driven businesses.
