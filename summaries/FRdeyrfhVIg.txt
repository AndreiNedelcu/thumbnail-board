# Summary

This video recounts the 1983 kidnapping of Freddy Heineken, CEO of the Heineken brewery, and his chauffeur in Amsterdam—one of Europe's most meticulously planned abductions. A gang of five Dutch criminals, led by 26-year-old Cor van Hout (nicknamed "Flip"), spent over two years preparing the operation with a budget of approximately 100,000 guilders. After selecting Heineken as their target due to his wealth and personal connection—van Hout's father had been mistreated by the Heineken family—the gang executed the kidnapping in 35 minutes, holding their victims in hidden concrete cells built inside a converted industrial shed. The criminals demanded 35 million guilders in ransom, carefully calculated based on how much cash they could physically carry. The video details their sophisticated multi-phase operation, including deliberate misdirection tactics (planted evidence suggesting German involvement), one-way communication through newspaper ads, and an elaborate ransom handoff involving multiple locations, instructions buried at various points, and vehicle transfers across the Netherlands and Belgium. The narrative highlights the gang's methodical planning against the police's scrambling response, with particular emphasis on the complex logistics of moving half a ton of currency undetected. This account, based on interviews conducted by investigative journalist Piet Bakker with the perpetrators themselves, presents the kidnapping as a masterclass in criminal organization and psychology.
