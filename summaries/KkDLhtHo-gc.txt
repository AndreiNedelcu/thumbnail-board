# Summary

This video examines Pantone's controversial 2022 decision to revoke its color licensing from Adobe, which caused millions of designers' Photoshop and Illustrator files to display their colors as solid black. The video traces Pantone's history from its 1950s origins as a printing subsidiary to its rise as the industry standard through the Pantone Matching System (PMS), which standardized color reproduction across manufacturers worldwide using numbered color swatches. The company's business model relied on selling annual color guide books, but in 1998 when Adobe integrated Pantone support into Photoshop, Pantone leveraged this dependency to force users into paid digital subscriptions alongside their expensive physical guides. By revoking Adobe's licensing rights, Pantone essentially held designers' existing work hostage, requiring them to purchase both products to maintain color accuracy. The video targets graphic designers and creative professionals affected by this licensing dispute, presenting it as a case study in corporate greed where a billion-dollar company exploited its monopolistic control over a fundamental design tool to increase revenue.
