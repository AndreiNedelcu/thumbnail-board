# Summary

This video documents filmmaker Isaac Carlton's attempt to create an action movie filmed entirely in one continuous shot, inspired by the cinematic technique popularized in films like 1917. The project centers on a home invasion scene featuring a fight sequence culminating in an actor breaking through a specially constructed wall—a stunt Carlton dreamed of since age 13. Working with friends Jake and Tyler, Carlton tackles the unique technical and logistical challenges of single-take filmmaking, including choreography, camera movement, neutral density filters for exposure management, and precise timing. The production requires extensive rehearsal since any mistake necessitates resetting the entire shot from the beginning. After nine attempts, Carlton captures the sequence but must still manage post-production audio by re-recording all sound effects to achieve professional quality. The video explores how shooting without editorial cuts fundamentally changes the filmmaking process, eliminating the ability to correct mistakes in post-production while demonstrating why most films rely heavily on editing. Carlton's project targets aspiring filmmakers and cinephiles interested in understanding both cinematic technique and the practical realities of one-shot filmmaking.
