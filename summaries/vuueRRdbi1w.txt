# Summary

This video explores why time perception changes throughout life and how to feel like time passes more slowly. The creator discusses research showing that subjective time (how we perceive duration) differs from objective time, influenced by emotions, boredom, and flow states. He introduces the "Holiday Paradox," a psychological phenomenon where experiences that feel quick in the moment later feel longer in memory, and vice versa—because memories create the sense of expanded time. The video targets people interested in personal development and psychology, offering three evidence-based strategies to slow down time: seeking "anchors of novelty" by breaking routines with new experiences, creating "anchors of reflection" through journaling and photography to preserve memories, and practicing mindfulness or meditation to enhance present-moment awareness. The underlying principle is that novel experiences and focused attention generate more distinct memories, which retroactively expand our perception of how much time has passed, helping us feel less like life speeds away.
