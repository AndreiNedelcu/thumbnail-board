# Summary

This video chronicles the 2016 Bitfinex cryptocurrency exchange hack—valued at $4.5 billion when stolen bitcoins later appreciated—and the couple who executed it. Raphael Icolangelo created Bitfinex in 2012, which became a major trading platform but suffered from inadequate security despite partnerships with BitGo. In August 2016, hackers exploited a fatal flaw in their API key system to steal 120,000 bitcoins worth $71 million at the time. The narrative then shifts to Heather Morgan and her Russian-born husband Ilia Lichtenstein, documenting their rise from ordinary entrepreneurs to money launderers. Heather, who falsified credentials and used social engineering throughout her career, met Ilia through startup accelerators. As authorities traced stolen funds through the blockchain in 2017, the couple began cashing out through dark web markets like AlphaBay while living luxuriously in New York. Ironically, Heather simultaneously launched a rap music career, performing poorly-produced songs that occasionally referenced their criminal activities. The video targets cryptocurrency enthusiasts and true crime audiences, detailing the sophisticated money laundering schemes and blockchain forensics that eventually led to their capture by the FBI.
