This video explores whether a falling coin can actually be lethal, challenging conclusions from MythBusters and Veritasium. The creator focuses on Australian 50-cent coins—much heavier and sharper than pennies—and investigates how spin affects trajectory and terminal velocity. He builds a wind tunnel using a leaf blower to measure how fast coins fall, discovering that spinning coins on their edge achieve terminal velocities exceeding 270 km/h, compared to around 60 km/h for flat-falling coins. To simulate impact, he constructs a custom coin launcher using spear gun components and pink rubber bands, achieving projectile speeds of 230 km/h. He then tests penetration power on plywood and a pig head, demonstrating the coin can pierce through both, before conducting a final drop test from a 45-meter dam with spin-launched coins. The video targets physics enthusiasts and debunking-curious viewers interested in combining myth-testing with practical engineering, featuring engineering challenges, custom fabrication sequences, and playful commentary throughout.
