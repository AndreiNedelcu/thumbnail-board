# Summary

This video examines the events surrounding Lee Harvey Oswald's employment at the Texas School Book Depository and the circumstances immediately preceding President Kennedy's assassination on November 22, 1963 in Dallas. The narrative traces how Oswald secured his job through a casual chain of connections—a conversation between neighbors about employment led to his hire in mid-October, just six weeks before Kennedy's visit. The video details Oswald's daily work routine, his quiet demeanor among colleagues, and the critical hours of the morning of the assassination. It explores the presidential motorcade route selection, noting how its final path through Dealey Plaza past the Depository was contingent on venue choices made only days before. The video documents eyewitness accounts from workers in the building, including contradictory reports about Oswald's location and activities, mysterious sightings of armed men in the building's windows, and the logistical details of the Depository's layout. It targets viewers interested in assassination history, forensic analysis of conspiracy theories, and detailed historical investigation, presenting information grounded in witness testimonies and official records while highlighting unexplained discrepancies in the historical record.
