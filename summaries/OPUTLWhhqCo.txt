This documentary follows an eight-day expedition into Krubera Cave in the South Caucasus, the world's deepest known cave system at 2,199 meters explored depth. The filmmaker joins a team of 11 international speleologists, including experienced Russian cavers led by Gennady Samokhin, to map unmapped sections and search for undiscovered animal species in this extreme underground environment. The expedition involves descending through multiple camps while navigating dangerous obstacles including 105-meter vertical wells, water-filled siphons with no air pockets, claustrophobic rock squeezes, icy temperatures around 3°C, and the constant risk of flooding or rockfalls. The team burns up to 8,000 calories daily, uses sophisticated rope and harness systems for safety, and faces multiple life-threatening situations requiring precise technical skills. Advanced technology like 3D laser rangefinders and NVIDIA RTX GPUs are employed to create detailed maps of the cave's topography. The documentary targets adventure and science enthusiasts interested in extreme exploration, speleology, and the physical and psychological challenges of underground research, while highlighting the remarkable geological formations and potential for discovering new species in Earth's deepest accessible environments.
