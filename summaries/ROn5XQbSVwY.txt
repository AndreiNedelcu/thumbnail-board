This video documents the creation of a custom $35,000 epoxy dining table for a yacht, representing the creator's largest and fastest commission to date. The project involves sourcing massive book-matched wood slabs (3.4m by 1.4m), pouring large-scale epoxy, and managing tight two-month deadlines under significant pressure. Key technical challenges include maintaining wood symmetry with thin book-matched cuts, properly leveling and curing the epoxy pour using heated blankets, and ultimately discovering that one corner became too thin during surfacing. The creator responds by salvaging offcuts to create nearly invisible wood patches as a structural fix. The video targets furniture makers, woodworkers, and viewers interested in high-end custom craftsmanship, addressing both technical problem-solving and pricing philosophy. The creator openly discusses his premium pricing strategy—charging as much as possible while remaining confident in the value—and acknowledges the trade-offs between maximizing individual project profit and building long-term customer relationships, ultimately defending transparency about both costs and mistakes as part of quality custom work.
