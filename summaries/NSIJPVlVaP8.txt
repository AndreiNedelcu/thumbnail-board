On March 24, 2015, Germanwings Flight 9525 crashed into the French Alps, killing all 150 people aboard, including a school class returning from a field trip. Investigation of the cockpit voice recorder and flight data revealed that the co-pilot deliberately hijacked the aircraft by locking the captain out of the cockpit while he was away, then initiating a controlled descent into the mountain. The 27-year-old co-pilot had a history of severe depression and mental health crises; he had lied on FAA applications about his psychiatric diagnosis, received multiple sick notes declaring him unfit to fly, and had researched the A320 cockpit door locking mechanism days before the flight. The video examines how post-9/11 security measures designed to prevent terrorist hijackings became tools for this intentional act, and raises questions about aviation safety protocols, mental health screening for pilots, and the ethical dilemmas surrounding doctor-patient confidentiality when public safety is at risk. It targets viewers interested in aviation incidents, mental health issues, and detailed forensic analysis.
