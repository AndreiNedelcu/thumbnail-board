# Summary

This video investigates the color blindness glasses industry, specifically examining viral marketing campaigns and product claims made by companies like EnChroma, Pilestone, and Kust. The creator, who is colorblind himself and has professionally worked with color correction for over six years, conducted a three-month investigation uncovering alleged deceptive marketing practices, staged reaction videos, fake reviews, and compromised scientific research. He details a highly successful 2015 marketing campaign by paint manufacturer Valspar and EnChroma that generated 800 million views through emotional user-generated videos. The creator then tests multiple brands of color blindness correction glasses and finds they don't deliver promised results—instead producing intense pink/magenta tints that make color discrimination worse rather than better. He discovers that review systems are compromised with companies filtering negative feedback, most five-star reviews come from test-takers rather than customers, and at least one viral video featured balloons with written color labels to stage a child's success. The investigation targets consumers skeptical of viral wellness products and those interested in exposing manipulative marketing and scientific misrepresentation in the medical aids industry.
