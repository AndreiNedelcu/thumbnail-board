# Summary

The video examines Apple's struggles to deliver on its artificial intelligence promises despite being the world's largest tech company. Apple announced "Apple Intelligence" at WWDC 2024 as its AI initiative across iPhones, iPads, and Macs, featuring writing tools, generative emoji, image creation, and a significantly improved Siri. However, months after the iPhone 16 launched specifically designed for these features, most promised capabilities remain unshipped with no clear timeline. The core issue is a massive disconnect: Apple is aggressively marketing Apple Intelligence through commercials, billboards, and website promotions, yet has never publicly demonstrated the most important features actually working, including the revamped Siri with on-screen awareness and contextual app integration. The video draws parallels to tech giants like Nokia and BlackBerry that failed to adapt to paradigm shifts, arguing that AI being software rather than hardware eliminates Apple's typical second-mover advantage, developer enthusiasm may be limited due to reduced app control, and missing deadlines during a critical industry moment could signal deeper problems. The creator targets tech-savvy viewers and investors, questioning whether Apple's delays indicate the technology isn't ready or whether marketing hype has outpaced actual progress.
