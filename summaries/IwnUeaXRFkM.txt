# Video Summary

This is a scathing critical review of Disney's Snow White live-action remake, attacking the film's departure from the original story and its overall execution. The reviewer criticizes the casting of Rachel Zegler and Gal Gadot, arguing their performances are wooden and unconvincing, particularly Gadot as the vain queen who lacks believable motivation to be jealous of Zegler's Snow White. The film fundamentally reimagines the protagonist from an innocent damsel into a "girl boss" character seeking justice and leadership rather than true love, eliminating classic songs and replacing them with new music. Instead of hiring actors with dwarfism, Disney created CGI "magical creatures" described as nightmare-fuel abominations that alienate child audiences. The reviewer systematically breaks down plot changes—the prince becomes a thief, Snow White picks apples instead of flowers, and her character arc lacks genuine development. Visual quality is consistently criticized as cheap and obvious green screen work. The overall assessment is that the film represents everything wrong with modern Disney adaptations: pandering progressive messaging, tone-deaf casting choices, and the gutting of beloved source material. The video targets cinema enthusiasts and those critical of contemporary Hollywood's approach to beloved classics.
