# System Design Fundamentals

This comprehensive tutorial covers the foundational concepts needed to excel in system design interviews, starting with computer architecture and progressing through distributed systems principles. It begins by explaining how computer components—CPU, RAM, cache, and disk storage—work together, then transitions to production system architecture including CI/CD pipelines, load balancers, databases, and monitoring systems. The content addresses the critical pillars of system design: scalability, maintainability, and efficiency, while introducing the CAP theorem and its tradeoffs between consistency, availability, and partition tolerance. Key metrics like availability (measured in nines), throughput, and latency are explained with practical examples. The tutorial covers networking fundamentals including IP addressing, TCP/UDP protocols, DNS, and firewalls, followed by application layer protocols such as HTTP, WebSockets, SMTP, IMAP, and POP3. It emphasizes real-world considerations like SLOs and SLAs, reliability and redundancy, debugging strategies, and the importance of planning for failure. The material targets software engineers preparing for technical interviews or those seeking to understand how large-scale distributed systems are architected and maintained.
