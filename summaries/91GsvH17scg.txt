Leila Hormozi, CEO of Acquisition.com managing a $200 million-plus revenue portfolio across 16 companies, discusses her entrepreneurial journey and strategic evolution. She addresses the common misconception that past success automatically translates to easier future achievements, emphasizing instead that different challenges emerge at scale. A key focus is rebranding Acquisition.com as an independent entity separate from her husband Alex's personal brand, recognizing that most people associate the company with him despite her leading day-to-day operations. She plans to build the acquisition.com team into recognized personalities and content creators, similar to Barstool Sports' multi-personality model. Strategically, she's shifting from acquiring smaller companies with low return-on-effort ratios to focusing on acquiring platform companies—larger foundational businesses that can roll up smaller "nodes" beneath them, maximizing value extraction across the portfolio. Her approach emphasizes quarterly strategic planning, team development, and slowing down to make deliberate decisions rather than impulsively acting on opportunities. She's targeting two major platform acquisitions by end of 2024, prioritizing depth over breadth in her investment thesis.
