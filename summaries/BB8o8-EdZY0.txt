# Summary

A minimalist YouTuber conducts a comprehensive audit of his and his partner's possessions by counting and categorizing every single item in their home. He establishes clear ground rules for what qualifies as "one thing"—treating boxes of screws as single items, pairing socks together, and excluding consumable food—then methodically goes through their apartment documenting clothing, camera equipment, kitchen items, holiday decorations, electronics, and miscellaneous household goods. The final tally reaches approximately 1,600 items combined, with detailed breakdowns revealing that he unexpectedly owns more possessions than his partner (498 versus 461 items), largely due to his work-related camera gear. Through this exercise, he emphasizes that minimalism isn't about owning exactly 100 items or maintaining an extreme lifestyle, but rather about intentionality—ensuring that everything in one's home serves a purpose, gets used, and brings value. Though the counting process proved anxiety-inducing and exhausting, the creator concludes it was worthwhile for gaining peace of mind and understanding that despite owning more than expected, their household maintains genuine intentionality in consumption.
