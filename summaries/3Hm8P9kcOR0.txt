This video uses metaphorical relationship language to explain why users should migrate from Evernote to Obsidian as their note-taking application. The creator identifies three "toxic signs" in Evernote: invasive data tracking and privacy concerns (including contact integration and content analysis), accumulation of external information that drowns out personal thoughts, and loss of trust following the company's acquisition by Bending Spoons and subsequent staff layoffs. The video then presents three criteria for choosing a replacement tool: responsiveness (speed matching thought), trustworthiness (end-to-end encryption and local file storage), and future-proofing (avoiding vendor lock-in). The bulk of the video provides practical technical guidance for switching, including exporting notes from Evernote as .enex files, importing them into Obsidian using the community importer plugin, and organizing them either as a subfolder or separate vault. The creator emphasizes the philosophical difference between the two platforms—Evernote encourages information hoarding while Obsidian facilitates idea cultivation and personal knowledge management. The target audience consists of long-time Evernote users feeling dissatisfied with privacy practices and performance, seeking a more personal, locally-controlled alternative for knowledge work.
