# Summary

This video explains the collapse of Silicon Valley Bank in March 2023, the fastest bank failure since 2008, which occurred in approximately 40 hours. The video provides a forensic analysis of how a bank run—when depositors simultaneously withdraw funds—triggered the institution's downfall. Silicon Valley Bank primarily served venture-backed startups and tech companies, with roughly 150 billion dollars in uninsured deposits above the FDIC's 250,000 protection threshold. The collapse was initiated by a March 8th press release announcing a 1.8 billion dollar loss from forced asset sales, triggering panic among the startup community through founder networks and social media. Key factors included the bank's concentrated customer base of interconnected tech companies, excessive holdings of government bonds that lost value as interest rates rose, and the CEO's earlier stock sale signaling internal concerns. The video traces how a Moody's downgrade rating prompted desperate measures, how prominent investors like Peter Thiel withdrew funds first, and how panic spread virally through founder chat groups. It targets tech entrepreneurs and startup founders who were directly affected by the bank's failure and its implications for their businesses and employees.
