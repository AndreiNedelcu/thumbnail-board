This video explores nine common cognitive biases that distort human thinking and decision-making. It covers confirmation bias (seeking information that supports existing beliefs), anchoring bias (overrelying on initial information), attribution bias (misjudging others' motivations), the halo effect (letting one positive trait influence overall perception), availability bias (overweighting recent or vivid information), the Dunning-Kruger effect (overconfidence when knowledge is limited), the ostrich effect (avoiding unpleasant but useful information), negativity bias (dwelling on negative information), and the spotlight effect (overestimating how much others notice us). Each bias is illustrated with practical real-world examples such as Steve Jobs' iPad pricing strategy and financial avoidance behaviors. The video explains why these mental shortcuts evolved and offers practical strategies to counteract them, such as seeking diverse perspectives, gathering comprehensive information, and recognizing unhelpful thought patterns. The content targets a general audience interested in psychology, personal development, and decision-making improvement, presenting complex cognitive science concepts in an accessible, narrative-driven format suitable for educational and self-improvement contexts.
