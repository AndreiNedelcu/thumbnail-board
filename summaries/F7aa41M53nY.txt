# Summary

This documentary-style video explores Ryan Reynolds' journey from an anxious, introverted child in Vancouver to becoming a major Hollywood star, with particular focus on his relentless pursuit of bringing Deadpool to film. Reynolds reveals how growing up as the youngest of four boys in a strict household shaped his use of humor as a self-defense mechanism and coping tool for lifelong anxiety. The narrative traces his early acting career, breakthrough roles, and pivotal moment when he discovered the Deadpool character while filming Blade Trinity in 2004, immediately envisioning himself in the role. The video chronicles his eleven-year battle to secure studio backing for an R-rated Deadpool adaptation faithful to the comics, detailing how leaked test footage and overwhelming fan response finally forced Fox to greenlight the project in 2016. It also discusses his critically panned Green Lantern role, where he met his now-wife Blake Lively, and examines how his personal struggles with anxiety have actually enhanced his acting ability and creative expression. The video concludes by positioning Deadpool as Reynolds' ultimate outlet for channeling anxiety and vulnerability while maintaining humor, demonstrating how perceived liabilities transformed into professional assets, all balanced with his emphasis on family priorities.
