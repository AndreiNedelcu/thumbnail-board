# Summary

This narrative describes the investigation into a double homicide that occurred in Chicago on February 28, 2005, when federal Judge Joan Lefcow's husband Michael and her elderly mother Donna were shot and killed in the basement home office of their residence. The story opens with Joan receiving an urgent call from her mother, then shifts to the morning of the crime, detailing Michael's routine day at home while recovering from a leg injury. After Joan returns home to find them missing, she discovers the bodies in the basement and calls 911, triggering an FBI investigation led by Special Agent Douglas Seeum. The investigation reveals several puzzling details: a broken window suggesting forced entry, shell casings, gunshot wounds to both victims' heads, and mysteriously, beer bottles left at the crime scene—suggesting the killer may have consumed alcohol during or after the murders. Neighborhood canvassing yields a description of two suspicious men in a red car spotted earlier that day. As Seeum interviews Joan, he considers multiple theories about the motive: whether Michael or Donna were targets, but ultimately focuses on Judge Joan's position as the likely intended target, given the numerous cases and rulings she's made that could have generated serious enemies seeking revenge.
