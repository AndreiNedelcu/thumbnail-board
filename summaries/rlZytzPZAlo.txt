# White House Inauguration Day Operations

On Inauguration Day, roughly 100 White House staff members have less than six hours to completely transition the residence from one president to another—a logistically complex operation that begins planning immediately after the election. The White House Chief Usher oversees this massive undertaking, coordinating with the incoming administration through questionnaires covering everything from dietary preferences to desired décor. The 20,000-square-foot executive residence, containing approximately 20 bedrooms, 35 bathrooms, and numerous office spaces, must be entirely packed, cleaned, and refurbished while outgoing staff say goodbye to the departing family. Only after the exiting president leaves for inauguration around 10-11 a.m. does the frantic transformation begin, with teams simultaneously moving furniture, packing priceless historic artifacts stored in Maryland, repainting rooms, and personalizing spaces according to the new family's requests. Critical to this process is the discreet handoff of the "nuclear football"—the briefcase containing America's nuclear launch codes—which transfers to a new military aide at precisely noon. The operation is meticulously timed to completion by 5 p.m., before the new president returns from inaugural celebrations. This coordinated effort, carried out by the dedicated White House staff regardless of political affiliation, exemplifies the institution's commitment to seamlessly transitioning presidential administrations.
