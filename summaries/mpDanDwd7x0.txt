This video reviews the ASUS ROG Strix SCAR 18, a high-end gaming laptop featuring a custom-designed 18-inch 4K miniLED display with advanced motion clarity technology. The machine packs a 24-core Intel CPU, RTX 5090 GPU, and 128GB DDR5 RAM, but draws up to 320W power, making battery life impractical. The display's standout feature is ROG Nebula ELM (Extreme Low Motion Blur), a strobing technology inspired by CRT displays that uses 2,000 dimming zones divided into 40 rows to refresh sequentially rather than all at once, reducing retinal smearing and motion blur without perceptible flicker. The reviewer performs multiple motion clarity tests using game footage and the Blurbusters UFO pursuit camera test, comparing performance against OLED panels and Nvidia's G-Sync Pulsar technology. Testing reveals exceptional color accuracy (delta E values around 1.4-1.86), peak brightness reaching 1,600 nits in HDR, and 5.7ms display latency at 240Hz with ELM enabled. The video targets enthusiast gamers and tech professionals seeking cutting-edge display technology, offering detailed technical measurements alongside visual demonstrations of the laptop's motion performance capabilities.
