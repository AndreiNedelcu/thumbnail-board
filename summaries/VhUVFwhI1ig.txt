# Summary

This video explores horror games that move beyond cheap jump scares to create genuinely unsettling experiences through psychological and atmospheric terror. The creator analyzes several indie titles—Squirrel Stapler, The Salvation Project, Slide in the Woods, and No Players Online—identifying what makes them effective. Central themes include the fear of the unknown, the terror of the unstoppable, and "kopsía," the eerie emptiness of spaces designed for crowds. The video highlights how games like Squirrel Stapler use the countdown mechanic and vague dread ("God is coming") to instill primal fear, while The Salvation Project leverages paranoia and psychological manipulation through a character guiding players toward harmful actions. Other techniques discussed include maintaining unsettling atmospheres without dynamic changes, using sound design strategically, and exploiting player expectations rather than relying on jump scares. The creator targets horror enthusiasts tired of conventional scare tactics, demonstrating how thoughtful game design can create sustained dread through ambient tension, narrative mystery, and manipulation of player psychology rather than cheap shock tactics.
