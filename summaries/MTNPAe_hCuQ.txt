A detective investigates the attempted murder of Helen Pastor, the wealthiest woman in Monaco, who was shot along with her driver in Nice, France, in May 2014. After both victims fall into comas, the investigator must determine whether this was a random carjacking or a targeted hit. Evidence at the scene—including an untouched purse and shotgun buckshot—suggests professional intent, though the broad daylight execution and weapon choice seem amateurish. Suspicion falls on Helen's children, Sylvia and her boyfriend Voych, as well as her son Gildo, who stand to inherit billions and face potential financial motives. The case is further complicated by jurisdictional issues between French and Monégasque authorities. As the investigator interviews family members, he uncovers financial details: Helen provides monthly allowances of $500,000 to each child, and Gildo reportedly carries substantial business debt. The narrative examines how wealth, family dynamics, and inheritance can intersect with serious crime, targeting true crime enthusiasts interested in complex international criminal investigations.
