This video explores "city boy" style, a Japanese reinterpretation of Western Americana that blends collegiate prep, skate culture, British and French workwear with distinctly Japanese silhouettes. The creator walks through essential wardrobe pieces and accessories that define this aesthetic, starting with a signature color palette dominated by blues, greens, whites, and earth tones. Key clothing items discussed include green army fatigues and khakis, striped shirts (particularly French styles), oversized button-downs, boat shoes, and sneakers—especially New Balances and alternative brands like Karhu and Diadora. Outerwear staples include chore coats in bright or faded blue and single-breasted trench coats in neutral shades. The video emphasizes accessories as crucial elements: bandanas provide color pops, hats (bucket caps, baseball caps, beanies) are nearly obligatory, and structured tote bags complete the look. Throughout, the creator prioritizes thrifting and secondhand shopping while recommending specific brands like Stan Ray, Todd Snyder, and Engineered Garments for new purchases. The target audience is fashion enthusiasts interested in Japanese streetwear, vintage styling, and sustainable consumption practices.
