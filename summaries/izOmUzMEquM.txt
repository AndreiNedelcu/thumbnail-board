Post Malone emerged as a significant figure in hip-hop during the mid-2010s, adopting visual aesthetics like braids and grills while drawing from SoundCloud rap influences like Future and Young Thug. However, his trajectory sparked substantial cultural criticism, with detractors labeling him a "culture vulture" for adopting Black cultural markers while lacking genuine commitment to the genre. Controversies including past use of racial slurs, dismissive comments about hip-hop's lyrical depth, and contradictory statements about his genre allegiances intensified skepticism within the rap community. Critics like Charlemagne tha God and Star Leo questioned whether he was exploiting hip-hop solely for commercial gain. Though projects like "Beer Bongs and Bentleys" achieved massive success and earned some defenders like Joe Budden, Post gradually pivoted toward rock, pop, and ultimately country music, particularly evident in his recent album "F-1 Trillion." His departure seemingly vindicated early critics who viewed his hip-hop phase as opportunistic. The video examines whether Post strategically used rap as an entry point to superstardom before abandoning it, or whether he simply evolved artistically, ultimately targeting audiences interested in hip-hop history, cultural criticism, and celebrity narratives.
