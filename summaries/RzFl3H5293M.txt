This video documents the iterative design process of creating a rolling dolly attachment for a Onewheel personal transportation device, solving the practical problem of carrying the heavy board through grocery stores. The creator prototyped eleven iterations, starting with mechanical snap-fit designs inspired by gym bench tilting mechanisms, then exploring magnets before discovering the Onewheel's threaded foot pad inserts, which provided the perfect mounting solution. Key challenges addressed include maintaining ground clearance while riding, ensuring removability without permanent modification, and achieving sufficient structural strength. The project heavily features Bamboo Lab's PPAF (premium fiber-reinforced nylon) filament, showcasing its metal-like strength and heat resistance through durability testing compared to standard PETG. The final "Wheelstand" design incorporates wheels for rolling mobility and an integrated kickstand for convenient storage in retail environments. The creator 3D-printed prototypes using different orientations to optimize layer strength and worked with another engineer to refine the attachment mechanism. The video targets makers and Onewheel enthusiasts interested in practical modifications, 3D printing techniques, and product development methodology, with the creator offering free design files online while pitching the concept to Onewheel's manufacturer for potential commercial production.
