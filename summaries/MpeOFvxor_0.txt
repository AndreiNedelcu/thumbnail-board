# Summary

This video investigates the apparent contradiction between Wikipedia's frequent, urgent donation campaigns and the organization's actual financial health. Founded in 2001 by Jimmy Wales and Larry Sanger, Wikipedia operates as a free encyclopedia maintained by millions of volunteer editors and funded entirely through donations to the Wikimedia Foundation (WMF). The analysis reveals that while these campaigns use dramatic language suggesting the platform is struggling and needs immediate financial support—particularly in developing countries like India—the Wikimedia Foundation is actually quite wealthy, with approximately $255 million in net assets plus a $120 million endowment fund. The foundation raised $165 million in fiscal 2022-2023 alone. Most donations support the WMF's 700+ staff members and initiatives beyond basic site maintenance, which costs only $3.1 million annually. The foundation also runs additional projects, lobbying efforts, and a for-profit subsidiary called Wikimedia Enterprise. The video presents criticism from longtime Wikipedia volunteer Andreas Kuba and community members who feel the fundraising campaigns misrepresent Wikipedia's financial situation and exploitation of volunteer editors. The WMF has begun responding to feedback by collaborating with the community to improve campaign messaging, though the tension between financial security and emotional fundraising appeals remains.
