# Summary

This video examines whether Twitter is truly dying by analyzing its 17-year history through the lens of company forensics. It traces Twitter's journey from Jack Dorsey's 2006 concept through its evolution as a crucial platform for public discourse, profitability challenges, and eventual acquisition by Elon Musk in October 2022. The analysis uses Google Trends and SEO data (via Ahrefs) to track the platform's search relevance over time, revealing patterns in user interest and engagement. Key turning points include the failed monetization strategy despite early viral products like Vine, the costly acquisition of Periscope, and the transition to private ownership. The video details Musk's controversial tenure, including mass layoffs, advertising exodus, failed verification rollout, rebranding to "X," and leadership changes. While acknowledging Twitter's undeniable cultural significance in shaping digital society and news discourse, the presenter argues that the company struggled to convert its social influence into sustainable business value, ultimately positioning it as a case study in wasted potential rather than outright failure.
