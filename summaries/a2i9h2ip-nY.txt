Midjourney, the AI company known for image generation, is launching Midjourney Medical, a healthcare division introducing "ultrasonic CT," an affordable imaging technology using half a million grain-sized sensors to fire ultrasonic waves through a patient's body submerged in water. The resulting terabytes of data are processed using AI algorithms Midjourney has perfected through years of image reconstruction work. The company plans to open Midjourney Spa, a 25,000-square-foot wellness facility in San Francisco by end of 2027, where full-body scans occur passively during spa visits. While the technology offers significant advantages over traditional MRI and CT scans—speed, accessibility, lower cost, and no radiation—medical professionals raise legitimate concerns about physical limitations: ultrasound cannot penetrate bone or air-filled organs like lungs and brains. The current prototype requires 20 minutes for a scan and lacks FDA clearance, with only body composition analysis currently permitted. Midjourney aims to refine hardware, complete research trials, build Gen 3 scanners by 2028, and deploy 50,000 machines by 2031.
