# Summary

This documentary examines a controversial land acquisition effort in Solano County, California, where billionaire-backed investors including Reed Hoffman, Laurene Powell Jobs, and Marc Andreessen have spent $900 million purchasing 66,000 acres through a shell company called Flannery LLC to build California Forever, a privately governed city on protected farmland. The project represents a broader "network state" movement among tech libertarians seeking to create self-governed communities with minimal regulation, similar to efforts like Prospera in Honduras. Local farming families faced aggressive acquisition tactics including lawsuits against those refusing to sell, prompting significant community resistance. The proposed city would house up to 400,000 people in a county of 450,000, raising concerns about water scarcity, environmental impact, traffic, and the viability of promised affordable housing and job creation. CEO Yan Schwam's tone-deaf public engagement and the billionaires' initial secrecy further alienated residents. Targeted at audiences interested in tech accountability, labor issues, and democratic governance, the video documents how grassroots coalition-building temporarily halted the November ballot initiative, with the company now planning a 2026 relaunch, though locals remain vigilant about potential political capture and broken promises.
