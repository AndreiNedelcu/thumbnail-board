# Summary

This video presents three tragic incidents, each accompanied by documented footage. The first story follows Vladimir Sherbov and Denis Berikov at a Russian ski resort in 2013, where they rode an illegally operated zorb (inflatable ball ride) down an unsecured, dangerously steep slope. The zorb veered off course, flew off a cliff, and ejected both riders; Denis died while Vladimir survived. The second incident describes Rishi Bhat taking a selfie video while zip-lining in India's Baisaran Valley in 2025, unaware that the distracted operator was signaling militants below to initiate a coordinated attack that killed 26 tourists. The third story covers Sarah Jones, a camera assistant on the low-budget film "Midnight Rider" in Georgia in 2014, who was killed when a train struck the film set on an active railroad bridge—a location the director accessed illegally after his filming permit was denied. All three stories examine how seemingly normal recreational or professional situations became deadly due to negligence, deception, or criminal intent, with the associated videos serving as evidence of these tragedies. The content targets audiences interested in true crime and mysterious stories presented in documentary format.
