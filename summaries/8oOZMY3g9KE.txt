This video explores Leonardo da Vinci's extraordinary life and multifaceted genius, examining how his scientific curiosity and anatomical knowledge directly informed his artistic masterpieces. The narrative traces Leonardo's journey from his illegitimate birth in 1452 through his apprenticeship under Andrea del Verrocchio, his years in Milan under Duke Ludovico Sforza's patronage, and his eventual move to France under King Francis I's support. The video details his groundbreaking contributions across diverse fields: his anatomical dissections of approximately thirty corpses, which produced scientifically accurate renderings including the first precise depiction of the frontal sinus; his revolutionary designs for flying machines like the ornithopter and aerial screw; his innovations in cartography that established modern mapmaking standards; and his military engineering expertise. The analysis particularly emphasizes how his deep understanding of human anatomy, proportion, light, and shadow converged in iconic works like The Last Supper and the Mona Lisa, where technical mastery creates psychological depth and emotional resonance. The video targets intellectually curious audiences interested in Renaissance history, art, science, and the intersection of creative and analytical thinking.
