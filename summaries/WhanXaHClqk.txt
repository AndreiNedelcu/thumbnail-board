This video examines Maxim Yakubets, the FBI's most-wanted Russian hacker operating under the alias "Aqua," who leads the cybercriminal organization Evil Corp. The narrative traces Yakubets' criminal evolution from early 2009 attacks using the Zeus trojan to steal from county treasuries, through the development and deployment of devastating malware like Bugat Dridex, which infected thousands of computers across the UK and US, stealing hundreds of millions of dollars. Evil Corp subsequently deployed additional ransomware variants including BitPaymer, Lockheed, and WasteLocker, targeting financial institutions, hospitals, and major companies like Garmin. Despite a five-million-dollar bounty, US sanctions, and criminal indictment, Yakubets remains untouchable in Russia, apparently protected by connections to Russian intelligence and high-ranking officials—reinforced by his 2017 marriage into the family of a former FSB officer. The video targets cybersecurity enthusiasts and true crime audiences, exploring theories about Russia's tacit tolerance of domestic cybercriminals who avoid attacking Russian targets, suggesting state-level complicity and protection in exchange for cyber operations potentially serving national interests.
