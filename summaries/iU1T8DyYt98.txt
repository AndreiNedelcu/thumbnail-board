# Video Summary

This video compares white t-shirts across three price tiers—$5, $25, and $100—to determine whether higher cost correlates with better quality. The creator tests two t-shirts at each price point from brands including Primark and H&M (budget), Uniqlo U and COS (mid-range), and Represent and Studio Nicholson (premium), evaluating each through four rigorous tests: fabric analysis examining softness and construction, fit assessment for silhouette and sizing, opacity testing under natural and artificial light, and a wash test measuring shrinkage and shape retention after two cycles. Key findings reveal that while $5 t-shirts deteriorate significantly after washing, the $25 range offers substantially better durability and shape retention despite minor shrinkage. The $100 options feature improved construction, intentional design details, and ethical production (often made in Portugal), though not always proportionally better quality. The video targets budget-conscious consumers, fashion enthusiasts, and minimalist wardrobe builders interested in understanding value-for-money in basic essentials. Uniqlo's affordable mid-range option ultimately scores highest overall, challenging the assumption that premium pricing guarantees superior performance.
