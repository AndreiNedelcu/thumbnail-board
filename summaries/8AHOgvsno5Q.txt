# Summary

This video examines Operation Northwoods, a classified 1962 plan proposed by the U.S. Joint Chiefs of Staff to justify military intervention in Cuba through false flag attacks on American targets. Frustrated by failed assassination attempts and the Bay of Pigs invasion, military leaders sought to fabricate attacks on Guantanamo Bay, blow up American ships, conduct terrorist campaigns on U.S. soil, and stage incidents involving civilian aircraft—all blamed on Cuba to generate public support for invasion. The plan included specific proposals to wound American citizens, destroy refugee boats, and explode bombs in Miami and Washington. Though President Kennedy rejected the operation, the video argues this represents not a functioning system of checks and balances but merely a disagreement on means rather than ends, as both Kennedy and the military shared the goal of removing Castro. The creator emphasizes that nothing fundamentally prevents such operations from recurring, drawing a parallel to post-9/11 justifications for Iraqi intervention, suggesting the government's underlying machinery remains unchanged. The video targets audiences interested in Cold War history, government accountability, and American foreign policy during the Kennedy administration.
