# Summary

This video investigates MindGeek, the parent company of Pornhub and numerous other adult websites, examining how it became a dominant force in the porn industry and the serious ethical problems it created. The video explains that Pornhub was founded by Matt Keuhn, Stefan Manos, and Osiem Youssef, then acquired and expanded by Fabian Tillman, who built a pornographic empire before selling to new owners in 2013. The company generates an estimated $460 million annually through subscriptions and advertising, making it more trafficked than Netflix or Amazon combined. However, the video documents MindGeek's systemic failures in content moderation, including hosting non-consensual videos, revenge porn, sex trafficking content, and child sexual abuse material (CSAM). Former moderators revealed they were expected to review 1,200 videos daily with inadequate resources and lax guidelines. Only after media scrutiny and Mastercard/Visa withdrawing payment processing did Pornhub remove millions of videos, revealing the company prioritized profit over victim protection. The video targets audiences interested in corporate accountability, examining how MindGeek operates as a largely unregulated monopoly while allegedly maintaining philanthropic facades, highlighting the tension between corporate secrecy and public responsibility.
