# Summary

This video explores the emerging trend of lithium supplementation and examines the scientific evidence supporting its use. While lithium has long been the gold standard treatment for bipolar disorder and mania due to its mood-stabilizing properties, recent research suggests broader neuroprotective benefits. A 2022 retrospective study found that elderly people prescribed lithium experienced a 44% decreased dementia risk compared to untreated controls, despite bipolar disorder patients typically having higher dementia risk baseline. The mechanism involves lithium inhibiting glycogen synthase kinase-3 (GSK3), which creates neural tangles linked to neurodegeneration. Clinical trials show lithium treatment reduces cognitive decline in people with mild cognitive impairment and Alzheimer's disease. Additionally, observational studies across multiple continents found inverse correlations between naturally occurring lithium in drinking water and suicide rates, homicide, and crime. The recommended supplemental dose is approximately 1 milligram daily, though biohackers and cognitive impairment treatments use higher amounts. The video targets health-conscious individuals and biohackers interested in cognitive optimization and longevity, though it acknowledges the need for caution regarding toxicity and pregnancy contraindications.
