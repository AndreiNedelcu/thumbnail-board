# Summary

This experiment tests whether zero-rated Fiverr SEO freelancers can effectively rank a new golf affiliate website on Google's first page. The creator hired multiple unvetted freelancers across key SEO disciplines: keyword research, AI content creation, backlink building, and technical SEO audits. The keyword researchers provided suspicious metrics (some fabricated data entirely), the AI-generated content was fluffy and poorly structured, the backlink builders offered links on no-indexed pages, and the technical SEO auditor submitted generic tool reports missing obvious planted errors like a homepage titled "poo poo bum crack." Despite these struggles, the site eventually ranked in Google's top 100 through one guest post link, though it never achieved first-page rankings. The creator ultimately concludes the real problem wasn't the freelancers' star ratings but his flawed strategy: he neglected competitive analysis and relied on low-cost, disconnected services instead of building quality content to compete against established golf manufacturers. The video targets digital marketers, SEO professionals, and aspiring content creators interested in understanding both Fiverr's limitations and fundamental SEO principles.
