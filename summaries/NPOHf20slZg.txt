# Summary

This video investigates the Rabbit R1, a $30 million AI-backed device that promises comprehensive voice-controlled assistance, examining both its non-functional product performance and the founder's questionable history with a previous cryptocurrency venture. The core investigation reveals that Rabbit Incorporated was formerly Cyber Manufacturer Co, a company that raised $6 million in 2021 for "Gamma," an NFT project pitched with grandiose promises including a carbon-negative Bitcoin 2.0, a metaverse, comic books, TV shows, and eventually AI-powered NPCs controlled by "brain logic." Founder Jesse Lyu claimed these promises would materialize within years, yet the project ultimately delivered minimal results—an open-sourced Unreal Engine game file with bugs rather than the promised ecosystem. The investigation documents how Rabbit's current team dismisses or contradicts previous explicit promises, claiming Gamma was never abandoned and falsely denying promises that are documented in archived videos and Discord posts. The video appeals to consumers and investors who funded both Gamma NFTs and the Rabbit R1, highlighting a pattern of overhyped vaporware from a team with a documented history of abandoning investors without accountability or refunds.
