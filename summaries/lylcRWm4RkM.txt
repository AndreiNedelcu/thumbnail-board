This video documents a creative engineering project where the creator converts a bed into a functional motorized vehicle to avoid paying delivery fees for coffee. Starting with a small bed frame from Amazon, he progressively adds components including plywood, wheels from Home Depot, an electric skateboard for propulsion, and a scooter for steering. The build process involves multiple trips to Home Depot, extensive drilling and measurements, and problem-solving when initial designs fail—such as adjusting wheel heights and creating spacers so the scooter steering wheel maintains ground contact. After securing a mattress, bedding, and pillows to make it look like an actual bed, he successfully drives the motorized bed to a Starbucks drive-thru to order coffee, attracting attention from customers. The video targets DIY and engineering enthusiasts who enjoy watching creative builds and experimental projects, combining humor with practical construction challenges that showcase trial-and-error problem-solving and resourcefulness.
