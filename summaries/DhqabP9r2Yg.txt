# Summary

This video explores a hypothetical day-by-day and month-by-month scenario of the United States withdrawing from NATO through Article 13 denunciation. The narrative details military base shutdowns across Europe, the withdrawal of approximately 75,000-105,000 American troops from countries including Germany, Poland, the UK, and the Baltic states, and the removal of approximately 100 American nuclear weapons from five NATO nations. The video examines cascading consequences including Europe's struggle to replace lost logistics support and intelligence cooperation, the breakdown of unified defense coordination, and the fracturing of trust between the US and European allies. It also describes impacts on the US including lost defense industry contracts worth tens of billions annually as Europe develops independent weapons systems, reduced geopolitical influence, and degraded nuclear deterrence capabilities. Additional consequences include increased cyber attacks from Russia and China, NATO's eventual inability to support Ukraine militarily, Russia's subsequent conquest of Ukraine, and a destabilized global order. The content targets audiences interested in geopolitics, military strategy, international relations, and speculative analysis of NATO's strategic importance to both Europe and American interests.
