# Summary

This video critiques how ancient Stoicism has been co-opted and commercialized in modern times, particularly through influencers and the manosphere. The creator traces Stoicism's origins with Zeno of Citium through Marcus Aurelius, then documents its twentieth-century revival via cognitive behavioral therapy pioneers like Albert Ellis and Aaron Beck, followed by a Silicon Valley boom spearheaded by figures like Ryan Holiday and Tim Ferris. While acknowledging genuine therapeutic applications, the video argues that Holiday and similar entrepreneurs have distorted Stoic philosophy into marketable life hacks and merchandise—divorced from its core teaching that external success, wealth, and status are morally indifferent. The analysis reveals how Stoicism became trendy among celebrities, athletes, and tech billionaires, subsequently spreading to online communities including the manosphere and red pill movement, where it's weaponized to promote misogyny and toxic masculinity under the guise of ancient wisdom. The video targets viewers interested in philosophy, media criticism, and understanding how authentic intellectual traditions get corrupted through commercialization and ideological manipulation.
