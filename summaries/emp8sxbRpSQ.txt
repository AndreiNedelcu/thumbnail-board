SpaceX developed advanced spacesuits by combining custom-fit design with automated pressure management systems. Unlike traditional NASA spacesuits that are one-size-fits-all, SpaceX creates individually tailored suits for each crew member, enabling better joint alignment and mobility. The suits address the fundamental challenge of pressurized suit rigidity through crisscross tensioners at joints, allowing astronauts to move more naturally. A key innovation involves consolidating multiple external valves and controls into a single umbilical port connected to the spacecraft, where onboard solenoid valves automatically regulate pressure and temperature. The suit operates at 40 kilopascals with custom oxygen-enriched air mixtures managed by integrated sensors and the life support system, eliminating the need for astronauts to manually adjust settings. These design improvements make SpaceX's suit significantly lighter and faster to don than the Space Shuttle suit, allowing astronauts to self-suit in minutes rather than twenty minutes with technician assistance.
