This video explores the historical origins of modern work culture and its psychological grip on contemporary life. The creator, a lawyer who left traditional practice to become self-employed, examines why he still maintains a rigid Monday-to-Friday schedule despite seeking flexibility. He traces the concept of work ethic through human history, contrasting the traditional work ethic of hunter-gatherers and ancient civilizations—where work was merely a means to survival—with the Protestant work ethic introduced by Martin Luther and John Calvin in the 1500s. This paradigm shift transformed work into a moral calling and life purpose rather than a practical necessity. The video explains how this religious concept became secularized and internalized by modern society, permeating corporate marketing strategies and political discourse. The creator argues that despite technological advancement equivalent to "billions of slaves," contemporary society doubles down on this outdated work ethic rather than pursuing Aristotle's concept of eudaimonia (human flourishing). Targeted at viewers who overwork themselves, the video challenges whether the Protestant work ethic still serves individuals and society, ultimately questioning whether alternative approaches to labor and leisure are needed.
