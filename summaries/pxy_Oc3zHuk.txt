This video documents a comprehensive modernization project of a PlayStation Portable, transforming a broken unit into a feature-rich handheld gaming device. The creator disassembles the complex PSP 1000 model and performs multiple upgrades: replacing the original screen with a high-brightness IPS display, fixing worn buttons and analog stick, installing a Samsung S22 Ultra battery with custom battery management circuitry to provide three times the original capacity, and adding USB-C and wireless charging capabilities via custom-designed 3D-printed mid-frames. The project includes comprehensive hacking via custom firmware installation to enable games to boot from internal storage, bypassing the failure-prone UMD drive entirely, and overclocking the processor from 222 MHz to 333 MHz for improved performance. Throughout the build, the creator demonstrates extensive soldering work, uses a Bamboo Lab P1S 3D printer to design protective components and frames, and tests various filaments including translucent PETG. The final result is housed in a transparent shell showcasing all internal modifications. The video targets electronics enthusiasts, retro gaming fans, and makers interested in console modification, hardware hacking, and advanced 3D printing applications.
