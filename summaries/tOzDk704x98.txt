In 2015, McDonald's faced its worst performance in 80 years as competitors like Shake Shack and Chipotle captured market share by emphasizing quality over quantity. The company's attempt to modernize through gourmet items like the Create Your Taste program and premium sandwiches failed to resonate with consumers, as did transparency campaigns debunking myths about McNuggets. When new CEO Steve Easterbrook took over mid-2015, he fundamentally restructured McDonald's from a restaurant operator into a real estate and franchising business, reorganizing the company around four market segments and pursuing aggressive franchising targets. His strategy pivoted away from menu innovation toward leveraging delivery platforms, introducing all-day breakfast, and launching celebrity partnership meals starting with Rick and Morty's Szechuan sauce. Rather than competing on health or premium offerings, Easterbrook repositioned McDonald's to capture occasional impulse purchases through limited-time offerings and convenience. The shift proved dramatically successful: while overall revenue declined, profit margins surged from eighteen to twenty-five percent, franchise sales grew consistently, and by 2021, delivery alone generated thirteen billion dollars annually representing forty percent of revenue.
