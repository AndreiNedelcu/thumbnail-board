This video presents a scathing critique of the Marvel Disney+ series She-Hulk: Attorney at Law, with the reviewer arguing it represents some of the worst television writing ever produced. The analysis focuses on multiple interconnected failures: poor CGI rendering that resembles "Gumby," lazy writing that relies on meta-commentary to deflect criticism, fundamental misunderstanding of legal procedures despite the show's courtroom focus, and problematic messaging around gender and identity politics. The reviewer extensively discusses how the show simultaneously portrays female characters as flawless while reducing all male characters to stereotypical buffoons or predators, creating what he characterizes as gaslighting of the audience. He argues that the show's attempt at feminism is actually regressive, contrasting it with better examples of empowerment-driven storytelling in films like Everything Everywhere All at Once. The video targets entertainment critics and general audiences interested in media analysis, offering detailed episode-by-episode breakdowns while incorporating his girlfriend's perspective as a female viewer outside the entertainment industry to validate his critique that the messaging alienates rather than empowers.
