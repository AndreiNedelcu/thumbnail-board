Meta is fundamentally an advertising company that monetizes user attention across Facebook, Instagram, and WhatsApp—its primary revenue generators—while simultaneously investing heavily in artificial intelligence and pursuing speculative ventures like VR hardware and the metaverse. The company rebranded from Facebook in 2021 partly to distance itself from privacy scandals and regulatory scrutiny, but also to reflect its sprawling portfolio of acquired properties and new bets. Despite spending nearly $80 billion on metaverse development that largely failed to gain traction, Meta has pivoted aggressively toward AI infrastructure, hiring top engineers with substantial salaries and building massive data centers—reportedly budgeting around $600 billion for AI capex. This AI focus aims both to maintain competitive advantage against rivals like OpenAI and Google, and to enhance core ad products through better targeting and personalization, which has already contributed to Meta's recent revenue growth resurgence. Internally, the company has become increasingly competitive and anxiety-inducing for employees, with management installing keystroke tracking software and reallocating engineering resources away from safety teams toward AI development. The video targets general audiences curious about corporate strategy and tech industry dynamics.
