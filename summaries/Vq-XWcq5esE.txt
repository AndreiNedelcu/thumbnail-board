This battery endurance test compares the iPhone 15 lineup (Pro Max, Pro, Plus, and base model) against previous generations, specifically the iPhone 14 Pro Max, iPhone 14, and iPhone 13 Pro Max. The tester conducts a full day of realistic use including social media, work tasks, video calls, gaming, and camera usage to measure actual battery performance rather than relying on manufacturer claims about power efficiency. A key finding is that Apple's claim of three times greater power efficiency doesn't translate proportionally to battery life gains, likely because performance improvements offset some efficiency gains. The iPhone 15 Plus emerges as the surprise winner with exceptional battery longevity, despite being a non-Pro model, while the smaller iPhone 15 Pro underperforms expectations due to its smaller battery capacity and additional features like Dynamic Island and the always-active A17 Pro chip. The video targets tech enthusiasts and potential iPhone buyers seeking practical battery life comparisons rather than marketing narratives, demonstrating that display technology and overall design choices matter more for endurance than chipset efficiency alone.
