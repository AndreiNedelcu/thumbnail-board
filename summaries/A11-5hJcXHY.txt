Nuclear energy is experiencing renewed interest in the United States as policymakers and industry leaders recognize its potential to address climate change and power artificial intelligence infrastructure. The video traces how the U.S. nuclear sector declined since the 1979 Three Mile Island accident, with plants closing due to competition from cheaper natural gas and renewables, but is now seeing a reversal. Key developments include Southern Company's completion of Vogtle reactors 3 and 4 in 2024, though significantly over budget and behind schedule, and the emergence of startups developing small modular reactors (SMRs) and advanced technologies like molten salt cooling. The video discusses how achieving net-zero emissions by 2050 requires nuclear as a complement to wind and solar, while acknowledging challenges including high construction costs, regulatory hurdles, and opposition from environmental groups concerned about safety and radioactive waste. The content targets general audiences interested in energy policy and climate solutions, examining whether utilities will commit to new nuclear projects and when next-generation reactors might become commercially viable, with forecasts suggesting retired plants reopening and SMRs operational after 2030.
