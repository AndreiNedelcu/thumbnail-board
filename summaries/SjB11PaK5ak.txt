# Summary

This video addresses why people with ADHD experience disproportionate exhaustion compared to neurotypical peers, even when accomplishing similar tasks. The creator explains that ADHD requires significantly more effort to complete work—a person with ADHD might spend four hours producing what takes a neurotypical person one hour—creating an energy deficit despite identical output. Additional factors include the adjustment fatigue experienced when adults recently diagnosed with ADHD begin "catching up" to normal life intensity, chronic stress and cortisol depletion from constant deadlines without breaks, and deep-rooted self-blame stemming from childhood misdiagnosis. The video highlights how undiagnosed ADHD children internalize that something is fundamentally wrong with them rather than understanding their attention works differently, forming a persistent belief of inadequacy that persists even with later success. The creator targets people with ADHD seeking to understand their fatigue and offers practical solutions: developing organizational systems and workflows specifically designed for distractible minds, addressing confidence issues through therapy, and learning techniques that compensate for ADHD rather than mimicking neurotypical approaches. The message emphasizes that ADHD success is achievable but requires customized strategies rather than self-blame.
