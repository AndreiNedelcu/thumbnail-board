# Summary

This video chronicles the remarkable story of Grigori Perelman, a Russian mathematician who solved the Poincaré Conjecture, one of the seven million-dollar Millennium Problems established by the Clay Mathematics Institute in 2000. The video traces Perelman's journey from his elite Soviet education through his groundbreaking 2002 solution, which built upon Richard Hamilton's Ricci flow mathematical framework and William Thurston's geometric decomposition theory. Perelman proved the conjecture by developing novel surgical techniques to analyze three-dimensional shapes, publishing his proof across three papers totaling 992 pages. Despite international mathematical acclaim and multiple prestigious job offers from institutions like Princeton, MIT, and Columbia, Perelman rejected the one-million-dollar prize, the Fields Medal, and academic positions, choosing instead to return to Russia with a modest salary of approximately $400 monthly. By 2005, he withdrew entirely from both mathematics and mainstream society, eventually living reclusively with his mother. The narrative explores how Perelman's extraordinary intellectual achievements were coupled with his principled rejection of recognition, institutional pressure, and societal expectations, presenting a thought-provoking examination of individual values versus external accolades. The video targets audiences interested in mathematics, genius biographies, and stories of unconventional life choices.
