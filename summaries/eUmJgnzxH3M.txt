# Summary

A fitness enthusiast with no powerlifting experience undertakes a 100-day training challenge to prepare for an official powerlifting competition featuring the three core lifts: squat, bench press, and deadlift. He works with Australian strength coach Sebastian Ojinni (who trains Thor Bjornsson) to master proper technique and follows a structured four-day-per-week training program progressing from skill acquisition with lighter weights to heavier one-rep maxes. The journey involves learning powerlifting mechanics, meeting strict competition rules, investing in specialized equipment, and committing to rigorous nutrition with daily macro tracking and meal-prep services. A significant setback occurs when a chest tear sidelines bench press training for several weeks, requiring adaptation of his program. Throughout the process, he documents dramatic strength improvements—aiming to progress from initial baseline lifts (185 lbs squat, 200 lbs bench, 256 lbs deadlift) toward ambitious goals (160kg squat, 120kg bench, 180kg deadlift)—while balancing the demands of a new parent's schedule. The content targets fitness enthusiasts interested in strength training, personal challenges, and the science behind athletic performance, combining practical training education with motivational storytelling about transformation and perseverance despite injury.
