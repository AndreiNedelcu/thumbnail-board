# Summary

The Humane AI Pin is a wearable AI assistant device the size of a large watch that costs $700 plus $24/month subscription, designed to be a standalone computer independent of smartphones. It features a camera, microphone, touchpad, and innovative laser projector that displays a 720p interface on the user's palm, controlled through hand gestures. The device targets early adopters interested in futuristic wearables and AI integration into daily life. However, the reviewer identifies this as the worst product they've reviewed, citing numerous critical failures: unreliable AI responses with frequent hallucinations, slow cloud-dependent processing, inconsistent battery life requiring constant swapping of external batteries, excessive heat generation, weight distribution problems, low-quality photos/videos, missing core features like timers and alarms, and crucially, the lack of smartphone integration that makes it inferior to existing phones for nearly every function. The device's fundamental flaw is positioning itself as a smartphone replacement while performing every task worse, slower, and with more friction than the phones users already own. The reviewer argues that smartphones are essentially "overpowered" for this niche, making the AI Pin feel redundant despite impressive engineering craftsmanship.
