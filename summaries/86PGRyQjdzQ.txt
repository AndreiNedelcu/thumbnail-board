# Summary

This video breaks down the evolution of UI/UX design skills across four career levels—beginner, junior, mid-level, and senior—by iteratively redesigning a real client project from a crypto rewards interface. At each stage, the creator identifies common mistakes including unclear copywriting, overuse of flashy visual effects, improper color distribution, excessive font variety, and inconsistent spacing. Key techniques introduced include the 60-30-10 color rule, the 8-point grid system for spacing, and font size reduction strategies. The video emphasizes that junior designers (1-3 years) still struggle with copywriting clarity, mid-level designers (3-6 years) overwork visuals but master color balance, and senior designers (7+ years) achieve harmony through reduction and intentional design choices. A critical insight for senior designers is recognizing that static UI design alone is insufficient in an AI-saturated market; the key to commanding premium rates and standing out is creating memorable, delightful experiences through thoughtful motion, transitions, and seamless user flows—treating design more like filmmaking than static image creation.
