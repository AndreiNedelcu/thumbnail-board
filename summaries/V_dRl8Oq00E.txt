# Summary

This video documents the construction of an extraordinarily complex Wither Skeleton farm in Hardcore Minecraft using version 1.18.2, undertaken because the creator needs access to numerous beacons for various beneficial effects. The project requires clearing an enormous 176-block square area down to bedrock using piston-based bedrock breakers, followed by careful trench excavation through Netherrack, basalt, and lava lakes—consuming nearly 700 diamond pickaxes. The farm's core mechanism relies on an update suppressor exploit (patched in later versions) to float approximately 4,000 wither roses as the spawn layer, allowing precise mob spawning control within the Nether fortress bounding box. The creator details the meticulous process of placing wither roses, furnace array setup for smelting materials, and the preparation of 26,000 glass blocks for the structure. Targeting advanced Minecraft players and technical enthusiasts, this video showcases extreme dedication to late-game optimization, exploiting outdated mechanics, and the enormous scale of infrastructure required for endgame farm automation.
