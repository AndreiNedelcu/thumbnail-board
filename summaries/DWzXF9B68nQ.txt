# Space-Filling Curves Visualization Through Water

This video explores space-filling curves—one-dimensional mathematical lines with fractal properties that can fill two-dimensional or three-dimensional space—by using water as a visualization tool. The creator demonstrates how Hilbert curves and other space-filling curves work by physically filling 3D-printed versions with water, a method that intuitively illustrates their fractal nature. Key concepts discussed include fractional dimensions, self-similarity, and how these curves approach every point in a given space through infinite iteration. The video features multiple demonstrations: a 2D Hilbert curve filled with water, a 3D version polished to clarity, a Gosper curve, and a Celtic Labyrinth pattern. The creator also interviews mathematician Matt Parker to explore whether all space-filling curves must be fractals and whether our current mathematical tools can rigorously prove all curves that visually appear to fill space. Additional visualizations include a 3D-printed marble run version and time-lapse footage of the curves being 3D-printed. The video targets audiences interested in mathematics, fractals, and creative scientific visualization, blending theoretical concepts with satisfying practical demonstrations.
