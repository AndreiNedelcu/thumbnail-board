On September 11, 2001, President Bush was at a Florida elementary school promoting an education initiative when American airspace came under attack. The video chronicles the harrowing journey of Air Force One that day, beginning when White House staff informed Bush of the plane crashes in New York and the Pentagon strike. As threats mounted—including a suspicious device reported near the runway and fears of additional hijacked aircraft—Air Force One executed emergency protocols including steep takeoff angles and extreme altitude climbs to evade potential threats. The presidential plane was diverted to Barksdale Air Force Base in Louisiana, then to Offutt Air Force Base in Nebraska, rather than returning directly to Washington as Bush wanted. The video details the controversial decision by Vice President Cheney to authorize shooting down civilian aircraft if necessary, examining the military chain-of-command questions this raised. It also documents false alarms, including an ominous call claiming "Angel" (Air Force One's code word) would be targeted next, and communication breakdowns throughout the day. The journey that should have lasted 2.5 hours stretched to over nine hours, testing the aircraft's safety systems and revealing vulnerabilities that would be addressed in subsequent years.
