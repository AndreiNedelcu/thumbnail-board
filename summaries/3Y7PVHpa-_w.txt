This video tours ASUS's research and development laboratory designed to test enterprise and data center hardware at rack scale, showcasing their testing infrastructure for NVIDIA's Grace Blackwell and upcoming Vera Rubin processors. The facility functions as a miniature data center with both air-cooled and liquid-cooled systems, featuring a dedicated coolant distribution unit capable of handling power densities exceeding 100,000 watts. The host examines the Grace Blackwell GB300 compute node in detail, explaining its dual NVIDIA Bianca boards with ARM Grace CPUs, Blackwell Ultra GPUs with HBM3e memory, and advanced 54-volt power architecture, along with water-cooled networking components equipped with leak detection sensors. The lab includes environmental chambers for long-term aging analysis and cooling validation testing under extreme conditions ranging from -40°C to 85°C. The video also demonstrates ASUS's software ecosystem—AIDC for deployment planning and management, and ASUS CC for long-term operational monitoring—highlighting how the facility enables rapid iteration between R&D and mass production located nearby. The audience comprises technology enthusiasts, hardware engineers, and data center professionals interested in enterprise infrastructure validation methodologies.
