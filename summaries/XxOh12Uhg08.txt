# Video Summary

This video provides a comprehensive first-hand review of Tesla's Cybertruck, covering specs, design, features, and driving impressions from a near-final prototype vehicle. The reviewer details the three available configurations—triple motor (0-60 in 2.7 seconds, $100,000 starting price), dual motor ($75,000-$85,000), and single motor variants—with 340-mile maximum range and 2,500-pound bed capacity. Key design elements include the stainless steel exoskeleton construction with its signature angular aesthetic, removable side mirrors, motorized tonneau cover (supporting 300 pounds), motorized front trunk, and the industry's largest windshield creating a glass canopy. Notable features include handle-less doors operated via indented buttons, 9.6kW onboard power output for charging other EVs, and a minimal interior with an unconventional steering wheel. The reviewer addresses both advantages—strong performance, practical bed design with drainage and power outlets—and concerns, including fingerprint-prone stainless steel, potential ice-related door-opening issues in cold climates, and compromised rear visibility with the tonneau cover closed. The content targets tech enthusiasts, truck buyers, and Tesla followers interested in detailed vehicle analysis and real-world practicality assessments.
