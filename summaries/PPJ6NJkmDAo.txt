# Summary

This video demonstrates a sophisticated "man-in-the-middle" attack on Apple Pay that allows thieves to withdraw $10,000 from a locked iPhone without user authorization. The exploit manipulates NFC (near-field communication) between a phone and payment terminal using a Proxmark device and laptop running a Python script to intercept and alter transaction data. The attack exploits Apple's express transit mode—a convenience feature allowing subway payments without unlocking—by broadcasting a fraudulent transit signal that tricks the phone into authorization mode. The attacker then flips three critical data bits: one to make the phone think it's a transit transaction, another to classify a $10,000 payment as "low value" (avoiding verification requirements), and a third to convince the reader that customer verification occurred. The vulnerability only affects specific iPhone and Visa card combinations; Samsung phones reject unusually large transit transactions, and MasterCard uses additional asymmetric cryptography that prevents the attack. Notably, cybersecurity researchers discovered this loophole in 2021, yet it remains unfixed. The video targets general audiences interested in smartphone security, payment systems, and cybersecurity vulnerabilities, explaining complex cryptographic concepts through accessible demonstrations with MKBHD.
