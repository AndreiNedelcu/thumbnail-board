# Summary

This video explores why Generation Z has surprisingly embraced premium American Express credit cards despite earlier predictions they would abandon credit cards altogether. The analysis reveals that 84% of Gen Z aged 22-24 now hold credit cards compared to just 61% of Millennials at the same age, with Gen Z and Millennials accounting for over 75% of new Amex signups last year. The video attributes this shift to several factors: Gen Z came of age in a stronger post-financial-crisis economy compared to Millennials; they're accustomed to subscription-based spending models, making annual fees like $250-$695 feel less intimidating; and premium cards offer substantial perks including airport lounge access, travel credits, and entertainment benefits that appeal to social media-savvy consumers. American Express successfully repositioned itself from an exclusive card for wealthy business travelers to a aspirational status symbol for younger demographics. However, the video notes a critical caveat: despite Gen Z's apparent financial sophistication in choosing premium cards, credit card delinquency has hit all-time highs, suggesting many young people may be accumulating unsustainable debt to fund their lifestyles rather than possessing genuinely superior money management skills.
