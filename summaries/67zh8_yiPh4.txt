Mike, an Australian SaaS founder, discusses his framework for building five successful apps that collectively generate over $200,000 in monthly recurring revenue. He shares his philosophy of minimizing risk by selecting ideas that have already proven market demand rather than pursuing novel concepts. His approach centers on assembling lean four-person founding teams with complementary skills (frontend/backend developers, designer, and generalist), prioritizing exceptional design, and maintaining strict cost control to maximize founder profits. Mike details his specific 10-step playbook that guides each launch, starting with identifying validated ideas and building minimal viable products quickly. Key tactics include offering lifetime deals to early customers for $59-$100, using platforms like AppSumo and Reddit to reach audiences, and generating content early for search engine optimization. His apps—Curator.io (social media aggregation), Thrill.co (customer feedback), Juno.co (digital signage), and Fluke.co (no-code tours)—exemplify "boring" but profitable niches. He emphasizes avoiding platform-dependent ideas like AI-focused businesses and advocates building products you genuinely enjoy creating alongside people you like working with.
