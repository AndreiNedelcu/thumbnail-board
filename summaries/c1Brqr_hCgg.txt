# Summary

This video examines the 2017 Vault 7 leak, the largest disclosure of classified CIA intelligence documents in history, which revealed the agency's cyber hacking and surveillance capabilities developed between 2008 and 2016. WikiLeaks released cryptic teasers before publishing 8,761 files detailing CIA tools and techniques, including Hive (command and control framework), Weeping Angel (which turned Samsung TVs into listening devices), Sonic Screwdriver (which manipulated Apple adapters), and others designed for espionage operations. The video traces how the leak originated from Joshua Schultz, a disgruntled CIA software engineer in the Operation Support Branch who had escalating workplace conflicts with a colleague named Amal, including death threats and a failed restraining order. After losing his administrator access and resigning in November 2016, Schultz allegedly leaked the tools to WikiLeaks. The FBI investigation discovered he was actually arrested on child sexual abuse material charges rather than espionage-related offenses, raising questions about whether personal grievance or principled whistleblowing motivated the leak. The video targets audiences interested in cybersecurity, government accountability, and high-profile intelligence agency stories.
