This video documents the grooming and bathing of Cookie, a 20-pound morbidly obese 10-year-old domestic shorthair cat staying at Big House Sanctuary, a rescue specializing in obese cats. Cookie recently lost both her owners and cannot self-groom due to her extreme weight; she is also declawed on all four paws, which affects her mobility and means she cannot defend herself with claws. The groomer approaches the process cautiously, using an air muzzle as a precaution while bathing and drying the cat, though Cookie ultimately proves to be docile and non-aggressive. The video follows the complete grooming process including brushing, bathing to address dry skin and matting, blow-drying, and finishing touches. The groomer discusses weight management challenges in cats, explains why Cookie cannot fit in certain drying equipment, and emphasizes the sanctuary's adoption process and health requirements before Cookie can go to a forever home. The target audience includes cat lovers, animal rescue supporters, and those interested in grooming techniques for special-needs pets.
