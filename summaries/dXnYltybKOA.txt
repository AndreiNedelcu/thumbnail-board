# Summary

This video teaches small business owners how to use process mapping as a foundational tool for systematizing their operations. The presenter demonstrates how to create visual process maps using simple symbols—boxes for actions, diamonds for decision points, arrows for flow direction, and rounded rectangles for start/stop points—on a whiteboard or paper. The methodology focuses on identifying high-value, high-pain areas in the business, defining clear start and stop points, and mapping discrete, delegatable actions at the appropriate level of detail. Rather than getting bogged down in minutiae, the approach emphasizes observable actions that take one work session to complete. The presenter then introduces a comprehensive five-step framework for complete systemization: mapping the "what" (processes), understanding the "why" (business purpose), identifying the "who" (responsible person), defining the "when" (timing and triggers), and documenting the "how" (instructions and templates). This content targets small business owners with teams under 30 people who struggle with delegation and repeatability, offering both a DIY approach using whiteboards and a pathway toward professional implementation through the presenter's Foundations program.
