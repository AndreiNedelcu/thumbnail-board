# Summary

This video presents three dark crime stories illustrating how appearances can deceive. The first case involves Jeong Yu-jeong, a 23-year-old woman posing as a struggling ninth-grade student who hired tutor Chae-un through a mobile app. After arriving at the tutor's apartment in Busan, South Korea, Jeong murdered Chae-un with a knife hidden in her suitcase, dismembered the body, and attempted to dispose of it at the Nakdong River. A suspicious taxi driver, noticing her empty suitcase on return, alerted police and prevented her escape. The second story follows Norm Johnson, a beloved retired teacher in Madison, South Dakota, shot at his home during a basketball game by an unrecognized gray-haired man. The transcript cuts off before revealing details, but introduces Carl Erickson, a 73-year-old retired insurance officer living an hour away, as a suspect despite seemingly having no connection to Norm. The series targets audiences fascinated by true crime narratives, psychological analysis of killers, and mysterious criminal investigations, presented as dramatized storytelling with contextual details that emphasize the unpredictability of violence and the unreliable nature of surface-level judgments about people.
