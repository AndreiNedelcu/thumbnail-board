This street interview video takes viewers through Ginza, Tokyo's luxury district, documenting what watches people are wearing. The creator visits the free Seiko Museum to explore the brand's history dating back to 1881, then interviews both locals and tourists about their timepieces. Featured watches include Rolex models (Submariner, Daytona, Sky-Dweller, Oyster Perpetual), Omega Speedmaster, Seiko automatics, Jaeger-LeCoultre, Patek Philippe, Vacheron Constantin, IWC, and a Jaeger-LeCoultre Reverso. Interviewees range from casual wearers who bought watches for aesthetic appeal to serious collectors with 14+ pieces in their collections, discussing holy grail watches and hunting for specific models. The video captures diverse backgrounds including restaurant owners from the Philippines, business owners from Malaysia, a solar installation entrepreneur from India, a mechanical engineer, and tourists from Denmark. The content targets watch enthusiasts interested in luxury timepieces, travel vloggers, and those curious about Japanese watch culture, while providing viewers an authentic glimpse into how different demographics in one of Tokyo's most exclusive areas value and collect watches.
