This documentary explores the forgotten legacy of Alexander Ygor, an early 20th-century Dutch-American sculptor, engineer, and inventor who designed the "Disc Copter," a vertical-takeoff flying saucer concept patented in 1944—decades before UFO imagery saturated popular culture. The film follows Randy Hunter, an art dealer and fine art collector who became obsessed with Ygor after discovering his work in 2008, spending millions of dollars and a decade tracking down sketches, personal belongings, and artwork to resurrect the inventor's reputation. Hunter believes Ygor was a hidden genius whose aircraft design directly influenced modern UFO mythology and architecture, and that the U.S. military appropriated his ideas without credit. The documentary examines themes of obsession, artistic legacy, and historical erasure through Hunter's increasingly consuming mission, which includes purchasing Ygor's former property to build a museum and recreate his studio. Targeted at audiences interested in forgotten inventors, art history, design innovation, and the intersection of creativity with personal fixation, the film presents a nuanced portrait of how one person's passion project can border on obsession while raising questions about what constitutes historical importance and cultural recognition.
