Three tech enthusiasts conclude their month-long Linux daily-driver challenge, sharing their final verdicts on whether they'll stay with Linux or return to Windows. The hosts discuss Bazite (a Fedora variant), Kubuntu, and Linux Mint, highlighting major improvements in gaming compatibility through Proton, system performance and responsiveness, simplified driver installation, package management elegance, and community support. They praise Linux's speed advantage over Windows, note Microsoft's aggressive feature promotion and questionable design choices, and appreciate the ability to fix issues themselves rather than waiting for corporate updates. Key frustrations include middle-mouse button behavior inconsistencies, search functionality differences, display capture issues with Wayland, Xbox controller pairing problems, and developer-enforced anti-cheat blocking on certain games like Marathon. The speakers acknowledge that despite Linux's rapid progress making it viable for daily use, significant adoption barriers remain—particularly game compatibility enforced by anti-cheat measures. They address community toxicity, emphasizing that constructive criticism helps Linux improve rather than harm it, while recognizing legitimate platform limitations shouldn't be ignored or dismissed. Overall, all three seriously considered staying on Linux, viewing the platform's momentum as evidence that the "year of Linux desktop" draws progressively closer.
