# Summary

This video examines the deteriorating quality of Google Search and other search engines in 2024-2025, arguing that internet discovery has become increasingly polluted with spam, AI-generated content, and plagiarized articles. The creator uses a viral Reddit post about searching for "John Wick 5" as a case study—where the top results include AI-generated fake trailers, speculative clickbait, and plagiarized content before any legitimate information appears. The video references academic research from Leipzig University showing that search results increasingly prioritize pages using SEO manipulation tactics rather than content quality, and discusses how Google's vague policies on AI-generated spam fail to address the problem adequately. Additional issues covered include plagiarized news articles copied from legitimate sources, Google Images flooded with AI-generated pictures instead of real photographs, and the rise of AI Overviews that sometimes provide dangerous misinformation (like smoking during pregnancy). The creator compares the current state to AOL's cluttered search in the 1990s, suggesting users now face an "internet pollution" problem that demands a new, cleaner search alternative. The target audience is tech-savvy users frustrated with declining search quality and concerned about information reliability online.
