# Summary

A founder who raised $5 million for a creator economy platform breaks down their original pitch deck with an early investor, analyzing what worked and what didn't. The founder was a first-year Stanford MBA student with a TikTok following and prior VC experience at Norwest when they pitched in April 2021 during the creator economy boom. Key elements discussed include the importance of warm introductions, opening with a unique hook (the founder's viral TikTok about interviewing creators), establishing a clear customer problem through persona-driven storytelling, and demonstrating early traction with 25 beta testers before asking for money. The investor critiques several slides for being visually ugly, conceptually vague, or failing to clearly articulate the specific solution and business model. Critical lessons emphasized include the need to differentiate from competitors, show genuine customer validation rather than just talking about market size, clearly explain what problem you're solving first, avoid claiming to do everything, and balance credibility-building with demonstrating founder capability. The conversation targets aspiring entrepreneurs and founders seeking practical pitch deck feedback and fundraising strategy insights.
