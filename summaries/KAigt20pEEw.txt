The Belgian Grand Prix delivered dramatic developments as Lewis Hamilton claimed victory following George Russell's post-race disqualification on technical grounds. Russell's Mercedes was found to be 798 kilograms, the minimum permissible weight without fuel, but fell below the limit after the required 2.8-liter fuel extraction, costing him the win. The result marked another unexpected Mercedes triumph, surprising given the team's struggles earlier in the season and their position outside championship contention. Russell's one-stop strategy proved brilliantly executed despite pre-race simulations favoring a two-stop approach, demonstrating exceptional tire management that earned praise even from rivals. Mercedes' resurgence stems from consistent upgrades since Miami, though the team abandoned a new floor on Friday due to balance issues, reverting to a known baseline that proved competitive. McLaren's Oscar Piastri and Lando Norris had the fastest car but squandered opportunities through execution errors, with Norris eliminated early at Turn One. Max Verstappen, despite a ten-place penalty, lacked the pace advantage needed for his typical comeback win, while Sergio Pérez's fifth-place finish disappointed Red Bull and raises questions about his future. Charles Leclerc secured an unlikely podium after Russell's disqualification, capping a weekend highlighting Ferrari's continued struggles relative to top teams.
