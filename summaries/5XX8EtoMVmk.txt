This video follows a jewelry maker creating a sentimental mother's ring using his children's baby teeth as the centerpiece stones, tapping into a Victorian-era tradition of incorporating personal mementos into jewelry. The creator shapes the extracted teeth using diamond wheels to resemble gemstone cuts—particularly baguette and cushion shapes—then designs a vintage-style ring using 3D modeling software. He fabricates the ring through lost-wax casting, creating a mold with investment powder, burning out the resin model in a kiln, and pouring molten 14-karat gold into the cavity using a vacuum casting machine. After finishing the gold casting with files and sanders, he carefully sets each tooth into custom-made gold prongs. When the largest central tooth unexpectedly cracks during setting, he pivots creatively by substituting a blue-green sapphire his wife had admired for years, creating an even more meaningful final piece. The video targets craft enthusiasts, jewelry makers, and sentimental parents interested in transforming keepsakes into wearable art, while addressing the initial awkwardness some viewers might feel about this unconventional material choice.
