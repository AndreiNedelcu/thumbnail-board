This video explains the coffee roasting process, covering both the fundamental chemistry and practical machinery involved. It examines how roasting transforms raw green coffee beans into the flavorful product consumers enjoy, detailing how heat causes browning reactions, pressure buildup, and the release of gases during "first crack" and "second crack." The presenter explains the relationship between roast duration and flavor characteristics—bitterness increases with longer roasts while acidity and origin characteristics decrease, which is why specialty coffee companies favor lighter roasts to preserve distinct flavor profiles. The video discusses roast levels (light, medium, dark) and how roasters use roast profiles—precisely controlling heat over time using temperature probes and computer software—rather than relying solely on color. It covers quality control methods including profile monitoring, roast loss measurement, and color testing with devices like Agtron meters. The content also compares three main roasting machine types: drum roasters (most common, using convection, conduction, and radiant heat), hot air roasters (air provides both heat and agitation), and hybrid models. This educational content targets coffee enthusiasts wanting to understand the science behind their beverage and how specialty roasters achieve consistent, high-quality results.
