# Summary

This video examines the economic impacts of climate change and argues that doing nothing to address it is the least cost-effective approach. The creator presents evidence that climate change is already costing consumers money across three major areas: food prices have increased due to droughts and heat waves affecting crop yields; taxes are rising to fund military spending, infrastructure adaptation, and healthcare expansion for climate-related diseases; and property insurance premiums are climbing as extreme weather events become more frequent and intense. The video references economic studies showing that the damages from unmitigated climate change will far exceed the costs of climate solutions, with one study finding that limiting warming to 2°C by 2100 costs six times less than climate impacts through 2050. The creator criticizes global fossil fuel subsidies of $13 million per minute as actively making societies poorer while climate solutions across renewables, efficiency, and other approaches are consistently more economically rational. The target audience includes people concerned about economics and climate policy who may underestimate climate change's current financial burden or overestimate the cost of climate action.
