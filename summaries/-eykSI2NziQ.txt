This video analyzes the costume design in the HBO series Succession through the lens of "quiet luxury" and "old money" aesthetics. The creator distinguishes between old money style—an inherited attitude toward understated, logoless fashion passed down through generations of wealthy families—and quiet luxury or "stealth wealth," which refers to anyone with significant disposable income who chooses subtle over flashy pieces. The video breaks down specific character outfits worn throughout the series, identifying designer pieces from brands like Loro Piana, Ralph Lauren, Zenya, and Brunello Cucinelli, explaining how these choices reflect each character's personality and arc. A key observation is that perceived luxury brands like Gucci and Burberry are actually worn by the upper-middle class rather than the ultra-wealthy. The analysis shows how characters' wardrobes shift with their emotional and financial circumstances—notably Kendall's progression toward louder Gucci and Prada pieces as he becomes estranged from his family, and Lucas's deliberately impractical new-money aesthetic. The video targets fashion enthusiasts and Succession fans interested in how costume design communicates character development and socioeconomic status.
