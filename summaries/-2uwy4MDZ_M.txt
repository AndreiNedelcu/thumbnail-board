A graphic designer seeking to enrich his life beyond corporate work decides to rebrand a small, beloved local sandwich shop called Logan's Heroes in Logan, Utah—without their knowledge initially. Through research and interviews with the shop's founder Hammed, he discovers that the business thrives on authentic customer service, fresh ingredients, and genuine personal connections rather than corporate polish. The designer identifies the core challenge: modernizing the brand while preserving its small-town charm and local appeal. He creates a comprehensive rebrand including custom typography, a new logo, a thoughtful color palette, and illustrations inspired by local newspapers, emphasizing that Logan's Heroes is a one-of-a-kind establishment rather than a chain. When revealing the rebrand to the owners, they enthusiastically embrace it, recognizing how the design helps the shop resonate with younger generations while maintaining its heritage. This video targets creative professionals, small business owners, and viewers interested in design philosophy, entrepreneurship, and the tension between preserving authenticity while adapting to changing markets and demographics.
