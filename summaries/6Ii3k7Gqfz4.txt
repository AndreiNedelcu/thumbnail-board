# Summary

The creator undertook a 30-day challenge to read three hours daily while maintaining a full schedule including freelance work, parenting, gym training, and other commitments. Drawing inspiration from Oxford professor Jonathan Bate's observation about declining reading habits among students, the creator implemented three rules: only physical books counted, no audiobooks, and no reading during work hours. The challenge aimed both to increase literary consumption and to "crowd out" digital distractions like social media through habitual reading. Throughout the month, the creator discovered key strategies including always carrying reading material, matching book genres to time of day (lighter science content morning, literary classics evening), and maintaining flexibility by keeping multiple books available to match current mood rather than forcing a predetermined selection. The creator successfully hit the three-hour target on 25 of 30 days, completing approximately 83 hours of reading across various titles. The experience revealed that reading time targets prove more effective than page or book count goals, as they encourage engagement with challenging, dense literature rather than quick, easy reads. The video targets people interested in reading habits, productivity, and literary culture, offering practical strategies for building sustainable reading practices.
