Notion has newly integrated with Cron, the calendar app acquired last year, enabling users to link Notion pages directly within calendar events. The integration allows seamless connection between workspaces, letting users attach relevant meeting notes or project pages that are automatically shared with event invitees through methods like copying page URLs or searching recent pages. Beyond this official feature, the creator demonstrates a custom automation built on Pipedream that addresses a current gap: syncing events from Notion to Google Calendar. This workflow, which can be deployed free through Pipedream, automatically scans a designated Notion database every hour and pushes new events with proper start and end times to Google Calendar, subsequently appearing in Cron. The setup process involves connecting Notion and Google Calendar accounts, selecting a database and date property, then deploying the workflow. While this automation doesn't handle two-way sync or event updates, it provides practical functionality for users wanting calendar events created from Notion tasks. The video targets productivity-focused users seeking to streamline workflows across Notion and calendar applications.
