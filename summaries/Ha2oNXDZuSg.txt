This analysis examines the business dynamics of the cookie industry, tracking how dessert trends cycle through boom-and-bust phases while exploring why Crumbl has achieved unprecedented dominance. The video compares legacy brands like Mrs. Fields, which pioneered premium cookies in the 1970s but declined due to high overhead and the mall recession, with newer competitors like Insomnia and Levain that adopted different strategies—college campus targeting and upscale positioning respectively. Crumbl, founded in 2017, combined these lessons by offering large, photogenic cookies with rotating weekly flavors that drive scarcity and social media virality, scaling to over 900 locations in four years. However, the analysis reveals Crumbl's true endgame: the company aggressively increased franchise fees and royalties while extracting profits through vendor kickbacks, ingredient markups, and payment processing fees. Store-level profitability has plummeted from 30% margins to 10% as sales decline and competition intensifies from similar chains like Chip City and Dirty Dough. The video profiles Cookie Good, a Santa Monica institution that innovated rotating flavors before Crumbl but chose sustainable, modest growth over aggressive franchising, targeting small-business owners interested in genuine entrepreneurship rather than those seeking fast returns.
