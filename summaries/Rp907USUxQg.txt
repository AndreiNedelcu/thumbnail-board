# Summary

This video explores the mystery of VelmaDinkley.com, an obscure fan website dedicated to the character Velma from Scooby-Doo that accumulated over 10 million page views. The creator, who used the pseudonym "John Lightglass," mysteriously abandoned the site around 2017 after maintaining it for approximately 20 years. Through internet archaeology—analyzing metadata, tracking updates across connected websites, and following digital breadcrumbs—the creator eventually locates Lightglass and discovers his real identity is JD, a lighting technician for rock bands. The investigation reveals that Lightglass created the site after being inspired by a childhood friend reminiscent of Velma's character and lost interest as the show's content evolved away from its original simplicity toward more complex characterization. The video simultaneously documents the online Scooby-Doo fandom community's dynamics, generational conflicts between legacy fans, and how one community member named Ashton used the fandom to process his identity as a gay man with a congenital heart condition before eventually leaving to rebrand himself. The narrative weaves together themes of nostalgia, online communities, personal growth, and how our interests reflect deeper personal connections.
