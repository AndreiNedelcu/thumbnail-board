# CECOT: El Salvador's Maximum-Security Mega Prison

This documentary explores CECOT, the world's largest prison by capacity located in El Salvador, constructed in 2022 to house the country's most dangerous criminals following a massive gang crackdown that began in March 2022. The facility, spanning 410 acres with eight separate modules surrounded by electrified 9-meter walls and monitored by 19 watchtowers, was designed to be inescapable. It houses over 40,000 prisoners, primarily members of MS-13 and Barrio 18 gangs who once terrorized El Salvador with extortion and violence. The documentary provides an inside look at the prison's extreme security measures, including full-body scanners, signal jamming, and armed guards, while documenting inmates' harsh conditions: overcrowded cells with minimal personal space, no outdoor access, monotonous meals, and 24/7 surveillance. The filmmakers interview "Psycho," a high-ranking gang member with a 34-year criminal history who has killed over 50 people. The documentary examines the controversial crackdown that reduced El Salvador's murder rate from 104 to 2.4 per 100,000 people by 2023, while questioning whether the approach represents true justice or merely chaos control, given concerns about wrongfully imprisoned individuals. The production targets viewers interested in crime documentaries, criminology, and global security issues.
