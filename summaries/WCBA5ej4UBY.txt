This video investigates a major financial collapse involving fintech companies like Yatta and Juno that partnered with Synapse, a middleman that connected them to Evolve Bank and Trust for FDIC-insured deposits. Nearly $100 million in customer funds disappeared when Synapse filed for bankruptcy, leaving users with frozen accounts and incomplete payouts—some receiving pennies on the dollar while others got nothing. Through internal emails and banking records, the creator traces the crisis to September 2023, when Evolve discovered customers weren't fully funded and demanded Synapse cover a $50 million shortfall while seizing $16 million in fees. The situation deteriorated when Synapse moved customer deposits to other banks on an opt-out basis without proper disclosure, and Mercury Bank—Synapse's largest client with $3 billion in assets—withdrew and was paid in full, while smaller platforms absorbed massive losses. The investigation reveals conflicting ledgers between banks, potentially improper fee charges against customers for years, and questionable decisions made by all parties despite known funding gaps. The video targets viewers interested in fintech risks, banking regulation failures, and consumer financial fraud, presenting evidence that regulatory safeguards failed ordinary people.
