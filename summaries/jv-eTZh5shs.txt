# Summary

This video ranks major European countries as destinations for software developers using a tiered system based on salary, taxes, cost of living, market saturation, interview difficulty, quality of life, remote work flexibility, English-friendliness, and tech ecosystem strength. Germany is placed in B-tier due to medium salaries (€45-95k depending on level), high taxes above 42% at higher brackets, bureaucratic burden, and limited remote work culture, despite offering stability through a mature job market. The United Kingdom earns A-tier status with higher senior salaries (€80-150k), strong financial sector competition, good remote flexibility, and cultural factors enabling crisis navigation, though London's cost of living is prohibitive. The Netherlands achieves A-tier for similar reasons: competitive salaries (€75-150k for seniors), excellent English-friendliness, superior work-life balance, and concentration of major tech companies like Booking and Adyen. Switzerland reaches S-tier for exceptional senior compensation (€120-200k) and quality of life despite high living costs and restricted market access. Ireland also ranks S-tier as the gateway to major US tech firms (Meta, Google, Stripe) with the highest senior packages available in Europe. Poland represents an affordable Eastern European alternative with medium salaries (€20-70k) and flexible B2B tax structures. The analysis targets developers seeking relocation decisions by balancing compensation against living expenses and professional opportunities.
