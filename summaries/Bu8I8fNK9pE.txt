# Summary

The video critiques Nvidia's handling of the RTX 5060 and 5060 Ti GPU launch, explaining why a full review cannot be completed. The reviewer addresses multiple failures: only the 5060 Ti 16GB arrived for testing while the base 5060 and 5060 Ti 8GB remain unavailable, Nvidia provided drivers and review guides only three business days before the embargo with no coordinated review date, and the cards arrived with uncharacteristically buggy software and hardware. The reviewer also highlights Nvidia's broader launch mismanagement, including oversold performance claims, cherry-picked demos, and an artificially elongated rollout across months. Critically, the video emphasizes that Nvidia's MSRP pricing has become meaningless as actual retail prices vastly exceed suggested prices, making value assessments impossible—particularly problematic for supposedly budget-oriented GPUs. The reviewer argues that tariff uncertainty and supply constraints compound the problem. While acknowledging that some AI features like DLSS frame generation are genuinely interesting, the video condemns Nvidia's misleading marketing that ignores downsides like reduced image quality and input latency. Ultimately, the reviewer refuses to participate in the review crunch culture incentivized by Nvidia's management, targeting tech-savvy gamers and industry observers frustrated with corporate practice degradation.
