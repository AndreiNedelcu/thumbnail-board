# Summary

This video provides a comprehensive breakdown of AI agents and agentic workflows, distilled from extensive research including multiple courses, papers, and practical experimentation. The creator explains that true AI agents differ from simple one-shot prompting by using iterative, circular processes to complete tasks. Four major agentic design patterns are covered: reflection (self-improvement through evaluation), tool use (leveraging external capabilities like web search or code execution), planning and reasoning (determining steps and necessary tools autonomously), and multi-agent systems (specialized AI models collaborating on tasks). The video then transitions to multi-agent architectures, detailing design patterns including sequential (assembly-line approach), hierarchical (manager supervising specialized agents), hybrid (combining sequential and hierarchical), parallel (simultaneous processing), and asynchronous systems. The creator demonstrates practical implementation using n8n, a no-code workflow platform, building a Telegram-based AI assistant that accesses Google Calendar and creates events. The content targets both technical and non-technical audiences interested in understanding or building AI agents, emphasizing prompt engineering as a critical skill and offering practical frameworks for implementation.
