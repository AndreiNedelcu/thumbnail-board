# Summary

This video examines the end of cheap AI pricing and its implications for developers and AI-powered coding. Starting June 1st, 2026, Microsoft will significantly raise Copilot multiplier costs—with Claude Opus 4.5 jumping from 3x to 15x pricing and newer models costing five times more. The speaker discusses how agentic workflows, which involve long-running AI agents with memory contexts making multiple API calls, become prohibitively expensive under new pricing structures. He explores two competing narratives: that AI inference was previously subsidized and companies are now revealing real costs, versus that pricing increases stem from IPO preparation and capacity constraints. The speaker argues that while AI-assisted engineers may be somewhat more productive, they still require significant human code review and refinement, making the "10x engineer" narrative flawed. He predicts companies will shift toward cheaper alternatives, open-source models, and self-hosted solutions. For software engineers, this means returning to fundamentals—strong technical skills, disciplined token usage, and understanding where AI genuinely adds value. He criticizes the hype around AI replacing developers, noting that great software requires technical creativity and thoughtful design, not just prompt engineering, and warns that "vibe coding" startups producing low-quality applications will struggle as costs rise.
