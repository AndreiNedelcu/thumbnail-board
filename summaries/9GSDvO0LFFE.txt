# Fitbit Air vs. Whoop Fitness Tracker Comparison

This video compares the Fitbit Air and Whoop fitness trackers, two screenless wearable devices aimed at serious athletes and fitness enthusiasts. The reviewer, a competitive ultimate frisbee player, tested both trackers alongside an Apple Watch across multiple activities including workouts, games, sleep tracking, and recovery monitoring over nearly two weeks. A central differentiator is pricing: Whoop requires a mandatory $200-350 annual subscription and becomes non-functional without it, while Fitbit Air costs $99 upfront with optional $100-annual subscription for premium features. Both devices feature similar form factors as lightweight pucks with optical heart rate sensors, though Fitbit's oval design is slightly smaller and more discreet. The video analyzes workout data accuracy, app features, AI coaching capabilities, battery life, and charging methods. Whoop offers more advanced metrics like strain scores, recovery percentages, and lab integration, while Fitbit Air provides a cleaner interface with accessible AI fitness coaching. The reviewer concludes both devices serve different needs: Apple Watch for beginners, Fitbit Air for intermediate users seeking ownership and data access, and Whoop for serious athletes obsessively optimizing performance despite its subscription model. The target audience includes athletes, fitness enthusiasts, and anyone considering switching from established trackers.
