This is a school comedy musical following a conflict between girls and boys at a newly co-ed academy. When the school council decides to accept boys for the first time, tensions escalate as students compete for the class president position, which will determine whether the school remains all-girls or stays co-ed. Chelsea, a popular girl leading "The Au Girls," campaigns to keep boys out and maintain the school's peaceful all-girls status, while Allan leads the boys' faction seeking equal representation. The video features multiple musical numbers and comedic scenes depicting the rivalry, including campaign debates, sabotage attempts, and romantic entanglements. The turning point comes when both candidates admit to dishonesty—Allan confesses to framing Chelsea and manipulating her affections, while Chelsea reveals she seduced Allan to win votes. Ultimately, both candidates prioritize honesty and unity over victory, with Chelsea winning the election and immediately proposing that boys and girls study together in harmony. The narrative targets younger audiences, combining school-based social drama with musical entertainment and lessons about integrity, acceptance, and choosing kindness over competitive conflict.
