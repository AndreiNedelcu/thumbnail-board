# Summary

This documentary follows the construction of nine massive container ships at a Shanghai shipyard operated by French shipping company CMA CGM, a monumental undertaking completed in under two years rather than the typical 18 months per ship. The vessels represent a technological breakthrough, each capable of carrying 23,000 containers and measuring 400 meters long—taller than the Statue of Liberty at nearly 78 meters high. The key innovation is their propulsion system powered by liquefied natural gas cooled to -161°C rather than traditional oil, significantly reducing greenhouse gas emissions. The documentary details the extraordinary engineering challenges: the massive 51-meter tank holding 18,000 cubic meters of liquid gas with 27-centimeter-thick polyurethane insulation, a 2,000-ton engine generating 85,000 horsepower, and a 92-ton propeller. Production employs a block-assembly method where thousands of independent steel sections are built separately like LEGO pieces then welded together with surgical precision, involving over 6,000 workers from around the world. The film appeals to audiences interested in maritime engineering, industrial manufacturing, environmental innovation, and large-scale construction projects, showcasing the intersection of human expertise and cutting-edge technology.
