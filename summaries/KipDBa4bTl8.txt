Palantir is a data analytics software company founded in 2003 that has evolved from a defense contractor focused on counterterrorism operations into a broader problem-solver for the U.S. Department of Defense and commercial clients. Originally developed to help identify insurgents and IED threats during the War on Terror, Palantir has expanded into AI-powered platforms serving military applications like autonomous drone systems, predictive policing, and AI-defined vehicles, while also securing commercial contracts with major corporations and government agencies worldwide. The company, backed by Peter Thiel and led by CEO Alex Karp, has secured over $1.3 billion in DOD contracts since 2009, alongside controversial work with ICE and international militaries. Its stock surged 340% in 2024, reaching a $250 billion valuation, driven by its pivot toward AI products and commercial expansion. The video targets audiences interested in defense industry dynamics, tech-government relationships, and investment trends, exploring how Palantir represents a new Silicon Valley approach to openly embracing defense partnerships—contrasting with traditional defense contractors like Lockheed Martin and challenging conventional tech industry narratives about avoiding military work.
