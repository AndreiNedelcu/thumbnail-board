# Summary

This psychological analysis examines Joe Goldberg from the Netflix series "YOU," tracing how childhood trauma and abuse created a serial killer with delusional romantic fantasies. The video explores complex trauma, attachment disorders, and moral detachment in Joe's development, beginning with his abusive father, neglectful mother, and traumatic early murder that was reframed as heroic protection. His upbringing in a group home and subsequent grooming by bookstore owner Mr. Mooney—who conflated control with love and punishment with discipline—further warped his ability to distinguish healthy relationships from manipulation. The analysis details how Joe manifests textbook psychopathy through superficial charm, lack of empathy, and cognitive dissonance that allows him to justify serial murder as protective acts. His behavior reflects erotomania (delusional romantic obsession), coercive control tactics including stalking and gaslighting, malignant narcissism, and progressive desensitization to violence. The video demonstrates how Joe doesn't pursue real women but rather constructs fantasies, then systematically eliminates obstacles to these illusions—murdering Benji, Peach, and Beck while continuing his patterns with new victims in Los Angeles. The content targets psychology enthusiasts, true crime audiences, and those interested in understanding pathological behavior through clinical frameworks.
