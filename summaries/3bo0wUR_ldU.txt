This video explores fall fashion inspired by Tokyo street style through the lens of the Japanese magazine Popeye, which has influenced city boy aesthetics since the 1970s. The creator analyzes the October issue's focus on amitur—a Japanese-American style blending workwear, military surplus, British traditionalism, and gorpcore filtered through minimalist Japanese sensibilities. The video highlights both expensive Japanese brands like Auralee and Kazuyuki Kumagai, as well as more accessible options including Margaret Howell, Ralph Lauren, Johnston's of Elgin, and outdoor brands like Nanamica and Scarpa. Key styling tips include layering sweaters as scarves, mixing high-end pieces with thrifted basics, and embracing wider silhouettes in coordinated sets. The creator emphasizes shopping secondhand for quality wool and cashmere sweaters and recommends specific workwear, footwear, and technical bag brands. Aimed at fashion enthusiasts interested in Japanese aesthetics and sustainable shopping practices, the video combines personal styling advice with brand recommendations that balance price points and quality craftsmanship.
