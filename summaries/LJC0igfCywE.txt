# Summary

This episode explores the gut-skin connection and factors affecting skin health, particularly acne and aging. Dermatologist Dr. Justine Kluk discusses why skin matters as both a protective barrier and a diagnostic window into internal health, explaining its three key functions: barrier protection, housing the skin microbiome, and reflecting systemic health. The conversation emphasizes how diet significantly impacts skin through the gut-skin axis, and covers the skin microbiome—millions of microbes living on skin's vast surface area that interact with immune function. While probiotic skincare products are increasingly marketed, the evidence remains preliminary; gentle cleansing is recommended instead. The episode addresses acne as the eighth most prevalent global disease affecting 9.4% of the population with psychological impacts, noting it's increasingly common beyond teenage years, particularly in women in their twenties through forties, with hormonal shifts during perimenopause contributing to recurrence. Key factors driving increased acne prevalence include Western diet adoption and stress levels. The speakers stress that skin health cannot be isolated from overall body health, making dermatology relevant to multiple organ systems and health outcomes.
