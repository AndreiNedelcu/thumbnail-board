# Summary

This video documents the creator's process of designing and building a custom stool perfectly fitted to their body using 3D scanning technology. The project begins with molding foam clay to capture a precise impression of the user's seated form, then scanning it with a phone-based 3D scanning app to convert the physical mold into digital data. The creator manually refines the scanned mesh in CAD software to create smooth, organic lines suitable for CNC machining. The seat is crafted from ash wood (a pun on "ash for my ass") using CNC milling, while the base features bent steel legs welded into a flowing, sculptural design. The video explores furniture ergonomics principles, the challenges of converting organic 3D scans into manufacturable CAD models, metalworking techniques, and wood finishing. The finished stool provides complete posterior support without pressure points, though the creator expresses some uncertainty about whether the steel base aesthetically matches the elegant wood top. This video appeals to makers, woodworkers, engineers, and design enthusiasts interested in custom furniture creation and personalized ergonomic solutions.
