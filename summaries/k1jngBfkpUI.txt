# Summary

This video documents a creator watching a compiled four-hour video containing one second from every YouTube video uploaded by channels with over 1,000 subscribers—approximately 24,000 channels totaling roughly two million video clips. A collaborator named Lemy created a bot that scraped WikiTubia for eligible channels, downloaded their entire video libraries, randomized them, and stitched one second from each video into a 144-tile grid display (9x6 frames) that refreshes every second. The creator attempts to watch the entire compilation while noting patterns of prolific creators like PewDiePie, Peppa Pig, FaZe Rug, and others who occupy consistent squares for extended periods. The video targets YouTube enthusiasts and long-form content consumers interested in algorithmic experiments and internet culture. Throughout the viewing, the creator reflects on the mathematical scale (approximately 8,000 videos per minute), discusses the artistic merit of Lemy's creation, and acknowledges that most viewers likely won't watch the entire unedited compilation, which includes sports highlights, cartoons, gaming content, and various intros representing nearly 18 years of YouTube history.
