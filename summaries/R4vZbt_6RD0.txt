# Summary

This video examines how major food delivery platforms—DoorDash, Grubhub, and Uber Eats—have dramatically increased costs for consumers through excessive fees and middleman capitalism. The creator traces the industry's evolution from restaurant-operated delivery systems to app-based monopolies, showing how companies initially used venture capital to subsidize cheap orders and gain market dominance, then systematically raised prices once customers were locked in. Key concepts discussed include Cory Doctorow's "enshittification" theory (a three-phase platform decay model), predatory fee structures hidden behind regulatory language, and how apps artificially inflate menu prices while charging restaurants exorbitant listing fees. The video argues these platforms provide no genuine innovation—merely functioning as extractive middlemen that have crowded out working systems—and compares this pattern to other industries like dating apps, telemedicine, and streaming services. The creator targets consumers frustrated by delivery costs and advocates for direct restaurant ordering, worker solidarity movements, and antitrust enforcement to reclaim value from venture capital-backed corporations.
