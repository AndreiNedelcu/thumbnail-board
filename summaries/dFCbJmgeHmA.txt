This video presents a dramatic minute-by-minute reconstruction of the Chicxulub asteroid impact 66 million years ago that caused the mass extinction of non-avian dinosaurs. The narrative follows the asteroid's approach, collision with Earth near the Yucatan Peninsula, and its catastrophic planetary consequences, including a magnitude-11 earthquake, kilometer-high tsunamis, global wildfires, and atmospheric heating that vaporized rock into space. The video details how debris falling back through the atmosphere ignited fires worldwide, how the resulting dust and soot blocked sunlight for months, triggering a global winter that crashed temperatures by 25 degrees Celsius and killed over 90 percent of marine plankton. Within days, the food web collapsed; within months to years, 75 percent of all species vanished. The content targets science enthusiasts and educators seeking to understand extinction-level events and Earth's deep history. The presenter uses scientifically grounded but speculative timelines to make the event comprehensible while acknowledging uncertainties in the historical record, ultimately connecting the dinosaurs' demise to mammalian and human evolutionary success.
