This video explains the 3-2-1 backup rule, a data protection strategy that recommends maintaining three copies of important files across two different local storage devices plus one offsite backup. The creator shares his personal experience losing files due to hardware failure and server downtime, then details his own backup infrastructure using a combination of warm storage (active SSDs), cold storage (traditional hard drives like WD Elements), and cloud services including Google Workspace, Amazon Photos for unlimited photo backup with Prime membership, and Backblaze for continuous automated backups. The video discusses various storage options with pricing comparisons, including Dropbox and Google Workspace's enterprise plans, while emphasizing simplicity over overly complicated systems for those with moderate data volumes under 20 terabytes. The target audience includes anyone concerned about data loss—from college students to content creators and small business owners—seeking practical, affordable solutions to protect digital files without requiring expensive network-attached storage systems. The creator recommends starting with a simpler 2-1 system if needed and scaling up gradually as storage requirements grow.
