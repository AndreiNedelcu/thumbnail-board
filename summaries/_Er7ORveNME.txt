# Angry Birds: From Mobile Gaming Phenomenon to Cautionary Tale

Angry Birds exemplifies how a successful franchise can collapse through greed and creative stagnation. Launched in 2009 by Rovio as a superior iteration of the game Crush the Castle, Angry Birds achieved unprecedented mobile success, becoming the top paid app within months and generating over $100 million annually by 2011. The franchise expanded aggressively across merchandise, multiple game variants, and eventually films, with the 2016 movie grossing $350 million. However, Rovio's strategy shifted dramatically toward aggressive monetization through microtransactions and psychologically manipulative game design. When original game iterations like Angry Birds 2 became stale and derivative spin-offs (Candy Crush clones, match-three games) proliferated, core fans grew alienated. The company's controversial decision to remove beloved original titles from app stores while renaming and paywalling the classic game sparked massive backlash on social media. This video traces Rovio's transformation from innovative developer to franchise-milking corporation, documenting staff layoffs, declining game quality post-movie, and the audience's loss of faith, illustrating how prioritizing short-term monetization over player experience destroyed cultural goodwill for a once-revolutionary gaming property.
