# Summary

This video documents a comprehensive power supply testing initiative that has revealed a troubling 23% failure rate among units tested, including highly-rated products sold on major retailers. The channel has developed an extensively instrumented testing facility featuring a Chroma AC source, automated load banks with thirteen independent MOSFETs, oscilloscopes, thermal monitoring, and compliance testing based on ATX specifications. Testing protocols extend beyond standard 80 Plus certification methods, measuring efficiency across 2–110% load ranges and subjecting units to stress tests including overcurrent protection, brownout simulation, and transient load excursions. The investigation uncovered discrepancies between manufacturer ratings and actual performance; notably, the Corsair SF 850L and FSP Vita 850GM exceeded their gold ratings but manufacturers conservatively maintained ratings due to production line variability and design timelines. Six power supplies completely failed catastrophic protection tests, earning a "cannot recommend" designation. The video targets PC builders, enthusiasts, and consumers making purchasing decisions, emphasizing that quality power supplies must protect against fire hazards to the system, computer, and power supply itself while maintaining reliable operation under non-ideal electrical conditions.
