# Summary

This documentary recounts how a group of teenage hackers, primarily operating under the name Crackers with Attitude (led by a 15-year-old from England called Kraka) and including a American hacker known as Default, infiltrated and embarrassed major U.S. intelligence agencies between 2015-2016. Inspired by Edward Snowden's NSA revelations and activist movements within Anonymous, these young hackers—motivated by opposition to U.S. foreign policy, mass surveillance, and other political grievances—exploited basic security vulnerabilities to breach the email accounts of high-ranking officials including CIA Director John Brennan and Director of National Intelligence James Clapper. Through social engineering and publicly available information, they accessed classified documents, personal files, and sensitive databases containing information on thousands of FBI agents and Justice Department officials, ultimately publishing them via WikiLeaks. The narrative traces their technical progression, ideological development, operational security measures, and eventual capture by federal authorities. The video targets audiences interested in cybersecurity, hacking culture, political activism, generational attitudes toward government surveillance, and true-crime narratives involving digital crime.
