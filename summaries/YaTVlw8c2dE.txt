# Summary

This video traces the rise of LockBit, the world's most prolific ransomware-as-a-service operation, from its 2019 origins as ABCD ransomware to becoming a dominant criminal enterprise responsible for extracting over $500 million in ransom payments. The narrative begins with law enforcement's arrest of affiliate Mikhail Vasilev in Bradford, Canada, then pivots to investigating the mastermind "LockBit Support," who operates the organization's infrastructure. The video explains how ransomware-as-a-service functions as a business model where affiliates deploy malware to extort victims in exchange for a percentage cut (LockBit's 20% fee), detailing the platform's evolution from humble beginnings on Russian dark web forums like XSS. Key technological innovations include LockBit 2.0's "StealBit" feature, which exfiltrates data before encryption to increase extortion leverage, and the public naming-and-shaming website that pressures victims through publicized victim lists and leak countdowns. The video covers major attacks on Accenture, Boeing, and critical infrastructure, while contextualizing LockBit's competitive positioning against rival groups like DarkSide and REvil. It targets cybersecurity enthusiasts, true crime audiences, and those interested in understanding modern organized cybercrime operations and law enforcement responses.
