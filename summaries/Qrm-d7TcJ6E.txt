This video analyzes the psychological transformation of Franklin Saint from the TV series Snowfall, examining whether he becomes a psychopath through his rise in the drug trade. The analysis traces Franklin's journey from an ambitious but directionless young man in 1980s South Central Los Angeles to a ruthless drug kingpin, identifying key psychological markers including narcissism, lack of empathy, manipulation, and desensitization to violence. The video explores how Franklin's circumstances—limited legitimate opportunities as a Black working-class man—drive him toward the drug business, where he partners with CIA agent Teddy McDonald and eventually becomes addicted to power and control. The creator draws parallels between Franklin's psychopathic traits and corporate leadership psychology, citing research suggesting CEOs display similar tendencies. Throughout his criminal empire, Franklin sacrifices his best friend Kevin, kills his neighbor André, manipulates gang leaders, and ruins countless lives through crack cocaine distribution. The narrative culminates in Franklin's moment of introspection where he realizes his selfishness has caused immense harm, leading to genuine psychological change and his decision to leave the game. However, this redemption is shattered when Teddy steals his $73 million, leaving Franklin broken but setting up his quest for revenge. The video targets fans of character psychology and crime drama analysis.
