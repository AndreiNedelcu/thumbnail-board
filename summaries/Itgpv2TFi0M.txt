An amateur gardener attempts to grow tomato plants from seeds extracted from a Subway sandwich, testing whether this unconventional source could lead to a profitable vegetable gardening venture. The experimenter acquires extra tomatoes from the sandwich shop, extracts the seeds, and plants them in a DIY greenhouse setup using a container with soil. The seeds sprout surprisingly quickly within days, and the seedlings thrive initially, though they develop "leggy" characteristics (thin, pale stems) around day twenty. Following online advice from a gardening expert, the creator repots the plants by burying the stems deeper to encourage stronger root development. After a three-week road trip where the plants are cared for by family, they return home to find the seedlings have grown considerably larger. The gardener then transplants them into an outdoor garden bed with support structures, hoping for tomato production. However, environmental factors including a windstorm damage the transplanted seedlings, and most fail to thrive in their permanent location. While the experiment technically demonstrates that sandwich seeds can germinate and grow into young plants, the final outdoor results prove unsuccessful, leaving the ambitious business plan unrealized despite genuine effort and research-backed horticultural techniques.
