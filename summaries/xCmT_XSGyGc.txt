# Summary

Ben Chestnut, founder of Mailchimp, reflects on building and selling the email marketing platform for $12 billion after a 20-year journey. Starting as a web design consultancy in 2000, Chestnut and co-founder Dan spent nine grueling years in a consulting toil before discovering email marketing as a core product. The company remained bootstrapped and independent, with no venture funding, allowing the founders to maintain complete control and ownership. In 2009, Mailchimp pivoted to a freemium model, which sparked exponential growth from hundreds of thousands to millions of users. The turning point came when the company achieved $1 billion in annual recurring revenue in late 2021, just months before Chestnut decided to sell to Intuit—timing he credits to luck, as the 2022 economic downturn that followed would have devastated valuation. Chestnut emphasizes that success stemmed from building slowly and organically, maintaining a lean team, solving genuine customer problems, and resisting the pressure to raise money or optimize for venture capital timelines. The conversation targets aspiring entrepreneurs, offering a candid counternarrative to Silicon Valley's glorification of startup culture while acknowledging the genuine hardship required for success.
