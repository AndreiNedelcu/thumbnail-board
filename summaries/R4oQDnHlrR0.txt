This video explores an unusually shaped triangular apartment building in Tokyo's Nima area, examining what makes it one of Japan's most challenging residential spaces. The five-story structure resembles a toblerone bar when viewed from above, with extremely narrow floor plans measuring just over 2.5 meters in width. The featured unit includes basic amenities—a compact kitchen with single induction burner, small refrigerator, bathroom with bathtub and shower, and an air conditioner—but the toilet is located outside the apartment requiring residents to step into the hallway. Notable drawbacks include severe space constraints, significant street noise from nearby traffic, limited electrical outlets (only two), fake brick wallpaper, and a dangerously narrow stairwell with low clearance. Despite these limitations, the apartment rents for only 40,000 yen monthly, making it affordable for budget-conscious residents willing to sacrifice comfort. The video targets viewers interested in urban real estate oddities, tiny space living solutions, and Japanese architecture and culture, providing both practical insights into Tokyo's densest housing and entertainment through exploring extreme living conditions.
