This video explores the effects of consuming a high-dose creatine supplement (28 grams daily for a week) based on recent scientific research suggesting creatine can counteract cognitive decline from sleep deprivation. The creator references a 2024 study demonstrating that creatine at 0.35 grams per kilogram of body weight reverses processing speed and cognitive performance losses after 21 hours without sleep, and documents his personal experience with this "loading phase" protocol. The video explains the biochemical mechanism—creatine's role in ATP production and energy availability to the brain—and discusses how elite athletes use smaller doses (100 mg/kg) to maintain performance on minimal sleep. Key findings from the experiment include noticeable cognitive sharpness and improved mental clarity within one to two hours of ingestion, though accompanied by mild gastrointestinal side effects like nausea and softer stools. The content targets fitness enthusiasts, biohackers, and people interested in optimizing cognitive performance and sleep efficiency through evidence-based supplementation, offering practical dosing protocols comparing short-term loading phases versus long-term maintenance approaches.
