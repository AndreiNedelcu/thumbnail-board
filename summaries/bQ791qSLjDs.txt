The video follows an ultramarathon runner attempting a marathon in World Athletics-banned shoes designed with illegal performance-enhancing technology. The shoes contain three carbon plates instead of the permitted one and feature 58mm foam stack height compared to the regulated 40mm maximum, making them potentially among the fastest footwear ever created. The runner's motivation stems from a personal quest to break his own record of 2 hours 58 minutes set while running a marathon in Crocs, which he then lost to a competitor named Kevin by seven minutes. By testing these banned shoes, he aims to verify whether a sub-2:50 time is achievable, which would inform his training strategy for reclaiming the Crocs marathon record. The race takes place at the Mercier Marathon in Spain, a flat course through scenic city streets. Throughout the approximately 42-kilometer run, the athlete documents challenges including dropped nutrition supplies, cramps, blisters from the laceless shoes, and the mental difficulty of marathon running. Despite targeting 2 hours 50 minutes, he finishes slightly slower while acknowledging that using banned equipment amounts to cheating, yet emphasizes that the journey, training, and shared experience with other runners proved more valuable than the time achieved.
