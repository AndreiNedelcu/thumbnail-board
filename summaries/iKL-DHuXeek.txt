This episode covers major tech news from Computex, with the primary focus on Google's new fake call detection feature for Android phones that uses cryptographic verification over encrypted RCS to prevent AI voice cloning scams. The hosts discuss how the feature sends real-time confirmation signals between devices to warn users of spoofed calls, while noting current limitations requiring both parties to use Google's Phone app, Android 12+, and have RCS enabled. They explore the urgency of this technology through personal anecdotes about AI voice scams, including a detailed story about a deceptive call impersonating a Canadian firefighters curling association fundraiser. The episode also celebrates Paint.NET finally securing its paint.net domain after 22 years of squatting, discusses other notable domain situations like steam.com and nissan.com, and touches on other announcements including Nvidia's RTX Spark chip for Windows laptops, AMD extending AM5 socket support through 2029, and Proton Mail's Gmail integration. The hosts target tech-savvy audiences interested in emerging security threats, consumer technology developments, and internet culture ironies.
