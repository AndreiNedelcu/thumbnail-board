David Lee, CEO and founder of IYUNO, shares the unvarnished journey of building the world's largest media localization company. IYUNO specializes in adapting content for global streaming platforms—not merely translating but recreating emotions and cultural context for clients including Netflix, Amazon, Disney, and Sony Pictures, producing around 600,000 hours annually. Lee recounts starting with just $6,000 in 2002 as a part-time venture during his final months before graduate school, initially automating DVD subtitle workflows. The company scaled by targeting progressively larger clients, relocating to Singapore to pursue international expansion, establishing offices across Asia, and eventually acquiring European competitors to become a global player. Key to success was developing proprietary cloud-based supply chain management software enabling resource optimization across distributed offices, securing SoftBank Vision Fund backing in 2018, and orchestrating major acquisitions including BTI and SCI. Lee emphasizes the importance of physical presence in new markets, cultural openness, cost optimization through technology, repeated small successes building resilience, and constant internal communication to align employees with company vision. The audience is primarily entrepreneurs and business leaders interested in scaling strategies and company culture.
