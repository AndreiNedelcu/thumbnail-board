The video provides a comprehensive exploration of how color works, starting from the fundamental physics that color is light with different wavelengths that get absorbed and reflected by pigments on objects. It explains how the human eye perceives color through three types of cone cells (S, M, L) sensitive to different wavelengths, and how the brain combines these signals to create our perception of color—including non-spectral colors like pink and magenta that don't exist as single wavelengths. The video then traces the history of color preservation in photography, explaining how the RGB additive color model developed from Maxwell's theory contrasts with the RYB subtractive model used in painting. It delves into multiple color spaces (XYZ, LAB, LCH, HSL) designed to represent colors mathematically for different applications, and covers display technologies from CRTs to OLED, explaining how each achieves color accuracy differently. The content addresses modern standards like sRGB, Adobe RGB, DCI P3, and Rec. 2020, culminating in discussions of HDR technology that dramatically expands the brightness and color range displays can show. This technical yet accessible explanation targets viewers interested in understanding the science behind everyday color perception and digital media.
