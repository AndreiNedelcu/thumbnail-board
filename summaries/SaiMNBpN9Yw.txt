# Beast Games: An Autopsy

This video provides a detailed critical analysis of MrBeast's Amazon Prime reality competition show *Beast Games*, examining the game design and storytelling execution rather than controversies surrounding the creator. The analyst reviews each game from the show's episodes, evaluating how effectively they work as entertainment and whether they accomplish their apparent narrative goals. A central criticism involves MrBeast's repeated use of "self-sacrifice" challenges that lack narrative coherence—asking contestants to sacrifice themselves for randomly assigned groups they have no connection to, undermining the emotional stakes the show seems to intend. The video compares the show's heavy influence from *Squid Game* but argues MrBeast has missed the lesson that *Squid Game* succeeds through careful writing and character development, not just high-stakes mechanics. The analyst also critiques the opening episode's games involving button-pressing and bribe mechanics, noting the disconnect between what's genuinely tense for contestants and what's entertaining for viewers. By episode two and three, some games like the captain bribe challenge work better by creating authentic social pressure. The video targets viewers interested in game design, reality television criticism, and storytelling mechanics, offering detailed breakdowns rather than sensationalism.
