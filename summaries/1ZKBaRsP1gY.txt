This explainer traces Xi Jinping's rise to power through the history of China's Communist Party, beginning with Mao Zedong's consolidation of authority during the Long March and Civil War. The video explains China's power structure, including the Central Committee, Politburo, and the elite Politburo Standing Committee, showing how Mao exploited these institutions to maintain absolute control through purges, personality cult-building, and ideological domination that caused tens of millions of deaths during the Cultural Revolution. After Mao's death, Deng Xiaoping introduced power-sharing to prevent dictatorship, establishing norms of collective leadership that drove three decades of economic growth and reform. Xi Jinping, son of a purged revolutionary, strategically positioned himself by working in provincial posts away from Beijing's elite competition, cultivating supporters while appearing humble and ambitious. Upon returning to power in 2012, Xi systematically removed rivals through anti-corruption campaigns, consolidated control over the party and military, and reintroduced the Mao-style system of personal ideological dominance through a third historical resolution making "Xi Jinping Thought" unquestionable party doctrine. The video targets viewers seeking to understand contemporary Chinese politics and geopolitics, explaining how Xi has become China's most powerful leader since Mao despite post-Mao reforms designed to prevent such concentration of authority.
