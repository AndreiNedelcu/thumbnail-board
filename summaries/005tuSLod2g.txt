# Summary

This video documents a civilian's unprecedented participation in a condensed version of the Army Special Forces Assessment and Selection (SFAS), the notoriously difficult three-week pipeline that trains Green Berets. Over four days, the participant and a fellow candidate navigate intense physical and mental challenges including a PT test with push-ups, planks, pull-ups, and a two-mile run; the infamous "Nasty Nick" obstacle course featuring over 20 progressively harder obstacles; twelve hours of land navigation through forests and waterways with map and compass; and a team event requiring the pair to transport heavy apparatuses—including 120-pound loads and 560-pound rigs made from ammo cans, pipes, and tires—across unknown distances. Rather than being scored numerically, candidates are evaluated on eight "RSOFT" core attributes: integrity, courage, perseverance, personal responsibility, professionalism, adaptability, teamwork, and capability. The video emphasizes the psychological and physical toll of sleep deprivation, heat stress, and relentless exertion, while highlighting that actual SFAS lasts twenty-one days with an under-26% success rate. Designed for audiences interested in military training, personal challenge content, and Special Forces culture, the video showcases the mental fortitude required to become part of the Army's elite unit.
