# Video Summary

This video recounts the true story of how a Microsoft developer named Andres Freund discovered and stopped a massive cyberattack targeting the internet's critical infrastructure. The attack centered on XZ Utils, a widely-used open-source data compression tool maintained by Lassa Collin, which is embedded in nearly all major Linux distributions that power servers, supercomputers, hospitals, and government systems worldwide. After Collin expressed burnout, a seemingly helpful contributor named Jia Tan gradually gained access to the project through years of careful trust-building, eventually becoming co-maintainer and inserting malicious code—a hidden backdoor—into XZ Utils that would have given attackers remote access to millions of systems globally. Freund noticed unusual CPU delays in SSH performance while testing the software and traced the problem to the backdoor, alerting authorities on March 27, 2024. His discovery prevented what could have been a catastrophic attack affecting critical infrastructure worldwide. The investigation suggests the attack was a sophisticated state-sponsored operation, possibly linked to Russian hackers, highlighting how vulnerable open-source infrastructure is when maintained by stretched volunteers and emphasizing the need for better support structures for open-source communities.
