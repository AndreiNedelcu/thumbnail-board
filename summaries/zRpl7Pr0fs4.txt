This video covers the arrest of Alex Mashinski, founder of Celsius Network, for operating what investigators determined was a massive Ponzi scheme that defrauded cryptocurrency investors of billions of dollars. The Department of Justice, SEC, CFTC, and FTC jointly filed charges including securities fraud, wire fraud, and market manipulation against Mashinski, who faces up to 115 years in prison. The investigation reveals that Celsius secretly used customer deposits to artificially inflate the price of their own CEL token, which insiders including Mashinski and Chief Revenue Officer Roni Cohen Pavon then sold for personal profit—Mashinski made $42 million. The company repeatedly lied to customers about profitability, token sales, revenue sharing, and Mashinski's personal token sales while knowing the business was insolvent. The video includes an interview with James Block from Dirty Bubble Media, who identified Celsius as fraudulent early by researching Mashinski's fabricated background claims. This content targets cryptocurrency investors concerned about scams and fraud in the digital asset space, as well as those following ongoing crypto industry accountability.
