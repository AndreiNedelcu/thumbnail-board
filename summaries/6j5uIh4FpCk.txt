On March 20, 1995, during Tokyo's morning rush hour, five members of Aum Shinrikyo released approximately five liters of sarin nerve agent across three subway lines in a coordinated attack that killed thirteen people and injured thousands. The attack was orchestrated by Shoko Asahara, a charismatic blind cult leader who had built Aum Shinrikyo from a yoga school into a militarized organization with a billion-dollar budget, elite scientists, and a twisted theology blending Buddhism with apocalyptic ideology. Asahara positioned himself as a god destined to lead humanity through Armageddon to a utopian paradise called Shambala, convincing highly educated followers including doctors, chemists, and physicists to develop weapons of mass destruction. After failed biological weapon experiments with botulinum toxin and anthrax, the cult focused on sarin production, initially testing it on sheep and in a targeted attack against lawyers opposing them. Facing a police raid following a kidnapping investigation linked to soil samples containing sarin precursors, Asahara ordered the subway attack as a desperate measure to divert law enforcement attention and trigger the chaos he believed would prove his prophetic power and justify his followers' absolute devotion to his apocalyptic vision.
