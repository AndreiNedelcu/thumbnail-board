The creator designs and tests a transparent polycarbonate hunting boomerang to understand how these ancient weapons work and measure their destructive potential. Using research on aerodynamics, he explains that boomerangs function as airfoils that generate lift, with the angle between wings determining whether they return to the thrower. He carefully shapes, sands, and polishes the polycarbonate material to create a finished weapon that achieves a 73-meter flight distance. Testing against various targets including watermelons, pineapples, aluminum cans, plastic bottles, and a ballistic dummy, he demonstrates the boomerang's lethality through both throwing and swinging techniques. The weapon generates approximately 69 joules of energy at around 21 meters per second, comparable to a deadly sling tested previously. His results confirm that hunting boomerangs can inflict serious damage through both impact and cutting, making them genuinely dangerous projectiles. The video targets engineering enthusiasts and viewers interested in historical weapons analysis combined with practical materials testing.
