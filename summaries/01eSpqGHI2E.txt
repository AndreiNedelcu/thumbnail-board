This video documents a year-long breeding project involving parthenogenetic morning geckos, a species where females reproduce asexually without males through a self-fertilization process. The creator starts with two geckos acquired for desk decoration but quickly encounters exponential population growth as the females begin laying eggs prolifically. The narrative follows the construction of bioactive enclosures—elaborate ecosystems featuring live plants, springtails, isopods, and other organisms that create self-cleaning environments. Key challenges include managing escape risks, preventing cannibalism of hatchlings, controlling fruit fly infestations, and tracking an increasingly unmanageable population that spirals beyond the creator's ability to count accurately. The project showcases ecological concepts like parthenogenesis and bioactive terrarium setup while maintaining a humorous, chaotic tone as the "army" grows from 2 to 23 geckos with additional individuals lost in the creator's house. The audience includes reptile enthusiasts, bioactive enclosure builders, and viewers interested in breeding projects and natural ecosystem dynamics, though the content also attracted controversy regarding the project's sustainability and ethics.
