This video explores the deceptive techniques game developers employ to create convincing water effects without simulating actual water physics. The creator, developing a witch-themed open-world RPG, examines how games fake water through mathematical wave modeling, vertex displacement, normal maps, and texture techniques—strategies that have remained effective since Wave Race 64 nearly 30 years ago. The video discusses the progression from water as impassable barriers (Dark Souls, early Assassin's Creed) to surface-level swimming to full underwater experiences, explaining technical challenges like buoyancy simulation, caustic effects, and underwater fog. Key technologies covered include split-screen effects showing above and below water simultaneously, subsurface scattering for realistic light transmission, and audio design that enhances immersion through filtered underwater sound. The creator demonstrates specific implementations in their game, from splash particle effects to breathing mechanics tied to stamina systems. The video targets aspiring game developers and technical enthusiasts interested in graphics programming, providing both historical context and modern examples from titles like Red Dead Redemption 2, Cyberpunk 2077, and Subnautica while emphasizing that sophisticated faking techniques have become so effective that full fluid simulation remains unnecessary for most contemporary games.
