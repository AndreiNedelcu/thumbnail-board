This video addresses two significant issues with the iPhone 14 Pro and Pro Max. The primary problem involves multiple users reporting grinding noises, vibration, and camera shakiness when using third-party apps like TikTok, Snapchat, and Instagram, with affected photos appearing blurry and distorted. Tech YouTuber Luke Miani's experience gained enough traction that mainstream outlets like The Verge covered the story. The issue appears isolated to third-party applications, suggesting a possible software bug rather than hardware failure, which could potentially be resolved through updates. The secondary concern focuses on the iPhone 14 Pro's continued reliance on the outdated Lightning connector with USB 2.0 transfer speeds (480 Mbps), despite the device's ability to capture large 48-megapixel Pro Raw photos. This is particularly frustrating given that Apple's own iPad Pro from 2015 supported USB 3.0 speeds up to 5 Gbps, making the newer Pro iPhone technically less capable. The video targets tech enthusiasts and iPhone users concerned about quality control and Apple's perceived lack of innovation in connectivity standards.
