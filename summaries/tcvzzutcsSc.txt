# Summary

This video examines how dating apps have fundamentally altered modern courtship while operating business models that incentivize keeping users single. The creator argues that dating apps exploit subscription-based retention metrics—companies profit when users remain in search mode rather than finding partners—and deliberately frame singlehood as desirable to counter societal pressure. The video traces how technology has decoupled sex from relationships, enabled "sex interviews" that skip traditional dating, and created digital-first interactions that avoid real-world rejection. However, despite unprecedented access to potential partners, Millennials and Gen Z are having less sex than previous generations due to financial insecurity, mental health challenges, and digital interactions replacing human connection. The analysis explores how economic factors—housing costs, student debt—make traditional life paths increasingly unattainable, forcing younger generations to rationalize delayed relationships as choices rather than acknowledge systemic constraints. The creator targets young adults and those interested in economics, technology criticism, and generational analysis, presenting data on dating preferences, costs, and psychological impacts while questioning whether delayed relationships reflect genuine desire or coping mechanisms for larger societal failures.
