This video uses satellite imagery to document the global impact of the COVID-19 pandemic across multiple continents. Starting with the outbreak's origins in Wuhan in late 2019, the video traces the virus's spread through China, the Middle East, Europe, and the United States, analyzing visible changes captured from space. Key observations include empty streets in Wuhan following lockdown measures, rapid construction of emergency hospitals completed in days, unusually high grave-digging activity in Iran's holy city of Qom indicating undercounted deaths, and the dramatic transformation of major cities like Venice and New York as people shelter in place. The video particularly emphasizes dramatic shifts in global aviation, showing airport runways converted to airplane parking lots at major hubs like Frankfurt and Copenhagen, as well as satellite-tracked decreases in nitrogen dioxide emissions across industrial regions in China, Italy, and Europe. By presenting these geospatial datasets alongside timeline progression, the video targets an audience interested in understanding the pandemic's scale through data visualization and remote sensing technology, ultimately illustrating how satellite data reveals the pandemic's unprecedented economic and social disruption on a planetary scale.
