This video presents three historically-based stories that blur the line between reality and fiction. The first recounts the tragic 1899 death of Alice Nott, a reclusive 23-year-old student in Washington DC whose beloved pet parrot Polly had a dangerous habit of inhaling illuminating gas from light fixtures for intoxication. When Alice closed her window on a cold night to avoid catching a chill and fell asleep, Polly continued accessing the gas valve, filling the room with lethal fumes that killed Alice while the parrot survived by seeking fresh air at the door. The second story follows Dr. Linda Gaudy, who in 1993 had a vivid premonition of dying in a car crash during a planned Caribbean cruise, prompting her to cancel the trip. However, she misinterpreted her vision—she was indeed murdered in her car, but at her Massachusetts hospital, not in the Caribbean, allegedly by her controlling boyfriend Tim. The third narrative begins with John McCall, an 18th-century Irish doctor, mourning his wife Marjorie's death from fever. The content targets audiences interested in true crime, mysterious historical incidents, and paranormal phenomena, delivered through compelling narrative storytelling about surprising deaths and unexplained events.
