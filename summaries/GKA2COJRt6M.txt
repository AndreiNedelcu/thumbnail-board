# Summary

This video explores the ZPM Nocturne, a failed 2011 Kickstarter espresso machine that raised $370,000 but never delivered a single unit to its 1,500 backers, and traces how its collapse inadvertently influenced the creation of the Decent DE1, arguably the most revolutionary espresso machine of the past decade. The ZPM aimed to combine off-the-shelf components with proprietary thermoblock and grouphead designs to create a technically advanced machine at an unprecedented $200 price point, but the founders underestimated the challenges of scaling from prototypes to mass manufacturing and ultimately failed to achieve safety certification by October 2015. The video features an interview with John Buckman, founder of Decent Espresso, who explains how his initial interest in ZPM led him to examine its design philosophy before ultimately building a different machine that prioritized direct-to-consumer sales and measurement-driven espresso brewing. Through hands-on testing with pressure and temperature measurement devices, the host demonstrates that the ZPM unit, while featuring an intuitive interface and interesting design, struggles with significant temperature inconsistencies and pressure regulation issues, ultimately questioning whether it could have ever delivered on its promises. The content targets coffee enthusiasts, espresso technology followers, and those interested in crowdfunding failures and product development.
