Japan's three-decade economic stagnation ended in 2024 when the Bank of Japan finally raised interest rates for the first time since 2007, exiting its negative rate experiment. The video traces Japan's dramatic economic journey from post-WWII recovery through an explosive 1980s bubble—when Tokyo's Imperial Palace land was valued equivalent to California—to the subsequent crash that triggered decades of deflation and wage stagnation. Facing persistent deflation, the BOJ employed increasingly unconventional tools including quantitative easing, negative interest rates, and yield curve control, yet these measures failed to reignite sustainable growth. The catalyst for change came unexpectedly through external factors: the 2022 Ukraine war sparked inflation and yen weakness, prompting other central banks to raise rates while Japan finally hit its 2% inflation target. This external pressure, combined with modest wage growth from labor agreements, pushed authorities to normalize policy. The documentary targets economics-focused audiences and those interested in monetary policy, examining how Japan's exceptional monetary experiment shaped daily life and what implications the policy shift holds for mortgages, government debt payments, currency strength, and both consumer prices and investment opportunities.
