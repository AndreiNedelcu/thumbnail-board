This video documents the remarkable engineering and coordination required to transform the United Center in Chicago from a hockey rink to a basketball court overnight. Following the Chicago Blackhawks' game against Detroit on November 6th, a conversion crew of approximately fifty people—a mix of full-time staff and part-timers—begins a methodical process that must be completed within 22 hours before the Chicago Bulls' game begins. The conversion involves removing protective glass panels, resurfacing the ice with a Zamboni, dismantling and repositioning hockey boards, converting telescopic seating configurations to accommodate a shorter basketball court with additional seating capacity, installing 530 pieces of insulated plywood flooring, laying down the basketball court itself in precisely numbered sections, and installing hoops and related equipment. Key challenges include managing the geometric differences between the 200-by-85-foot hockey rink and the 94-by-50-foot basketball court, operating specialized equipment like hovercrafts for moving seating sections, and maintaining strict precision to ensure proper alignment. The entire process takes approximately two-and-a-half hours, and the crew repeats this regularly throughout the season while managing variations for concerts and other events, demonstrating an impressive feat of logistics that entertains Chicago audiences year-round.
