# Summary

This video explores Boulder, Colorado, America's "skinniest city," where 42% obesity rates contrast sharply with the community's fitness-focused culture. The creator documents the town's infrastructure supporting health—60 public parks, only two McDonald's, extensive bike lanes, high-end health restaurants like Just Be Kitchen and Flower Child, and the nation's largest Whole Foods. While the surface presents an idyllic active lifestyle with rock climbing, hiking, and outdoor recreation deeply embedded in youth culture, the video reveals a darker psychological underbelly: Boulder leads the nation in eating disorder rates, with nearly triple the national average among University of Colorado Boulder women. Through interviews with locals, teenagers, and eating disorder recovery specialist Brooke Alexander, the creator uncovers widespread disordered eating patterns often disguised as healthy living. Conditions like atypical anorexia—restricting calories while maintaining normal weight—go undetected. Residents describe intense body image pressure, particularly affecting women and younger girls, with some admitting they skip meals or obsessively exercise. The video targets general audiences interested in wellness culture's hidden costs, examining how communities emphasizing fitness and thinness can paradoxically foster mental health crises and eating disorders among inhabitants.
