Clear space is the protective area surrounding a logo that prevents other design elements like typography or layout components from overlapping or crowding it. This video explains the concept and demonstrates practical techniques for establishing clear space in graphic design. The presenter uses a method of borrowing shapes from within the logo itself—such as individual letters or geometric elements—and replicating them around the logo's perimeter to create consistent spacing guides. This approach ensures visual harmony across different applications and marketing materials. The tutorial covers three logo variations: a pictorial mark, a script-based wordmark, and a geometric icon, showing how clear space principles adapt to different design styles. The video also explains how to export clear space guidelines as artboards in SVG format for easy implementation in grids and digital layouts. Targeting graphic designers and logo design professionals, the content emphasizes that establishing clear space takes only minutes but significantly benefits both designers working with the logo later and maintains brand consistency across all applications.
