# Video Summary

This video explores the emerging concern of microplastics accumulating in the human brain—currently comprising about 0.5% of brain weight—and presents evidence-based strategies for removal and prevention. The presenter explains how microplastics enter the brain via two pathways: through the bloodstream crossing the blood-brain barrier and via inhalation through the olfactory bulb. For removing microplastics from circulation, the video recommends plasma donation as the most accessible method, while also discussing plasma exchange, extracorporeal blood oxygenation (EBOO), and ultraviolet blood irradiation as clinical options. To clear microplastics already in the brain, the video emphasizes the glymphatic system's role during sleep, recommending adequate deep sleep and melatonin optimization. Additional removal strategies include sauna use and sweating, which excrete microplastics and related toxins. The video provides ten practical lifestyle modifications—switching from plastic to glass containers, wooden cutting boards, stainless steel cookware, and cotton underwear—to minimize microplastic exposure from food, cooking, and everyday products. The target audience includes health-conscious individuals interested in biohacking, longevity optimization, and understanding modern environmental health risks.
