# Video Summary

City Corp Center in Manhattan became a structural crisis in 1978 when engineer Bill Lameasure discovered that its innovative design had a fatal flaw. To accommodate a church that refused to be demolished, the building was constructed on stilts at the midpoint of each face rather than at the corners. Lameasure ingeniously solved this challenge with diagonal chevron bracing and a tuned mass damper—a 400-ton concrete block on the roof that absorbs swaying motion. However, cost-cutting decisions to use bolts instead of welds for the braces, combined with a critical oversight, created danger: the original calculations hadn't accounted for quartering winds hitting the building diagonally. When Lameasure recalculated, he found the braces needed 14 bolts but had only four, and worst-case wind scenarios could trigger collapse with a one-in-six annual probability. Facing potential professional ruin but knowing thousands of lives depended on his actions, Lameasure reported the problem to leadership and emergency measures were implemented to reinforce the building. The video explores how one engineer's honesty prevented a potential catastrophe and how architectural compromise can create unexpected structural vulnerabilities.
