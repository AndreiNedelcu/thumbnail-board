This automotive review examines the Fisker Ocean, a midsize electric SUV positioned against competitors like the Tesla Model Y and Hyundai Ioniq 5 in the $70,000-$75,000 price range. The reviewer praises the Ocean's exterior design, comfortable seating, good visibility, and several innovative features including a motorized rear window, solar roof panels promising 3-6 miles of daily range, and a unique high-mounted turn signal. However, the core of the critique focuses on pervasive software and engineering issues that undermine the vehicle's fundamentals. Problems include incomplete software lacking solar panel energy tracking, a buggy 360-degree camera system, inconsistent Bluetooth connectivity, flashing dashboard displays during throttle transitions, missing brake hold functionality, unresponsive key fob communication, and frequent unexplained error messages from driver assistance systems. The reviewer also highlights confusing design choices like drive modes accessible only through an unlabeled button, a 500-use lifetime limit on launch control, and interior storage compromises. The video targets potential EV buyers and enthusiasts seeking honest long-term ownership insights beyond brief test drives, ultimately concluding that despite promising initial qualities, the car's incomplete execution makes it unreliable for daily use.
