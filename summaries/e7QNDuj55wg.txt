This apartment tour showcases a 500-square-foot Toronto residence that blends curated design, technology, and personal collections into a cohesive living space. The owner, who has occupied the apartment for six years with a roommate and two cats, emphasizes collecting meaningful objects—books, records, CDs, sculptures, and vintage furniture—that inspire and reflect their personality rather than adopting minimalism. Key design elements include a CB2 and Goop couch, vintage pieces like 1960s nightstands and an Eames-inspired desk, statement furniture such as a Bang and Olufsen CD player and Samsung Serif television, and smart home integration through Apple HomeKit controlling lights, blinds, and thermostats. The space features multiple display and storage solutions from IKEA and custom builds, extensive vinyl and CD collections, decorative gallery posters, and practical workspace combining a vintage Orix desk with a modern standing frame and Herman Miller Embody chair. The tour targets design enthusiasts, collectors, and home decor audiences interested in how to maximize small spaces through thoughtful curation, vintage finds from thrift stores and marketplaces, and the intersection of functionality with aesthetic appeal in urban living.
