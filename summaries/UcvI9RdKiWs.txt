This video compares the 11-inch and 12.9-inch iPad Pro models through a week-long practical test to determine whether the larger screen size justifies the trade-offs. The creator uses both devices for everyday tasks including video editing reference monitoring, spreadsheet work, PDF annotation, and content consumption across various settings like home, cafes, and production sets. Key factors evaluated include weight differences (roughly 200 grams), screen real estate and productivity gains, display technology (mini-LED versus LCD), brightness levels (600 nits sustained brightness for both models, with newer M4 versions reaching 1,000 nits), and portability. The creator also incorporates personal context about using iPads as secondary monitors alongside MacBook Pro setups, production work, and family usage patterns. Targeting creative professionals and iPad buyers seeking to choose between sizes, the video concludes that the 12.9-inch model offers superior productivity benefits for professional use cases like spreadsheets and document work, while acknowledging the 11-inch excels only in specific scenarios like fitting in car seat gaps. The creator emphasizes that buying used units can offset the price premium.
