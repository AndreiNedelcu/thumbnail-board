# Summary

This video demonstrates a crow's problem-solving abilities by testing a named crow called Cheryl through an elaborate nine-puzzle escape room designed to access her favorite food reward. The creator, motivated by crows stealing his food deliveries, constructed increasingly complex challenges incorporating physics principles, electronics, and behavioral psychology—including a water-level puzzle requiring rock placement, a balance scale challenge, facial recognition test using portraits, money collection with NFC chips, tool use for fishing, cup stacking, string manipulation, and an egg drop requiring gravitational force. For comparison, young human participants attempt the same puzzles, revealing contrasting approaches to problem-solving. The video explores crow cognition through interactive engineering demonstrations while examining documented crow intelligence in nature, such as their ability to use tools, coordinate actions, recognize human faces over years, and engage in strategic thinking. The content appeals to audiences interested in animal behavior, engineering challenges, DIY STEM projects, and comparative intelligence between species, blending educational content about animal cognition with entertainment and technical demonstration.
