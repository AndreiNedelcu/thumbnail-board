# Summary

The creator conducted a secret shopping experiment on their own store by hiring five community members to purchase products and evaluate customer service. Each shopper received $300 and was asked to fabricate reasonable complaints while also reporting on any genuine issues encountered. The experiment revealed mixed results across multiple customer service interactions. Key problems identified included damaged thermal pads that leaked during shipping, inventory stock-outs on desk pads, sizing discrepancies with merchandise, and complicated international tax policies causing double-charging. The Creator Warehouse support team's responses varied from immediate, no-questions-asked replacements (earning five-star ratings) to delayed responses over weekends and form-letter replies that initially seemed dismissive before eventual escalation and resolution. Response times ranged from same-day to three days, with Friday submissions taking longer due to reduced weekend staffing. The experiment provides valuable feedback about support quality, inventory management, and policy refinements needed for scaling operations toward one million orders. The video targets both current and potential customers while offering transparency about operational challenges and improvements being implemented.
