This video reviews premium leather cases for iPhone 16, comparing design, material quality, and functionality across eight major brands including Andar, Bullstrap, Grams 28, Lawley, Muo, Nomad, and Ryan London. The reviewer evaluates each case across multiple criteria: leather feel and quality, button and cutout design, MagSafe strength, USB-C opening accommodation, and—most significantly—how each brand handles the iPhone 16's new camera control button. A central frustration throughout is that most leather case manufacturers opted for precision cutouts around the camera control rather than integrated buttons, resulting in recessed, difficult-to-use controls. The video identifies only Apple and Beats cases as having proper button-on-button solutions. Ryan London emerges as the top choice for premium leather quality and craftsmanship, while Andar offers the best value. The review targets consumers seeking durable, aesthetically pleasing leather protection for their new iPhones who prioritize material quality and usability, with prices ranging from $30 to over $100 depending on brand and features.
