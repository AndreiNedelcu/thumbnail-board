# Summary

This review examines the M4 MacBook Air, emphasizing its position as an exceptional value laptop with minimal physical changes from previous generations. The device features Apple's M4 chip—already proven in the iPad Pro and Mac Mini—paired with a base memory increase to 16GB and a price drop back to $999, addressing concerns about the previous generation's cost increase. Key upgrades include a slightly larger battery (52.6 to 53.8 watt-hours), improved 12-megapixel Center Stage webcam, enhanced Thunderbolt connectivity, and a new "Sky Blue" color option that's barely distinguishable from silver. The reviewer highlights the M4's powerful neural engine for AI-specific tasks and notes that despite passive cooling limiting sustained performance compared to the Mac Mini, the baseline M4 MacBook Air handles demanding workflows like 4K/8K video editing that previously required Pro-level chips. The main critique concerns the display quality, which remains unchanged and lacks the mini-LED, high refresh rate, and nano-texture coating reserved for MacBook Pro models. Targeted at budget-conscious consumers seeking reliable everyday computing, the review concludes the M4 MacBook Air represents an easy recommendation for most users, though previous-generation models remain viable budget alternatives at $799.
