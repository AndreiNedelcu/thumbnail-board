Dr. Matthew Johnson from Johns Hopkins discusses psychedelic-assisted therapy, particularly focusing on psilocybin treatment for mental health conditions. He explains the "heroic dose"—30-40 milligrams of psilocybin that produces profound psychological effects—and contrasts it with microdoses and recreational doses. Johnson details his therapeutic protocol, which involves extensive preparation, supervised sessions with eye shades and music, and integration discussions afterward. He emphasizes that psychedelics are physiologically safe compared to other drug classes, though they produce intense subjective experiences ranging from transcendent insights to challenging "bad trips." A key therapeutic mechanism involves patients achieving "duh moments"—fundamental realizations about their own agency and suffering patterns that intellectually knew but emotionally internalize. Johnson highlights psychedelics' potential in treating depression, cancer-related anxiety, and addiction by expanding people's narrowed mental patterns. He also addresses ethical concerns unique to this intimate therapeutic work, warning against boundary violations and "guru complex" dynamics. The audience includes mental health professionals, researchers, patients exploring alternative treatments, and individuals interested in neuroscience and consciousness studies.
