This documentary examines how private equity firms have aggressively acquired British high street companies since Brexit, fundamentally reshaping the UK retail and hospitality landscape. Using the mechanics of leveraged buyouts—where companies are purchased with significant debt financing—the video explains how firms like Clayton Dubilier & Rice acquired major brands including Morrisons supermarket, Pizza Express, The Body Shop, and New Look. The analysis reveals that post-Brexit economic uncertainty and pandemic-induced valuations made British companies attractive bargains compared to their American counterparts, prompting nearly $200 billion in private equity investments between 2016 and 2023. The central problem emerges when interest rates rise: the debt-laden companies struggle to remain competitive, potentially harming consumers through higher prices and job losses. With 1.9 million people employed by private equity-backed firms and another 1.3 million in their supply chains, the stakes are significant. The video targets economically literate audiences interested in finance, policy, and the future of British business, while highlighting the political dilemma facing policymakers who need foreign investment but worry about private equity's destabilizing effects on the economy and employment.
