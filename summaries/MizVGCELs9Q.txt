This video documents a four-year biohacking experiment where the creator tested rapamycin, a drug initially developed for organ transplant immunosuppression that showed promise in animal studies for extending lifespan by up to 34%. Rapamycin works by inhibiting mTORC1, a growth pathway linked to aging, but it also inhibits mTORC2 and suppresses immune function, creating a risk-benefit trade-off. Despite carefully calibrating dosing protocols and monitoring blood levels, the creator experienced side effects including oral infections, cholesterol disruptions, elevated blood glucose, and increased resting heart rate—arguably the most concerning given its impact on sleep quality. The turning point came when a October study using epigenetic clocks revealed that rapamycin actually accelerated biological aging in humans rather than slowing it, contradicting the animal research and the underlying hypothesis. The creator maintains a measured scientific perspective, acknowledging the study could be flawed while recognizing the ironic outcome of taking an anti-aging drug that paradoxically aged him faster. The video targets health-conscious individuals interested in longevity research, biohacking, and understanding how experimental interventions can sometimes produce unexpected results despite rigorous scientific methodology.
