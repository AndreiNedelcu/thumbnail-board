Samsung aims to challenge established enterprise laptop brands with the Galaxy Book 6 Enterprise Edition, equipped with Intel vPro technology to deliver performance, security, and manageability without compromise. The device features a clean, bloatware-free operating system, dual user-accessible M.2 PCIe Gen 4 storage slots, and comprehensive connectivity including Thunderbolt 4, full-size Ethernet, and HDMI. The Samsung Knox embedded security chip supports corporate fleet management, while Samsung's configuration services allow IT departments to preconfigure systems with custom imaging and asset tagging directly from the factory. The standout feature is Intel vPro Fleet Services, a free remote management platform that enables IT teams to control machines even when the operating system is severely compromised, providing capabilities like remote BIOS access, secure erase, and one-click recovery. The video demonstrates vPro's practical value through the Crowdstrike outage example, where vPro-equipped systems recovered in one day versus a week for traditional methods. Available in 14-inch and 16-inch configurations with Intel Core Ultra processors, the Enterprise Edition targets IT departments and users requiring robust remote management solutions for both corporate and personal use cases.
