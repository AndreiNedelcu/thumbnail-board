# Summary

This Wall Street Journal documentary investigates Yevgeny Prigozhin and the Wagner Group, Russia's state-backed paramilitary organization disguised as a private military company. Beginning with Prigozhin's September 2022 recruitment video at Russian prisons, the documentary traces Wagner's evolution from a shadowy operation in Ukraine's 2014 annexation of Crimea to a sprawling international network spanning Africa, Syria, and beyond. The investigation reveals how Wagner operates through approximately 64 shell companies to launder money, exploit natural resources, and conduct military operations while maintaining plausible deniability for the Kremlin. Key findings include Wagner's control of Syrian oil fields generating tens of millions annually, industrialized gold mining operations in the Central African Republic, and documented atrocities including the Boyo Massacre. Using shipping records, corporate databases, and satellite imagery, the documentary exposes how billions in government contracts flow to Prigozhin-linked companies, funding Wagner's recruitment of prison inmates for the Ukraine war. The documentary targets sophisticated audiences interested in geopolitical analysis, financial crime, and the intersection of private military operations with state power, demonstrating how authoritarian regimes obscure military aggression through corporate structures.
