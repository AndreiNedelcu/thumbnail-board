This video documents an innovative permaculture water retention project in Carlton, Oregon, where Brenda Smaffold and collaborator Zachary Weiss from Water Story transformed a degraded grazing farm into a multi-pond ecosystem. The design features cascading unlined ponds that capture and store runoff across steep terrain, creating a "water battery" that recharges the aquifer and sustains year-round greenery. Key engineering elements include rock-armored spillways with biofilters, a precision-engineered ridge road with one-inch gravity tolerance directing water between valleys, and a spring-fed system that increased flow from three gallons to ten to fifteen gallons per minute. The project demonstrates tangible agricultural benefits: longer cattle grazing rotations, improved pasture productivity, and increased farm income alongside ecosystem restoration supporting biodiversity like frogs, dragonflies, and herons. The video targets permaculture practitioners, regenerative farmers, and environmental advocates interested in landscape-scale water management, though it also candidly addresses regulatory challenges the owners faced obtaining retroactive permits, arguing that current wetland protection laws inadvertently obstruct beneficial restoration work while supporting industrial water extraction practices.
