# Summary

This video examines how to determine if you're training with sufficient intensity to maximize muscle growth using scientific evidence and practical methods. The presenter defines muscular failure as the point where muscles cannot produce enough force to move weight, distinguishing between absolute failure and technical failure, while introducing rep speed slowdown as an objective marker approaching failure. The content compares two training approaches: going to failure every set, which limits volume but is time-efficient, versus training closer to but not at failure, which allows greater weekly volume—a key factor in hypertrophy according to meta-analyses. The video introduces RPE (rating of perceived exertion) and RIR (reps in reserve) as subjective tools for quantifying training intensity, presenting expert opinions that range from leaving five to seven reps in reserve to stopping just one to three reps short of failure. Using multiple real-world examples from professional bodybuilders and athletes, the presenter demonstrates how to assess proximity to failure through video analysis and rep speed measurement, while acknowledging that individual differences in tempo and mind-muscle connection affect these assessments. This content targets serious lifters seeking evidence-based strategies to optimize their training intensity without sacrificing recovery or training volume.
