Paris faced a significant challenge in hosting the 2024 Olympics due to water quality issues in the Seine River, which was planned to host the opening ceremony and multiple aquatic events including open water swimming and triathlon. The Seine has been off-limits for swimming for a century because combined sewer systems—infrastructure from the 1850s designed to handle both sewage and stormwater in the same pipes—overflow into the river during heavy rainfall, contaminating it with E. coli and other fecal bacteria. Paris constructed a massive underground infrastructure project called the Alore Basin, a 13.2-million-gallon reservoir costing $1.5 billion, designed to capture excess stormwater and sewage overflow to reduce contamination events from 10-15 times annually to just twice yearly. However, preliminary testing continued to show excessive bacterial levels, and the video explains that water quality can only be verified within 48 hours of events, making success dependent on weather conditions. The documentary targets viewers interested in urban infrastructure challenges, Olympic preparation logistics, and environmental remediation, illustrating how major cities with aging sewer systems struggle with water quality management in modern contexts.
