# Liza Koshy: From Vine Star to Multi-Platform Celebrity

This video analyzes Liza Koshy's career trajectory from six-second Vine videos to becoming one of YouTube's biggest creators with 25 million subscribers, before stepping back from the platform. The creator examines her rise through relationship-based content, her collaboration with David Dobrik and the Vlog Squad, and her strategic "social hacking" through parody videos like her Vogue 73 Questions remake that led to mainstream opportunities including Met Gala hosting, MTV's TRL, YouTube Red projects, and eventually Netflix and Hulu productions. The video identifies three key lessons for aspiring creators: the power of social hacking for growth without direct collaboration, understanding time distribution as a creator (where only five percent involves being on camera while ninety-five percent involves writing, editing, and planning), and the "third-party effect" where building a professional team and treating content as a product rather than personal life creates sustainable, scalable careers. The narrative emphasizes Koshy's evolution from vulnerable personality-driven content to acting and hosting, exploring how creators can transition from bedroom production to professional media operations while potentially risking audience connection, ultimately targeting audiences interested in creator economy strategy and entertainment industry dynamics.
