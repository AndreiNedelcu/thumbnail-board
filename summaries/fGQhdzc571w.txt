On August 13, 2016, a mysterious hacking group calling themselves the Shadow Brokers announced they had breached the NSA's Equation Group, stealing sophisticated cyber weapons including zero-day exploits and surveillance tools like Cottonmouth, Dropoutjeep, and Ragemaster. The group initially attempted to auction the stolen arsenal for one million Bitcoin but received minimal bids, then shifted tactics by releasing IP addresses used by the NSA for global cyber attacks and later posting decryption passwords for free. The FBI arrested Harold T. Martin III, a defense contractor with access to classified materials, suspecting him of the leak, though subsequent Shadow Brokers posts proved he couldn't be responsible. The leaks subsequently enabled ransomware attacks like WannaCry and NotPetya, causing billions in damages. After Trump's election and subsequent strike on Syria—which the Shadow Brokers perceived as betrayal of their political interests—the group released the complete file collection to the public as an act of defiance, fundamentally compromising U.S. cyber intelligence capabilities and demonstrating vulnerability in American national security.
