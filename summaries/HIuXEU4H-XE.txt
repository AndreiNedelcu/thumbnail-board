# MH370 Search Resumed After 7-Year Hiatus

Malaysia Airlines Flight MH370 disappeared on March 7, 2014, with 239 people aboard, and after nearly seven years without organized search efforts, renewed investigation is underway. Evidence suggests deliberate human action rather than accident, including manual disabling of the aircraft's transponder at a strategic location between air traffic control regions, avoidance of Thailand's air defense zone, and sophisticated power management to disable satellite communications systems. Ocean Infinity, a deep-sea exploration company specializing in autonomous underwater vehicles and high-resolution seabed mapping, has restarted the search on a "no find, no fee" basis—they receive $70 million if successful, nothing otherwise. The company previously searched vast areas (112,000+ square kilometers in three months) far more efficiently than the initial three-year effort. New analysis includes detailed Boeing 777 systems studies by experienced pilots and controversial whisper radio propagation data suggesting the aircraft flew in evasive patterns matching simulator data found on the captain's home computer. This video targets aviation enthusiasts and those interested in aviation mysteries, explaining technical evidence while documenting why search resumption now seems justified despite skepticism about discovery prospects.
