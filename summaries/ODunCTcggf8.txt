# Summary

A 34-year-old experimented over 60 days with various natural methods to increase his height, combining supplements, stretching exercises, decompression techniques, improved sleep, and posture correction. His supplement regimen included vitamin D and K2, dietary calcium from cheese and bok choy, magnesium, zinc, protein shakes, and a desiccated organ supplement targeting bone health and growth hormone production. For physical interventions, he built a homemade decompression table for his lower body, created an inversion apparatus using stairs and rope, performed daily spinal decompression stretches, and implemented red light therapy sessions. He also adopted sleep hygiene improvements by blocking light and cooling his bedroom, switched to a firmer sleeping surface, and used a posture-correcting vest to combat slouching. Results showed his morning height measurements gradually increased from 183 cm to measurably higher levels by week six onward, while his midday measurements shifted from 181 cm upward, with final data suggesting a net height gain. The experiment targets fitness enthusiasts, biohackers, and people curious about maximizing natural physical development through lifestyle interventions.
