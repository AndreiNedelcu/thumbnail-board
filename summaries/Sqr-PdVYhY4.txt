# Puzzle Robot Video Summary

This video documents the three-year development of Jigsaw, a robot engineered to solve jigsaw puzzles faster than humans. The creator breaks down the four complex tasks humans perform when assembling puzzles—picking up pieces, rotating them, positioning them, and deciding placement—and explains how specialized hardware and software replicate each function. For manipulation, Jigsaw uses a precision suction cup, a fine-tuned servo motor accurate to 0.005 degrees, and upgraded CNC motors capable of 1/10th-hair-width accuracy. The critical challenge was solving the visual-spatial reasoning problem: identifying which pieces fit together. The solution involved converting each puzzle piece's edges into mathematical splines and using a backtracking algorithm to find the only configuration where all edges match perfectly. The video features Jigsaw tackling a 1,000-piece all-white puzzle—conditions designed to eliminate human advantages in pattern recognition and color identification—against Tammy McLeod, a Guinness World Record-holding competitive puzzle solver. Along the way, the creator incorporates practical puzzle-solving tips from Tammy and demonstrates Jigsaw's engineering marvel, targeting viewers interested in robotics, engineering, problem-solving innovation, and competitive challenges between human and machine capability.
