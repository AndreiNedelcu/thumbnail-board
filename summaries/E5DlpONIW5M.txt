This video reviews Samsung's Galaxy Watch 4 and Watch 4 Classic, which mark Samsung's transition from Tizen OS to Google's Wear OS 3. The reviewer explains that this collaboration between Samsung and Google produces the fastest Wear OS smartwatch to date with improved app support, featuring a touchscreen bezel on the standard model and a rotating physical bezel on the Classic. Key highlights include comprehensive health monitoring capabilities—ECG, blood oxygen, sleep tracking with snore detection, and bioelectric impedance for body composition analysis—alongside 95 workout types and Google Maps integration. However, the reviewer emphasizes that the watch functions best within Samsung's ecosystem, requiring multiple Samsung apps (Wearable, Galaxy Health, Samsung Pay) to unlock full functionality, even on non-Samsung Android phones. Battery life provides roughly one day with always-on display and GPS usage. The review targets Android users considering smartwatch options, particularly those already invested in Samsung devices, while cautioning non-Samsung users that alternatives remain limited. Privacy concerns regarding Samsung Health's data collection practices are also discussed, with the reviewer noting Samsung's inferior transparency compared to Apple's health privacy approach.
