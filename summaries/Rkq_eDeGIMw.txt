The video demonstrates Adobe Firefly version 3's capabilities for creating 3D-textured designs without traditional 3D software, using only Adobe Illustrator and Firefly combined with Photoshop. The creator sketches a bold letter A, then uses Firefly's AI to generate detailed material textures by inputting prompts and using the sketch as a structural reference, creating photorealistic renders of various surfaces like green slime and inflatable cushions. The workflow involves using Kive AI for image upscaling to enhance quality, then importing results into Photoshop Beta to refine compositions with generative fill and adjustment layers. Beyond this, Firefly 3 enables users to generate complete photorealistic product mockups directly in Photoshop—skin care bottles with cosmetics, business cards on wooden surfaces—allowing designers to create unique, commercially-safe client presentations without expensive stock photography or external mockup tools. The video targets graphic designers and creatives worried about AI replacement, positioning Firefly as a design tool that enhances rather than replaces professional work, streamlining workflows for mockups, texturing, and conceptual visualization while maintaining creative control and commercial viability.
