After one week of daily use, the reviewer shares their experience with the mid-to-high-end M4 Pro MacBook Pro 14-inch model featuring 48GB RAM and 2TB storage in space black. They use the laptop primarily for video editing in Final Cut Pro, photo editing in Photoshop, 3D design in Shaper 3D, and general daily tasks. Key highlights include macOS Sequoia 15.1 with iPhone mirroring capabilities and early Apple Intelligence features, the acclaimed mini-LED display now offering 1,000 nits brightness and optional nanotexture coating, three Thunderbolt 5 ports, and the refined keyboard and trackpad. Performance-wise, the M4 Pro handles intensive creative workloads smoothly, though upgrades from M1 Max show only incremental improvements rather than dramatic leaps. Battery life ranges from 4-5 hours under heavy use to all-day capability for lighter tasks. The reviewer targets potential MacBook buyers evaluating upgrades, emphasizing that while this represents Apple's best laptop offering, those with older Apple silicon models may not find compelling reasons to upgrade immediately, whereas Intel or Windows laptop users would experience substantial performance gains justifying a purchase.
