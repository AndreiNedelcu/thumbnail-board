# Meta Quest 3 Review

This in-depth review evaluates the Meta Quest 3 as a versatile VR and mixed reality headset after a month of extended use. The device excels as an all-around package, featuring sharper displays via upgraded pancake lenses, significantly improved graphics performance from the Snapdragon XR2 Gen 2 chip, and impressive color passthrough cameras enabling convincing mixed reality experiences like virtual boardgames and workspace setups. Key strengths include PC VR capability, hand tracking, haptic-enabled controllers, and a growing game library with titles like Blade and Sorcery Nomad and the newly released Ghostbusters. However, several drawbacks emerge: the 60% price increase from Quest 2 becomes problematic when factoring in necessary accessories, pushing total costs toward PS5 levels; battery life disappoints at 1.5-2.5 hours; the headstrap feels flimsy; and developers haven't yet fully leveraged the enhanced graphics capabilities. The reviewer targets consumers deciding between Quest 3, Quest 2, and emerging alternatives like Apple Vision Pro, emphasizing that while technically impressive, the Quest 3 demands serious financial commitment and dedication to VR gaming to justify its premium positioning.
