This video examines some of LEGO's lowest-rated and most disappointing set releases throughout the company's history. The presenter analyzes multiple failed designs, starting with the 2020 Fire Helicopter Response set, which features an oversized molded piece and a flying mechanism that doesn't work properly, resulting in a price of 32 cents per piece. Other notable failures include the 2004 Knights Kingdom 2 constructible figures with cardboard shields, the spider monkey and Captain America buildable figures with terrible price-to-piece ratios, the Ben 10 sets, and early Technic vehicles. The video also covers the Audi R8 from Speed Champions (plagued by excessive stickers), the traffic light patrol Junior set, and the Legends of Chima Speedor series' worst entry. The presentation reveals that LEGO management deemed the 2005 giraffe set 37 pieces so poor they cancelled its release entirely, and shows that the 2002 McDonald's mini gold figure holds the record as the absolute lowest-rated LEGO set with a 1.4-star rating. The video targets LEGO enthusiasts and collectors interested in understanding which sets to avoid.
