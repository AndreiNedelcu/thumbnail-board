# Samsung Galaxy S25 Ultra Review Summary

Samsung's $1,299 Galaxy S25 Ultra appears largely unchanged from last year's model, featuring only minor design tweaks like boxier edges and a slightly thinner bezel, but houses the significant Snapdragon 8 Elite chip that delivers exceptional performance and efficiency. However, the reviewer criticizes Samsung for playing it safe by skipping improvements found in competing 2025 flagships, such as silicon carbide batteries, IP69 rating, and higher-frequency PWM dimming. The phone includes one useful camera upgrade—a 50-megapixel ultra-wide lens offering improved sharpness—but notably removes Bluetooth functionality from the S Pen stylus, justifying the cut based on low usage statistics while simultaneously undermining the "Ultra" positioning that should offer premium, niche features. One UI 7 brings thoughtful iOS-inspired updates like the bottom notification bar and enhanced lock-screen customization, alongside AI features of mixed quality, including solid object eraser and gallery search but underwhelming daily briefs. The broader context suggests Samsung faces diminished competitive pressure in the US market where consumers rely on carrier availability, allowing the company to deliver an excellent but not best-in-class device at a premium price that misses genuine innovation opportunities.
