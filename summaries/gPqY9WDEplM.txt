# Summary

This video presents a critical investigation into the chiropractic profession, framing it as a normalized scam with deep historical and institutional roots. The analysis traces chiropractic's origins to D.D. Palmer in 1895, who built the profession on the pseudoscientific concept of vertebral subluxations (misaligned vertebrae) as the cause of 95% of diseases—a belief lacking any scientific evidence and impossible to detect through modern imaging. The video documents how Palmer's successor, B.J. Palmer, transformed chiropractic from a spiritual practice into a commercial enterprise, inventing devices and tools with no clinical value. It explores connections to Scientology, describing how L. Ron Hubbard adopted technology (the E-meter) invented by chiropractors, and how Scientology operatives infiltrated government agencies investigating the profession. The investigation details how chiropractors won a landmark 1987 antitrust lawsuit against the American Medical Association despite lacking scientific legitimacy, and shows common scams like prepaid treatment packages for unrelated conditions. The video targets skeptical viewers seeking truth about alternative medicine, featuring interviews with critics, science experts, and patients, while examining how chiropractic achieved insurance coverage and government legitimacy despite being fundamentally unscientific.
