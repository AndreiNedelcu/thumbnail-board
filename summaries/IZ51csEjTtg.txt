# Summary

A logo designer experiments with oversimplifying three well-known brand logos—Nando's, Starbucks, and Gorilla Glue—to explore the tension between minimalism and brand recognition. Using design software, the creator strips each logo down to essential geometric shapes and reduced color palettes, demonstrating techniques like path simplification, geometric eye shapes, and flattened illustrations. The process reveals why many brands resist extreme simplification: while minimalist logos are theoretically more memorable and scalable, they often lose the distinctive character and personality that makes logos iconic. The Nando's rooster becomes nearly unrecognizable, the Starbucks siren loses its elegance, and Gorilla Glue's charm diminishes with each iteration. Through side-by-side comparisons, the video illustrates that simplification works best when it maintains core design elements and brand identity rather than stripping logos to their bare geometric essences. The content targets aspiring graphic designers and logo design enthusiasts interested in understanding design principles, branding strategy, and the practical challenges of logo simplification.
