# Summary

This video explains the triage system for time management, a methodology developed through eight years of medical practice that prioritizes tasks by importance rather than order received. The speaker introduces six core components: understanding that task order matters more than volume completed; the daily reset (a morning practice that identifies weekly goals and today's single most important task); the handwritten box method (a visual status-tracking system where boxes show task progress through diagonal lines, half-shading, and full completion rather than simple checkmarks); real-time triage (constantly re-prioritizing based on actual urgency versus importance, accepting that infinite to-do lists mean some tasks will never be completed); and the ward round protocol (systematically reviewing all active projects to determine next action steps). The system acknowledges that modern life presents an infinitely growing to-do list, making it impossible to complete everything; therefore, the goal shifts from clearing all tasks to consistently identifying and executing the highest-priority items. This approach is designed for busy professionals, entrepreneurs, and creatives who struggle with time management and want to make meaningful progress on what truly matters rather than simply achieving activity.
