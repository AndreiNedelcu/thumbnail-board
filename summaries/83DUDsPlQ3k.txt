iPadOS 16 introduces significant multitasking improvements centered on Stage Manager, Apple's implementation of floating, resizable windows that fundamentally transforms iPad productivity. Key features include the ability to open and arrange multiple apps simultaneously in customizable floating windows, a new "More Space" UI scaling option for better display utilization, and full external display support that allows users to run different apps on the iPad and an external monitor simultaneously, mimicking a desktop computer setup. The update also brings most iOS 16 features to iPad, though notably excludes the lock screen customization. However, a major limitation restricts the most powerful new features—Stage Manager, external display support, and desktop-class improvements—exclusively to M-series iPad models (M1 iPad Air, M1 iPad Pro, and future M-series devices). Older iPad models, including the 2020 iPad Pro, iPad mini, and standard iPad, receive only minor enhancements, effectively receiving a partial software update. The presenter targets current and prospective iPad owners while expressing disappointment about Apple's restrictive feature distribution strategy, though ultimately recommending the upgrade for its significant workflow improvements on compatible devices.
