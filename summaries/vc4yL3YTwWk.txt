This video presents a comprehensive investigation into Honey, a PayPal-owned browser extension marketed as a money-saving tool through influencer partnerships. The creator alleges three primary forms of fraud: Honey systematically hijacks affiliate commissions by replacing tracking cookies with their own during checkout, ensuring they receive credit for sales they didn't generate; Honey Gold offers minimal cashback (89 cents on a $35 commission in one test) while stealing the full affiliate payment; and Honey intentionally withholds better coupon codes available online, instead prioritizing discounts approved by partner retailers paying Honey. The investigation reveals that partner stores control which coupon codes appear on the platform, contradicting Honey's promise to find all available deals. The creator documents how prominent influencers like Linus Tech Tips, who promoted Honey extensively (160 sponsored segments reaching 194 million views), eventually discovered the affiliate manipulation but failed to publicly warn their audiences. This exposé targets tech-savvy viewers interested in consumer protection, affiliate marketing ethics, and corporate deception, arguing the scam costs content creators millions while misleading consumers about deal accessibility.
