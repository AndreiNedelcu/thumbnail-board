# Summary

This interview explores mastering software engineering craft through the perspective of an experienced CTO with 25+ years in the industry. The discussion covers how to develop genuine mastery while navigating constant technological change, emphasizing that learning should be driven by real project requirements rather than personal interest alone. Key topics include deep language fundamentals, reading quality open-source code to understand design patterns, starting with core development before expanding into infrastructure and operations, and maintaining pragmatism about context-specific solutions. The conversation addresses the chicken-and-egg problem of technology selection, the importance of delivering working prototypes quickly to validate approaches, and strategies for persuading skeptical stakeholders through evidence-based reasoning rather than authority. The speaker advocates for depth in specialized areas while recognizing the modern tech landscape's breadth, cautioning against spreading junior developers too thin across full-stack responsibilities. The interview targets software engineers at various career stages seeking to deepen their craft, particularly those struggling with continuous learning in rapidly evolving environments.
