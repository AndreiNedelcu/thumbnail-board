This video examines ASUS's ROG Ally X20, a refined anniversary edition gaming handheld that addresses previous criticisms through substantial upgrades. The centerpiece is a new 7.4-inch OLED display with exceptional specs including 1,400 nits peak brightness in HDR, Dolby Vision support, and variable refresh rate up to 120Hz—a major leap from the original LCD screen. Beyond the display, ASUS redesigned the entire PCB to accommodate improved cooling, added rubberized grips, implemented TMR joysticks with reduced dead zones, enhanced the D-pad with switchable tactile dome switches for fighting game flexibility, and replaced the micro SD slot with faster micro SD Express. The device maintains the same core processor and RAM as its predecessor while gaining a translucent smoked black aesthetic with gold accents. The teardown reveals careful engineering decisions, such as repositioning the USB 4 port to allow better airflow over the OLED panel. Additional accessories include a new dock with improved TV communication and premium XR glasses offering a 171-inch microLED display. The video targets gaming enthusiasts and handheld computing enthusiasts interested in technical specifications and design innovations.
