# Summary

This video features Sisun Lee, a Silicon Valley entrepreneur with a decade of experience, sharing lessons from his career spanning major tech companies and startups. Lee worked as a product manager at Facebook (where he developed insights in emerging markets like India and Kenya through on-the-ground research), Uber, and Tesla before founding MoreLabs in 2017, a consumer beverage company focused on hangover recovery products leveraging arbitrage from Asian markets. After raising a Series B round, he stepped down as CEO to hand leadership to a retail-focused successor, recognizing the company required sales and marketing expertise rather than his technology background. He currently works on Ramper, a blockchain infrastructure business based in San Francisco and Seoul. Throughout his narrative, Lee emphasizes that entrepreneurial success stems not from innate intelligence but from resilience, grit, and iterative learning through failure. He discusses navigating COVID-19's 90% revenue impact without laying off staff, the importance of ruthless prioritization, and recognizing when to step aside for the right leadership. The content targets aspiring entrepreneurs, product managers, and business professionals interested in Silicon Valley dynamics, personal career transitions, and practical wisdom from scaling companies.
