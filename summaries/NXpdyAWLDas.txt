OpenAI has released Sora, a new AI video generation model that can create full-minute video clips from text prompts alone, marking a dramatic leap forward in generative AI capability. The technology understands not just visual composition but also physics, materials, lighting, and temporal consistency—generating photorealistic footage of everything from Tokyo streets and speeding vehicles to puppies in snow and stylized scenes. While flaws remain visible upon close inspection (uncanny hand movements, inconsistent reflections, glitchy physics), the videos are convincing enough to fool casual viewers and already suitable for stock footage applications, advertisements, and presentations. The tool, currently in limited access with red teamers and trusted creators, presents significant implications: it threatens to displace video licensing, stock footage markets, and cinematography work, while raising serious concerns about election-year deepfakes and misinformation. Though the technology includes safety measures and watermarks, the rapid improvement trajectory—comparing current capabilities to crude AI-generated videos from just one year ago—suggests these limitations will diminish quickly, making Sora simultaneously impressive and alarming for content creators and society broadly.
