# Summary

Nvidia's rise to become the world's most valuable company in June 2024 at $3.34 trillion stems from its pioneering role in graphics processing and artificial intelligence technology. Founded in 1993 by Jensen Huang and others, the company introduced the GeForce 256, the first marketed GPU, which revolutionized gaming by enabling parallel processing. The real accelerant to its dominance came with CUDA in 2006—a software platform that allowed developers to harness GPU power for non-graphics tasks, making Nvidia the default infrastructure for AI development and training. As AI exploded over recent years with ChatGPT, Gemini, and similar tools, virtually all of this computational demand ran on Nvidia chips, embedding the company into both standalone AI applications and cloud services like AWS. The final catalyst was a 10:1 stock split in June, making shares ten times more affordable for retail investors seeking AI exposure. While recent market corrections have tempered valuations, Nvidia's entrenchment in digital graphics, digital twins, and the metaverse means its GPU technology will likely remain foundational to computing infrastructure regardless of AI's long-term trajectory. The video targets general audiences seeking to understand modern tech economics and why Nvidia achieved unprecedented market dominance.
