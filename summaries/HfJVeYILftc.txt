# Summary

This video explores why many successful designers and creative professionals exhibit elitist and arrogant behavior despite claiming to want to improve people's lives. The creator examines how the creative process itself—which demands constant self-criticism, pursuit of perfection, and unwavering confidence in unconceived ideas—naturally fosters egotistical traits. Key figures like Frank Lloyd Wright, Stanley Kubrick, and others demonstrate how perfectionism can become pathological, though such obsessiveness rarely justifies the suffering it causes. The video discusses how designers must convince clients to fund non-existent products, requiring an almost delusional level of confidence that can blur into dishonesty. It analyzes how design operates as an exclusionary club with narrow aesthetic standards rooted in European modernism and mid-century minimalism, rejecting non-conformist work like Luigi Colani's biomorphic designs. The video also critiques design awards as expensive, questionable credibility markers and notes how media celebrations of individual genius reinforce narcissism. However, the creator argues that internet democratization is slowly dismantling design elitism by making education accessible, enabling diverse voices, and fostering open-source attitudes toward improvement. The piece targets aspiring and practicing designers interested in industry culture and ethics.
