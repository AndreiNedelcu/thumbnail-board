# Summary

This video explores the emerging market of unconventional open-back headphones and earbuds, which allow sound to pass through and let wearers hear their environment simultaneously. The reviewer tests five different models: Sony LinkBuds Open (ring-style earbuds with a hole in the center), Bose Ultra Open (flexible ear-hook design), Nothing Ear Open (behind-ear curl style), NWM open-back headphones (lightweight floating drivers), and the unreleased Movetone M600 (ring-design headphones). Each design prioritizes transparency and environmental awareness over bass response and sound isolation found in traditional closed-back audio. The video targets tech enthusiasts and audiophiles interested in audio gear innovation, particularly those seeking comfort during extended wear or need to remain aware of surroundings while listening. The reviewer evaluates each model on sound quality and transparency, noting that modern alternatives like AirPods Pro transparency mode offer comparable functionality with added versatility through noise cancellation switching. Ultimately, the analysis positions true open-back designs as superior for authentic environmental hearing compared to digital alternatives, functioning as "AR glasses" rather than virtual reality headsets.
