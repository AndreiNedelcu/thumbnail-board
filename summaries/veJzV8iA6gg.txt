# Summary

This video demonstrates seven free at-home tests to assess biological age—how functionally old your body is versus chronological age. The creator, who has built an anti-aging protocol through extensive personal data collection, partners with Lauren, a PhD researcher, to perform push-ups, sit-rise tests, sit-and-reach flexibility measurements, single-leg balance with eyes closed, reaction time tests using a smartphone app, waist-to-height ratio calculations, and grip strength testing (with a $20 dynamometer as the optional seventh test). Each test is benchmarked against age-specific averages, allowing individuals to compare their biological age to their actual age. The video emphasizes that biological age reflects physical function rather than time lived, and that baseline measurements enable people to track improvement over time. While acknowledging these tests don't replace comprehensive health assessments like blood work or imaging, the creator positions them as accessible entry points for understanding personal fitness levels and motivating incremental self-improvement. The target audience is health-conscious individuals interested in quantifying their physical condition and anti-aging strategies without expensive medical testing.
