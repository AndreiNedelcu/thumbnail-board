# Video Summary

This crime documentary chronicles the investigation into the 2010 murder of 54-year-old Diane Zeleleski in Union Township, New Jersey. Found brutally stabbed over 40 times in her basement after failing to pick up her sister-in-law for a doctor's appointment, Diane becomes the focus of Detective Robert Henderson's investigation. The crime scene reveals unusual characteristics: excessive blood, fire extinguisher powder used to contaminate evidence, items dunked in soapy water to destroy fingerprints, and her body splashed with toilet cleaner. Despite the killer's deliberate cleanup efforts, forensic evidence emerges—unmatched male blood droplets suggesting Diane wounded her attacker during the struggle. With no forced entry and evidence suggesting the killer knew Diane, Henderson theorizes she was murdered by someone from her active online dating life. Interviews with her best friend Joanne reveal a concerning ex-boyfriend, Mike Prunell, a former landlord whom Diane had to escape after ending their relationship. The narrative builds tension around identifying suspects and extracting DNA evidence to match the unknown male blood at the scene, targeting an audience interested in true crime investigations and forensic detective work.
