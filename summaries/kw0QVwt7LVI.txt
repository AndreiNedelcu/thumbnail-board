This documentary examines the mechanics of modern money creation and its systemic consequences. The video explains how approximately 97% of the UK money supply consists of electronic bank deposits created by commercial banks when issuing loans, with only 3% comprising physical currency created by the central bank. Key concepts include how banks generate money "out of nothing" through accounting entries, the historical shift from government-regulated banking in 1844 to contemporary digital money systems, and how this debt-based money creation ensures continuous economic growth requirements and cyclical boom-bust patterns. The documentary argues that because money only enters the economy through bank loans, society becomes inherently dependent on increasing debt, making the current system structurally unstable. It criticizes politicians and economists for misunderstanding this fundamental mechanism and blaming individuals for over-borrowing when the system itself necessitates debt. The video targets general audiences seeking to understand economic policy and monetary systems, particularly those concerned about financial crises, inequality, and government economic strategies. It presents the argument that reforming money creation mechanisms is essential to addressing economic instability.
