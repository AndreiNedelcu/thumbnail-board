# Summary

This video documents a 100-day experiment where the creator investigates his persistent focus and attention problems by consulting ADHD specialists, reviewing neuroscience research, tracking his daily work habits, and taking a clinical ADHD assessment. Though he doesn't meet clinical criteria for ADHD, he discovers through time-journaling that he only focuses about 50% of his workday, prompting deeper investigation. Counterintuitively, a meta-analysis of 31 years of research reveals human attention spans have actually improved since 2000, suggesting the problem isn't diminished capacity but rather modern environmental distractions. By analyzing his patterns, the creator identifies three focus killers: mental exhaustion without relief, lack of daily planning, and boredom. He implements structured work routines alternating demanding and easy tasks, nightly checklists, strategic breaks, and mindless work sessions—mirroring successful strategies used by friends diagnosed with ADHD. This approach significantly improves his focus metrics. The video targets people struggling with productivity and attention who suspect they might have ADHD or simply want to understand their focus challenges better.
