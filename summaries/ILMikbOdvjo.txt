# Summary

This video chronicles how Google's algorithm updates devastated multiple websites belonging to Morgan and Sean from Charleston Crafted, a DIY home improvement and woodworking blog that had grown into a successful $325,000-per-year business. After learning SEO and discovering how to create search-optimized content, Morgan built three additional websites focused on kids' crafts, plant care, and paint colors. However, Google's September 2023 Helpful Content Update and subsequent March 2024 Core Update crushed traffic on her secondary sites by 80% and reduced Charleston Crafted's traffic by 75% overnight—despite Google previously featuring them as a creator success story. Morgan was invited to Google's headquarters but left with no solutions, only confirmation that the traffic wouldn't return. The video explores how her business had become over-dependent on Google search traffic, affecting ad revenue, affiliate income, and sponsorships. Working with an SEO expert, Morgan develops a new strategy: consolidating her paint color content onto an e-commerce subdomain to promote her 287 digital paint palettes she sells for $1,500 monthly, pivoting from Google-dependent ad revenue to direct product sales. The story illustrates both the power and peril of relying on search traffic for business sustainability.
