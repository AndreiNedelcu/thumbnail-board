This video demonstrates how to build a PET bottle filament extruder using salvaged parts from an old Ender 3 3D printer, enabling home recycling of plastic waste into usable 3D printing material. The creator documents the complete process of assembling the machine (based on Joshua Taylor's Mark 5 kit design), including modifications to the hot end, construction of a bottle cutter, and setup of the winding mechanism. Key technical details include adjusting the nozzle diameter to 1.7mm, maintaining temperatures around 210°C (below PET's melting point), and achieving approximately 39% yield from source bottles. The video targets makers, sustainability enthusiasts, and 3D printing hobbyists interested in circular economy solutions, providing practical guidance on preparing bottles, operating the extruder, and printing with recycled PET filament. Testing reveals that annealed PET filament achieves superior tensile strength (72 MPa) compared to conventional 3D printing materials, making it suitable for both cosmetic and functional parts. The content emphasizes that while economically challenging in developed countries with deposit systems, PET extrusion holds significant potential for regions lacking affordable access to printing materials and abundant plastic waste resources.
