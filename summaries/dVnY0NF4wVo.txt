# Anonymous: From Internet Trolling to Global Activism

This documentary-style interview traces Anonymous's evolution from its origins around 2006 as a loosely organized group of internet trolls on 4chan and IRC chat systems to a coordinated global activism movement. The narrator, Greg House, a key early activist and spokesperson, explains how Anonymous began with playful pranks like the "Habbo Hotel" raids before taking on targets they felt deserved opposition, such as neo-Nazi radio host Hal Turner. The turning point came with Project Chanology in 2008, when the leaked Tom Cruise Scientology video catalyzed the creation of an iconic protest message delivered by the "Guy Fawkes" masked persona—now synonymous with Anonymous. Through rapid video distribution and automated account creation, the group expanded from dozens to thousands of coordinated activists across 143 cities, eventually organizing the February 10, 2008 worldwide protests outside Scientology centers. The account examines how Anonymous strategically leveraged their growing numbers and media attention to break through decades of legal intimidation that had silenced critical journalism about Scientology. Targeting internet history enthusiasts and activists interested in digital movements, the content reveals the tension between chaotic grassroots participation and informal operational leadership structures.
