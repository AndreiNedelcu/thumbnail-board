# Summary

Argentina's president Javier Milei became embroiled in a memecoin scandal after tweeting about Libra, a token allegedly designed to support the Argentine economy and small businesses. The project rapidly collapsed after accusations of insider trading and rug-pulling emerged, with the development team potentially extracting over $100 million from the liquidity pool. The investigation reveals a complex scheme involving Mauricio Nolfi and Manuel Gdoí from Tech Forum Argentina, along with Hayden Davis from Kelsier and Julian Pay from Kip Protocol, who allegedly orchestrated the launch while profiting through token sniping—buying tokens before public launch at cheaper prices. Evidence shows Davis and his team sniped not only Libra but also Melania Trump's memecoin and others on the Medora platform, with insider communications revealing attempts to "max extract" profits. Milei denies involvement and claims he only broadcast rather than promoted the token. The scandal exposes how meme coin launches function as rigged insider games, with retail investors systematically disadvantaged while platforms and insiders profit through coordinated trading tactics. The president now faces fraud charges and potential impeachment, while various participants may face legal consequences.
