# Summary

This video documents how speedrunners discovered numerous exploits and skips to dramatically reduce completion times for "Get to Work," a precision-based rage game about climbing a corporate ladder on rollerblades. The developer released the game on December 2nd and held a speedrun competition with daily leaderboards, expecting players to take 8-15 hours to complete it. However, speedrunners systematically broke the game through creative routing strategies: discovering skip sequences in sections like the unpaid internship, warehouse manager route, bumper section, and middle management pyramid; exploiting physics mechanics like wall bouncing and the "hang" mechanic to bypass intended paths; and identifying optimal jump angles to shave minutes off completion times. Starting from an 3-hour baseline run by Dan Corona on day one, subsequent runners like Frost Killer achieved a 23:48 completion (sub-25 minutes), and Kem discovered additional shortcuts bringing times below the developer's "secret" sub-30 achievement threshold. The video appeals to gaming enthusiasts interested in speedrunning, level design, and how player creativity can subvert developer intentions in physics-based platformers.
