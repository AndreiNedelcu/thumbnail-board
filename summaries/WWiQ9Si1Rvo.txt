This video explores the current state of six classic online multiplayer games from childhood—Pop Tropica, Club Penguin, Toontown, Webkinz, Wizard101, and Pirates of the Caribbean Online—to determine whether they're truly abandoned or still maintain active player bases. The creator logs into each game and attempts to make a new friend as a metric for measuring vitality; success indicates the game retains life, while failure suggests it's dead. The games discussed represent a mix of shut-down titles with fan-made recreations (Club Penguin, Toontown Rewritten, Pirates of the Caribbean Online) and still-operational games (Wizard101, Webkinz). Key challenges include navigating changed interfaces, limited chat systems, and sparse player populations spread across multiple servers. The video targets millennials and Gen Z nostalgic players who grew up with these early 2000s browser-based MMOs, offering both entertainment and insight into how beloved childhood games have aged. Through humorous anecdotes and genuine attempts at connection, the creator documents which games surprisingly maintain communities and which have become virtual ghost towns.
