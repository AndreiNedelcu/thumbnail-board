This tutorial demonstrates how to build a comprehensive workout tracking system in Notion from scratch. The creator walks through setting up a calendar database to log workout days, then adds templates for specific workout routines like ab workouts that include checklist-style to-do items for individual exercises. To track workout frequency, a duplicate table view with filtering and counting functions shows how many workouts were completed within a specific timeframe. The tutorial then expands the system by creating a separate exercises database with properties like muscle group focus (using multi-select tags), allowing filtered views that display only relevant exercises for each workout type. Finally, it shows how to convert the exercises database to a gallery view, where users can add photos and video links demonstrating proper exercise form, creating an all-in-one resource that combines scheduling, tracking, instruction, and accountability. This approach targets fitness enthusiasts and Notion users seeking to organize their training routines and monitor progress without using external fitness apps.
