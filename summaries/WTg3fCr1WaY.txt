This video presents a multi-year analysis of note-taking app popularity based on survey data from students in the "Building a Second Brain" community, tracking usage patterns from August 2020 through early 2024. The presenter examines four major surveys spanning different cohort sizes, revealing that Evernote, Notion, and Rome Research consistently dominate, collectively accounting for approximately 60-75 percent of user adoption across most studies. The analysis introduces a framework categorizing note-taking users into archetypes: "librarians" who favor Evernote's organizational structure, "architects" who prefer Notion's dashboard and team capabilities, and "gardeners" interested in graph-based networking tools like Rome and the rising Obsidian. The data shows significant shifts in user preferences, with Notion recently surpassing Evernote in one survey, Obsidian experiencing notable growth, and emerging competitors like Craft and LogSeq gaining traction. The video targets productivity-focused individuals and students interested in digital note-taking systems, helping them understand which applications align with their organizational style and learning preferences within an increasingly crowded marketplace.
