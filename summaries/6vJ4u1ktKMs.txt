# Video Summary

This video traces Robert Pattinson's career trajectory following the conclusion of the Twilight franchise, focusing on how he deliberately distanced himself from his vampire heartthrob image by pursuing challenging independent films. After Twilight ended when he was twenty-two, Pattinson rejected the typical path of capitalizing on mainstream fame, instead seeking out unconventional projects with limited budgets and artistic risk. He collaborated with directors like David Cronenberg on Cosmopolis and the Safdie Brothers on Good Time, films that allowed him creative freedom and the anonymity needed to develop as an actor outside public expectations. The video documents the initial skepticism and backlash when he was cast as Batman, with many fans associating him exclusively with Twilight. However, critical acclaim for his indie work and his performance in The Batman ultimately vindicated his strategy of prioritizing artistic fulfillment over commercial appeal. The narrative emphasizes Pattinson's philosophy that actors should pursue projects that scare them rather than cater to audience expectations, and how his willingness to take unconventional paths eventually redefined his public perception and established him as a serious actor rather than a franchise star.
