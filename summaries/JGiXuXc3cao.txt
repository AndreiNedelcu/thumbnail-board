# Summary

This video traces the decades-long effort to locate the wreck of the RMS Titanic on the ocean floor, nearly 2.5 miles below the surface. It explains how the ship's distress coordinates were initially inaccurate due to a miscalculation of time zones, expanding the search area significantly. The narrative follows multiple expeditions: Wilston Beasley's 1953 attempt using early sonar, Robert Ballard's failed 1977 effort where expensive equipment was lost, and eccentric oil tycoon Jack Grim's well-funded but unconventional 1981-1983 searches. Grim's scientists disputed his findings, including a controversial propeller-shaped object never confirmed. The breakthrough came in 1985 when Ballard, leveraging a partnership with the U.S. Navy and French oceanographic institute IFREMER, applied innovative thinking: instead of searching for the ship itself, he hunted for its debris field, which ocean currents had dispersed in a comet-like pattern. Using the remotely operated vehicle Argo with real-time video cameras, Ballard's team discovered the Titanic's iconic boiler within days, leading to the discovery of both the bow and stern sections. The video targets history enthusiasts and documentary viewers interested in exploration, technology, and maritime mysteries.
