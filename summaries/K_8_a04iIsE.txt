# Summary

This video follows an experiment testing whether the fashion world's credibility can be challenged by taking a completely ordinary person to the top of Fashion Week. The creator selects Raymond, a 76-year-old grandfather with no fashion background or interest, and dresses him entirely in ridiculous items from Wish.com, including waterproof shoe covers, plastic bags, googly eyes, and random novelty items. They then attempt to get him treated as a VIP throughout London Fashion Week's major events. Through confidence, a fabricated backstory (portraying him as related to the Armani founder), and strategic networking, Raymond successfully gets photographed by paparazzi, secures front-row seats at exclusive fashion shows, appears on Vogue's "Top Looks of the Week," and gains entry to the exclusive afterparty alongside celebrities like Serena Williams and Justin Bieber. The video satirizes the fashion industry by demonstrating how perception, presentation, and networking can elevate anyone regardless of actual fashion credentials or appropriate attire. It targets audiences skeptical of high fashion culture and those who enjoy prank-style social experiments.
