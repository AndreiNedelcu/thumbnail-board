# Summary

The video documents the creation of a world's largest functioning iPhone, a 15x-scaled version built from scratch over a year. The device incorporates a Canon EOS R5 full-frame camera with an 8K sensor 1,000% larger than Pro iPhone sensors, a Sony RX10 Mark IV with 25x optical zoom using a periscope mirror system, and a custom 88-inch 8K OLED touchscreen (costing $25,000). Internally, it runs Android on high-end PC hardware including 128GB RAM, an AMD 6950 XT graphics card, and a 24-core Intel Core i9 processor, dual-booting Windows alongside Android with 4 terabytes of storage. The phone features eight pneumatically-dampened buttons, 400-watt LED flashlight with cooling system, 50-watt speakers with subwoofers, replaceable 3,840 watt-hour batteries, and a custom magnetized charging port. The creators test its capabilities—from gaming benchmarks and video calls across distances to purchasing ice cream via NFC and streaming video—before a Guinness World Record adjudicator verifies its dimensions and functionality against standard iPhone specifications, ultimately certifying it as the world's largest functioning smartphone.
