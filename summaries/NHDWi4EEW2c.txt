# Summary

RentAHitman.com is a satirical joke website created in 2005 by California resident Bob Ennis as a tongue-in-cheek advertisement for penetration testing services, playing on the concept of being a "hitman" for eliminating network vulnerabilities. After abandoning the site for years, Ennis discovered hundreds of genuine murder-for-hire solicitations in his email inbox from people who believed the obviously fake website was a real contract killing service on the open internet. Rather than ignore these threats, Ennis began documenting the requests and reporting them to law enforcement, adopting a fictional Hitman persona named "Guido Finelli" to extract detailed confessions from would-be killers. The website's satirical elements—including fake credentials like "HIPAA" (Hitman Information Privacy and Protection Act)—were so obvious that only the most desperate or unintelligent criminals fell for it. Over nearly two decades, dozens of individuals have been arrested and convicted through the site, including people seeking to murder ex-partners, family members, and high school classmates, as well as someone attempting to apply for a job as a contract killer. Law enforcement has credited Ennis's work with potentially saving over a hundred lives, making his joke website an unexpected crime-fighting tool that demonstrates how criminals often overlook obvious red flags when pursuing illegal services online.
