CES 2025 marked a major turning point for VR hardware, particularly with the mainstream arrival of 4K-per-eye micro-OLED displays from BOE appearing across multiple headsets. The video covers major manufacturers including Shiftall's Mega X Super Light 8K (a $1,900 Steam VR headset with impressive optics but no eye tracking), Play for Dream's Mr (an Android-based Vision Pro alternative at $1,500 less), Pimax Crystal Super (highest resolution but with distortion issues), and emerging glove technology from Viper X and EOZ offering viable controller alternatives. Additional coverage includes haptic vest iterations from bHaptics, Sony's enterprise-focused $5,000 Zen headset, optical lens solutions from Optics with integrated eye tracking, and the standout innovation: FreeAim omnidirectional motorized shoes providing natural VR locomotion. The presenter targets PC VR enthusiasts and hardware hobbyists, emphasizing technical specifications, ergonomic design, and comparative analysis of competing technologies. Key themes include the consolidation around BOE displays, the viability of glove-based input, and locomotion innovations, with the presenter providing hands-on impressions from both booth demonstrations and extended testing sessions.
