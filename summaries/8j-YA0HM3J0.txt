This video explains false color, a professional exposure metering tool used by videographers to achieve proper exposure quickly and consistently. False color maps different exposure levels (measured in IRE units from 0-100) to distinct colors, making it easy to identify underexposed and overexposed areas at a glance. The presenter demonstrates how to use false color with external monitors like Atomos and Small HD to expose for skin tones, typically aiming for 60-75 IRE for standard picture profiles. For shooters using log profiles, he explains that false color readings change and recommends applying LUTs to monitors before setting exposure. He introduces lzone, a Small HD-specific tool that uses middle gray as a reference point and displays skin tones in stops of light, enabling faster exposure adjustments through simple aperture or lighting tweaks. The video targets videographers and filmmakers using mirrorless cameras who want practical methods to nail exposure in the field without spending excessive time on histogram analysis, whether they invest in external monitors or use smartphone apps as alternatives.
