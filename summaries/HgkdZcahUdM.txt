Amy founded a cloth nappy business that generates $100,000 daily by addressing a real problem she encountered when her newborn leaked through standard disposable diapers. Starting from her hospital bed, she initially hand-sewed nappies at home, then scaled through Alibaba manufacturers while building community on Facebook Marketplace and TikTok. Her competitive advantage is a patented design fitting newborns through toilet training, compared to competitors' limited 5-15 kilogram range. The business grew organically without paid advertising, accumulating 15 million TikTok views and launching on Shark Tank, reaching $125,000 in year one and $100,000 in a single day. The video targets aspiring entrepreneurs and business builders, emphasizing key lessons: solving genuine customer problems, building transparent community relationships, leveraging organic social media content, and persistence through manufacturing setbacks. Amy's personal backstory—overcoming childhood poverty and a suicide attempt at seventeen—contextualizes her entrepreneurial drive and resilience in scaling a bootstrapped operation from zero employees to a multi-million-dollar venture centered on sustainable baby products.
