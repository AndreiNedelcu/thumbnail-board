# Video Summary

This video documents an ambitious DIY project where the creator attempts to build a large table using approximately 1,000 feet of denim laminated with 20 gallons of epoxy. Drawing inspiration from a craftsperson who makes denim sunglasses, the project involves sourcing bulk denim, saturating 50 sheets layer-by-layer with liquid glass epoxy using a labor-intensive squeegee method, and constructing custom white oak legs wrapped in stitched leather by a professional upholsterer. The process proves messier and more challenging than anticipated, with significant epoxy spillage requiring cleanup, drill bit complications during brass mounting plate fabrication, and adhesion issues with the melamine mold. The creator documents troubleshooting various setbacks including incomplete epoxy saturation requiring a restart, meticulous routing and finishing work, and custom flush-mounted brass electrical outlets. The finished denim-epoxy hybrid material behaves somewhat like wood, allowing traditional woodworking techniques while offering advantages like dimensional stability. The video targets DIY enthusiasts, makers, and woodworkers interested in experimental furniture design and epoxy projects, emphasizing the value of learning from mistakes and innovative problem-solving.
