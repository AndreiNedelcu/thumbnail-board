The creator builds a transparent polycarbonate katana inspired by seeing an "invisible sword" concept online. After determining that glass is too brittle, he selects polycarbonate—a material ten times more impact-resistant than steel by weight and used in riot shields and armored vehicles—as the ideal material. The project involves cutting the blade shape from a large polycarbonate panel using a band saw, grinding a sharp bevel with a custom angle-setting tool, then spending hours sanding and polishing with increasingly fine grits to achieve transparency while minimizing distortion caused by the blade's variable thickness. He creates a minimalist transparent guard and handle by gluing additional polycarbonate pieces and shaping them on a grinder. After discovering the rigid handle made the blade snap, he redesigns it for flexibility. The transparent katana is then tested against progressively harder targets including pineapple, watermelon, pumpkin, wood, ballistic gelatin, and brick, demonstrating impressive cutting ability and durability. The video appeals to engineering enthusiasts and makers interested in materials science, tool work, and practical testing of unconventional designs.
