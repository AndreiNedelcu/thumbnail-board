# Summary

This video investigates the explosive growth of gas station pizza, particularly Hunt Brothers Pizza, which operates over 9,000 locations—more than Dominos—and generates $24-30 million annually. The creator travels to Milwaukee, Wisconsin, the gas station pizza capital, to understand the business model's profitability and scalability. Hunt Brothers, founded by three brothers in 1991, pioneered a franchise-like approach by installing pizza-making equipment and selling ingredients to convenience stores and gas stations without contracts or licensing fees. Initial setup costs range from $13,000 to $25,000, with operators purchasing ingredients directly from distributors. Store managers report selling 400-600 pizzas monthly at $1.99-$12 each, generating annual pizza revenues of $30,000-$45,000 with 50% gross margins. The video also examines competitors like Casey's, which operates 2,600 locations across 17 states with $14 billion in annual revenue. Key success factors include high-traffic locations, limited local competition, reliable ingredient distribution, and proper equipment. Geographic limitations exist—Hunt Brothers operates in only 36 states—but expansion into Texas, Tennessee, and Kentucky suggests rapid growth. The analysis targets entrepreneurs interested in franchise opportunities or prepared food businesses within the convenience store sector.
