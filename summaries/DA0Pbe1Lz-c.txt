This video explores nine common cognitive distortions—unhelpful thought patterns that misrepresent reality and contribute to anxiety and depression. The content explains how the mind interprets circumstances rather than circumstances themselves driving emotional responses. The distortions covered include all-or-nothing thinking, which views situations as entirely good or bad; labeling, which defines people based on single events; mind reading, where assumptions about others' thoughts are made without evidence; fortune telling, predicting negative outcomes; and catastrophizing, expecting worst-case scenarios. Additional distortions discussed are mental filtering, which focuses only on negative details; discounting the positive, dismissing positive outcomes; emotional reasoning, treating feelings as facts; and should statements, creating unrealistic expectations. For each distortion, the video provides practical strategies to counter them, such as recognizing shades of gray, questioning assumptions, de-catastrophizing worries, and seeking evidence for thoughts. The target audience includes people experiencing anxiety or depression seeking to understand their thought patterns and mental health professionals or educators teaching cognitive behavioral techniques. The video emphasizes that awareness and practice in recognizing these distortions is key to changing unhelpful thinking patterns.
