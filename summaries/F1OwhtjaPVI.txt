# Action Camera Comparison Summary

This video presents an eight-week field test comparing the GoPro HERO13, DJI Osmo Action 5, and Insta360 Ace Pro2 across real-world adventure activities rather than controlled lab settings. The reviewer, who professionally relies on action cameras, evaluates four primary criteria: image quality, field of view, slow motion capability, and stabilization, plus secondary features like battery life, low-light performance, photo capabilities, form factor, and screen quality. In image quality, GoPro leads with superior dynamic range and highest bitrate (up to 200 Mbps), DJI places second, and Insta360 third due to 8-bit limitations. For field of view, GoPro's 8x7 ratio captures more vertical space than competitors' 4x3 ratios, while both DJI and Insta360 crop significantly in slow-motion modes. GoPro dominates slow-motion performance, offering up to 400 fps at 2.7K, versus competitors' 240 fps at 1080p. In stabilization, GoPro clearly excels, especially with wide-angle adapters and underwater modes. DJI offers superior battery life and low-light management, while GoPro leads in photo capabilities. The reviewer provides raw footage for viewer verification, targeting serious content creators and adventurers seeking objective performance data.
