# River Table with Living Fish - Improved Design Build

The creator rebuilds their viral aquarium table project using viewer suggestions to improve the original design. The updated table features a significantly deeper water channel, enhanced waterproofing through strategic epoxy application, and transparent acrylic top and bottom panels allowing full visibility of the interior ecosystem. The construction process combines woodworking, epoxy resin work, and aquarium design, using layers of clear acrylic base, decorative wood cookie slabs, blue-tinted resin poured with white wave effects reminiscent of ocean aesthetics, and decorative pebbles to create a miniature beach environment. The table incorporates a hollow wooden base housing filtration and circulation systems. Throughout the multi-phase build, the creator experiments with untested techniques—particularly bonding acrylic to epoxy—while troubleshooting waterproofing challenges at joints and corners. The target audience includes DIY enthusiasts, epoxy resin artists, woodworkers, and viewers interested in aquascaping and functional furniture design. The video documents the experimental nature of the project, emphasizing problem-solving and creative iteration rather than following established tutorials.
