This video demonstrates how to implement the PARA organizational system—Projects, Areas, Resources, and Archives—within Apple Notes to streamline information management. The presenter replicates a previous setup shown in Evernote, illustrating how to structure the app's sidebar with these four collapsible folders for easy navigation on mobile and desktop devices. The system captures and organizes three types of content: email attachments (like coaching notes), web articles with source links, and social media posts. The key workflow involves a weekly review process where captured notes are sorted into the appropriate PARA category based on actionability and relevance. The video emphasizes practical decision-making through a priority hierarchy: content goes into Projects first if applicable, then Areas for ongoing responsibilities, Resources for reference material, or Archives for completed items. The presenter illustrates real examples including coaching notes for a book launch project, an article about cognitive decline that prompted creating a new "Parents" area, and fusion energy content filed under climate change resources. The approach targets digital organizers, knowledge workers, and anyone seeking a simple, structured method to manage growing information without overthinking placement decisions.
