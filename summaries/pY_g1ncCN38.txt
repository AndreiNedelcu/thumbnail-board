# Summary

This video documents an individual's second attempt at the SOCOM Diver Screener, a rigorous military qualification test used by the United States Special Operations Command. The screener involves underwater exercises with bound hands and feet, including two minutes of bobbing, two minutes of floating, a 50-meter travel sequence, and a final phase requiring front flips, backflips, and mask retrieval. After failing the first attempt two years prior due to panic and loss of breath control, the participant trains extensively with Prime, a co-founder of Deep End Fitness, using breath work, extended swims, and CO2 tolerance building. The video emphasizes the psychological rather than physical difficulty of the test, exploring themes of mental control, managing panic responses underwater, and overcoming self-imposed limitations. Through coaching that reframes the experience as manageable rather than a survival situation, the participant successfully completes all components, achieving a breakthrough moment that restores confidence. The content targets individuals interested in military fitness challenges, mental resilience training, and underwater conditioning, while conveying broader lessons about confronting obstacles and strengthening mental fortitude alongside physical capability.
