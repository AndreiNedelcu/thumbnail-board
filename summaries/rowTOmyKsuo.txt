# YouTube Video Summary

A professional hairstylist with 20 years of experience in Southern California and Tennessee discusses current men's hair trends across different age groups. For clients aged 18-25, longer, messier, flowier styles with natural textures like waves and perms are increasingly popular, moving away from crisp, tidy cuts. The 25-35 demographic favors "lived-in" hair that appears effortless and unstyled, resembling celebrity looks rather than fresh haircuts. Across all ages, the dominant trend emphasizes avoiding any appearance of effort—people want messy, organic finishes without clean lines or heavy products. Specific popular styles include 1990s-inspired disheveled cuts, mullets, long layered hair, short messy buzzcuts, and the "American Psycho" style. Falling out of favor are broccoli cuts, designs, side parts, and anything requiring visible daily styling or maintenance. For beards, clients request faded edges instead of sharp lines for a more natural, low-maintenance look. The overarching shift reflects a broader cultural move toward working with natural hair textures, minimizing styling effort, and maintaining an effortless aesthetic that appears uncontrived. The stylist targets everyday clients across various professions who value practicality and authenticity in their appearance.
