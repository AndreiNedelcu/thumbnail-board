In 1973, French astrophysicist Pierre Lena orchestrated an unprecedented scientific mission by using the Concord supersonic jet to chase a solar eclipse across Africa, achieving the longest eclipse duration ever experienced by humans at 74 minutes. Working with legendary test pilot André Turcat and Aérospatiale, Lena equipped Concord with scientific instruments and modified its cabin with special windows to study the sun's corona—the superheated outer atmosphere normally invisible except during total eclipses. The mission required extraordinary precision: Concord had to intercept the moon's shadow traveling at over 2,200 kilometers per hour across the Sahara Desert, arriving at a specific point within one second of the calculated time and one kilometer of the planned location. Teams of international scientists aboard conducted multiple experiments, including measuring infrared radiation from the corona and discovering acoustic waves that cause it to pulse every five minutes. The video targets audiences interested in space exploration, aviation history, and scientific achievement, explaining the eclipse mechanics and technical challenges involved in this landmark achievement through animations and detailed flight planning narratives.
