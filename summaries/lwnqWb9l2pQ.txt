# Summary

This video investigates the viral conspiracy theory that The Simpsons predicts the future with uncanny accuracy. The creator examines famous examples like the show's 1998 prediction of the Higgs boson mass equation (15 years before its 2012 discovery), Trump's presidency, and various cultural events. Rather than attributing this to a shadowy "new world order," the analysis reveals a simpler explanation: The Simpsons' writing team consists of highly educated individuals with degrees in mathematics, physics, computer science, and related fields who deliberately research and incorporate accurate scientific and cultural details into their satirical jokes. When writers like David X. Cohen—who studied physics and published mathematical papers—craft equations or reference emerging trends, they're creating sophisticated humor rooted in real knowledge, not making supernatural predictions. The video employs statistical analysis to demonstrate that the show's apparent predictions are actually products of intelligent, informed writing combined with the law of large numbers: with thousands of jokes across multiple decades, some are bound to align with future events. The creator concludes that good satire about American culture naturally reflects real trends and emerging issues, making it seem prescient when those trends later materialize.
