# Summary

This experimental video compares seven methods of growing potatoes to determine the most effective approach. The methods tested include: five-gallon buckets, grow bags, traditional trenching, single potatoes planted at various depths (6 inches and 12 inches deep), cut potato halves, and the Ruth Stout method (potatoes covered with straw rather than buried). Over several months, the growers tracked plant development, then harvested and weighed the yields from each method. Results showed the five-gallon bucket without drainage holes performed poorly (0.6 oz), while the grow bag yielded 12.1 oz from three potatoes. The Ruth Stout method produced 2 lb 13 oz but faced pest damage. The traditional trenched method yielded the most total potatoes at 11 lb from five plants. However, a single potato planted 12 inches deep outperformed on a per-plant basis with 3 lb 9.2 oz. The video targets home gardeners seeking practical growing advice, evaluating trade-offs between yield, effort, and crop quality across different planting techniques.
