# Summary

This is a classic Thai horror folktale about Mak, a soldier who returns home from war in 1850s rural Thailand after a year away, only to find his reunion with his wife Mae Nak and newborn son increasingly unsettling. While initially thrilled by their emotional reunion and domestic bliss, Mak grows disturbed by his wife's paranoid behavior—she refuses to let him leave the house and claims villagers are spreading rumors about her. When a fruit stand owner reacts strangely to learning Mak's wife is home, and later a Buddhist monk warns him that his wife has been terrorizing villagers, Mak becomes suspicious despite seeing no evidence of her leaving the house. When Mak investigates further, he discovers the fruit stand owner brutally murdered, and upon returning home, observes his wife behaving strangely on the balcony in ways that suggest something deeply wrong. The narrative is presented as a campfire story targeting audiences interested in supernatural folklore and psychological horror, building tension through unreliable perceptions and mounting dread as reality becomes increasingly questionable.
