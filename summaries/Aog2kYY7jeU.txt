# Summary

This review covers the Xiaomi 15, a compact flagship Android phone that addresses traditional small-phone compromises. The reviewer praises its design—featuring a matte finish, curved edges, and minimal bezels comparable to iPhone—and highlights its impressive 5,240mAh battery (larger than the Samsung S25 Ultra) paired with the efficient Snapdragon 8 Elite chipset. The display offers 3,200-nit peak brightness and a 12-bit color panel. The camera system, built on Leica collaboration, provides an engaging shooting experience with fun filters, editing tools, and AI-powered pixel generation features, though video recording is inconsistent. Hyper OS 2 software improvements include refined camera shortcuts, side docks, haptic feedback, and customizable controls, though widget organization and gallery app syncing issues persist. Battery life easily lasts a full day with heavy use. Notable drawbacks include significant heat generation during 90W fast charging, potential thermal management concerns, and an alphabetically disorganized app widget system. The phone targets photography enthusiasts and compact-phone advocates who prioritize camera experience and premium build over absolute performance, making it arguably the best small Android option available.
