# Summary

This video examines five of the world's most difficult exams, analyzing their structure, question types, and why they're so challenging. The U.S. Medical Licensing Examination (USMLE) is a three-step process spanning years, with Step 3 alone lasting 18 hours across two days, covering 412 multiple choice questions and patient simulations. India's JEE (engineering entrance exam) sees only 2.5% of 1.5 million test-takers ultimately qualify for elite IIT admission, featuring complex physics, chemistry, and mathematics problems designed to eliminate rather than select candidates. China's Gaokao, taken by 13 million high school students annually, determines university placement through a single yearly administration with science and arts tracks covering subjects like physics and chemistry. Oxford's All Souls Fellowship Exam consists of twelve hours of essay-based questions across four papers, with only a 1.33% acceptance rate, testing subjective thinking on abstract topics like "Can an Institution Care?" Finally, the Putnam Mathematical Competition stands out for its median score of zero, challenging 4,000-5,000 of North America's brightest undergraduates with twelve problems requiring exceptional mathematical intuition. The video targets students and educators interested in understanding global academic rigor and competitive exam structures.
