# Summary

This video investigates an internet mystery surrounding where massive quantities of glitter are being used, which originated from a 2018 New York Times article. The creators journey to uncover the truth by contacting journalists, glitter company managers, and conspiracy theorists who have proposed various theories—from boat paint to military radar chaff to cosmetics. The investigation leads them to Meadowbrook Inventions Incorporated and Henry Rushman, whose father accidentally invented modern glitter while working on precision metal-cutting machines for the Manhattan Project during World War II. Through an exclusive farm visit and interview, they discover that glitter companies are actually precision-cutting specialists that have supplied materials to the U.S. military and government contractors for decades, producing everything from radar chaff and airplane brake components to rocket fuel pellets and security features on currency. The mystery isn't fully resolved—while multiple industrial uses are confirmed, the single largest buyer remains unclear—but the video reveals that glitter's true significance lies in its military and aerospace applications rather than consumer cosmetics, explaining why manufacturers have maintained secrecy around the industry.
