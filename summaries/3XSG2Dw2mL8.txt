# Summary

This video examines how climate denial rhetoric has fundamentally shifted over the past decade. While explicit denials that climate change exists or is human-caused have decreased, a new form of climate skepticism has emerged—what the creator calls "new denial"—which argues that solutions are ineffective, too expensive, or that humanity is "doomed" regardless of action. Drawing on a Center for Countering Digital Hate report analyzing over 12,000 YouTube videos, the analysis shows that "new denial" claims increased from 35% to 70% of climate skeptic content between 2018 and 2023, often originating from the same creators who previously promoted outright climate denial. The video targets a general audience interested in understanding online misinformation and climate discourse, arguing that this shift is strategically motivated by free-market ideology and resistance to government regulation rather than scientific evidence. The creator emphasizes that this new doomerism—spreading hopelessness about climate action—is particularly dangerous because it discourages policy support at a critical moment, making it harder to refute through personal experience than old-style denial. The video advocates for climate pessimism (realistic concern paired with action) while rejecting climate doomerism (fatalistic surrender).
