# Summary

This video analyzes the historical and contemporary costs of constructing the White House, starting from its original 1792 build at $232,300 (equivalent to $7.6 million today) and tracing how expansions under various administrations—particularly President Truman's major structural overhaul—increased expenses dramatically. The analysis reveals that modern construction in Washington DC costs $500-$1,000 per square foot, but the true expense comes from security measures: the 13-foot reinforced, blast-resistant fence alone costs $64 million, bulletproof windows on 147 windows cost $1 million, and rumored underground tunnels beneath DC allegedly cost $376 million. Additional costs include a $50 million renovation of the Situation Room, a $10 million Rose Garden, and $1.1 million for historical amenities like bowling alleys and movie theaters. The video targets general audiences curious about American history, government infrastructure, and real estate valuations, ultimately concluding that rebuilding the White House today would cost approximately $500 million based on a 2017 Zillow study adjusted for inflation.
