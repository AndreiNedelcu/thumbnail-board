# Summary

On June 27, 2017, a devastating cyberattack called NotPetya brought much of Ukraine's economy to a standstill and spread globally, affecting major international corporations like Maersk, FedEx, and pharmaceutical companies. The attack, attributed to Russia's GRU military intelligence agency and its Sandworm hacking unit, exploited a Ukrainian tax software called MeDoc that was installed on computers nationwide. NotPetya used stolen NSA exploits (Eternal Blue and Eternal Romance) and a credential-stealing tool (Mimikatz) to spread rapidly across networks, encrypting files and the master boot record—making it a destructive wiper masquerading as ransomware. Researchers discovered a kill switch: the malware checked for a file named perf_c and would abort if found. The video traces NotPetya's origins through Russia's historical cyber operations, explains the technical mechanisms of its spread, and chronicles the investigation by security researchers and Ukrainian authorities who discovered how the malware infiltrated networks via MeDoc updates. This incident demonstrated the first full-scale cyberwarfare attack on national infrastructure, targeting an audience interested in cybersecurity history, threat analysis, and geopolitical cyber conflict.
