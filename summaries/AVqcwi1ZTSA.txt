This video documents an undercover investigation into influencer scam promotion, specifically targeting MMA fighter Dillon Danis, who had publicly criticized Logan Paul for scamming people while himself promoting fraudulent cryptocurrency projects. The creator orchestrates an elaborate scheme involving a fake NFT platform called "sour.gg" (designed to mirror the notorious CryptoZoo project) and uses an associate to pose as a legitimate NFT salesman approaching Danis for promotional services. The investigation reveals that Danis agreed to promote the fake project for $5,000 per post with minimal due diligence, demonstrating the hypocrisy of influencers who condemn scams while actively participating in them for payment. Throughout the video, researchers uncover over 20 deleted scam promotions from Danis's Twitter history. The ultimate payoff involves paying Danis to post a link that initially directs to the fake landing page but then switches to a website documenting all his previous fraudulent promotions, effectively having him inadvertently expose himself to his followers. The video targets audiences interested in cryptocurrency fraud, influencer accountability, and investigative journalism, exposing how influencers perform minimal due diligence when accepting paid promotions.
