Samuel Rondo, a self-taught coder who transitioned from being an optician, shares his strategy for building profitable SaaS applications. Rather than pursuing entirely novel concepts, he identifies already-successful products and improves them incrementally—a philosophy he demonstrates with three apps generating $35,000 monthly combined revenue: Usimus (LinkedIn scraping tool), Story Short (AI video generator), and Capacity (AI coding tool). His validation framework relies on concrete signals like MRR screenshots, traffic source analysis via Ahrefs, and personal product enjoyment. He emphasizes finding ideas primarily through Twitter communities and determining market demand by examining whether growth stems from both paid ads and organic SEO, indicating authentic demand. His growth strategy progresses sequentially: launching paid ads on Google and Meta for rapid validation, building SEO once traction appears, leveraging faceless YouTube channels for automated promotion, and operating affiliate programs to boost virality. The speaker targets aspiring indie developers and solopreneurs interested in building sustainable internet businesses without complex technical backgrounds, advocating for modern tech stacks like Next.js and Node.js combined with AI coding assistants to accelerate development.
