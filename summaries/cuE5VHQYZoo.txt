This social experiment surveys 100 gym-goers across independent, commercial, and elite bodybuilding facilities to understand attitudes toward anabolic steroid use. The creator investigates whether the widespread steroid promotion on social media—particularly through TikTok influencers like the "Tren Twins" and fitness creators—reflects actual gym culture or represents a skewed online narrative. The video examines how younger lifters (many in their late teens and early twenties) are influenced by images of chemically-enhanced athletes, discussing SARMs as a perceived safer alternative despite research showing comparable side effects to steroids. It covers serious health consequences including cardiovascular disease, liver damage, and sexual dysfunction, while featuring candid interviews with both natural lifters and those actively using testosterone and primobolan. The creator explores misconceptions about reaching natural muscle-building potential and how social media pressure drives consideration of performance-enhancing drugs among impressionable youth. Targeted at fitness enthusiasts, gym-goers, and those concerned with drug use in bodybuilding culture, the video balances education about steroid dangers with realistic documentation of how prevalent these substances have become in gym communities.
