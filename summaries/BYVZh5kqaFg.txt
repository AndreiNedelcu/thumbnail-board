# Summary

This video documents an ambitious three-year engineering project to break the world record for highest egg drop by launching a raw egg to the edge of space via weather balloon and rocket. The creator, drawing on experience from Mars rover landings, designs an increasingly complex system to protect the egg through extreme conditions: freezing temperatures, vacuum, supersonic speeds exceeding Mach 2, and a controlled descent to Earth. The project evolves from an initial precision-guided rocket design (abandoned after consulting NASA engineers who note the ethical implications) to a final system inspired by Curiosity rover landing technology, featuring thermal protection, parachute deployment, and airbag cushioning. The video follows multiple test attempts in California and the desert, detailing engineering failures, problem-solving iterations, and the integration challenges of combining independent rocket and balloon systems. It targets engineering enthusiasts, space exploration fans, and anyone interested in applied physics and creative problem-solving, while weaving in lessons about resilience, redundancy, and learning from failure.
