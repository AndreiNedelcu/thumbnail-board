# Summary

The upcoming Disney Snow White remake has become a cultural phenomenon for all the wrong reasons, accumulating significantly more dislikes than likes on its official trailer. The film represents a troubled production marked by multiple controversies, including casting decisions regarding dwarf actors, activist statements from lead actress Rachel Zegler, and creative direction that attempts to modernize the 1937 classic. The trailer reveals poor CGI quality, oversaturated colors, unconvincing creature designs, and artificial set pieces that undermine the production's $269 million budget—a figure that swelled due to costly reshoot decisions following public backlash. Lead actress Zegler has become a lightning rod for criticism following her inflammatory social media posts about recent political events, while villain actress Gal Gadot's performance appears stiff and disengaged. The broader production suffers from a disconnect between honoring the original animated film and creating a modern retelling, resulting in a confused narrative identity. Disney's delayed trailer release strategy seemingly aimed to minimize early ridicule, but online commentary has been brutal. Industry analysts predict the film will struggle to break even given its massive budget and damaged reputation, with the video's comment section becoming more entertaining than the movie itself appears destined to be.
