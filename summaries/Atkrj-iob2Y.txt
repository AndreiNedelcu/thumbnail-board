# Summary

This video documents a 60-day experiment keeping three wild-caught rain frogs, mythologized online as nearly impossible pets that typically die in captivity. The creator purchases the frogs for $650 through an exotic animal marketplace and immediately confronts multiple challenges: building an appropriate habitat with proper substrate, testing the frogs for deadly pathogens like chytrid fungus and ranavirus, and critically, getting them to eat. Rain frogs are wild-caught African species that remain buried for weeks and require specific conditions—including simulated rainfall—to trigger feeding behavior. The video chronicles the struggle to keep the smallest female alive as she refuses food for nearly a month before the creator discovers that rain-triggered feeding mimics their natural ecology. The content targets exotic pet enthusiasts and curious viewers interested in unconventional animal keeping, while also addressing ethical concerns about wild-caught animals and the misinformation spread online about these species. By successfully maintaining all three frogs through day 60, the creator demonstrates that with proper research and adaptation, these frogs can survive, though they remain challenging pets better suited to experienced keepers.
