# Video Summary

This narrative recounts the shocking 1984 case of Graham Backhouse, a struggling English dairy farmer whose life spiraled into a criminal nightmare. Already burdened by inheriting an unprofitable farm he never wanted and preferring writing to farming, Graham began receiving threatening letters in late March 1984. The threats escalated with a severed lamb's head and "You next" note on his fence post, yet police took minimal action. The situation became catastrophic on April 9th when a bomb detonated under the seat of Graham's car, severely injuring his wife Margaret with shrapnel wounds to her legs and hips. With his wife hospitalized, children sent away for safety, and police seemingly stalled in their investigation, Graham became convinced his neighbor Colin—with whom he'd feuded over property lines—was responsible. Growing increasingly paranoid and desperate for answers, Graham visited Colin's house to observe his reactions, finding his neighbor's suspicious neutrality suspicious. Days later, isolated at the farmhouse with no arrests made and feeling an imminent threat, Graham received a mysterious late-night visitor at his door, prompting him to trigger his police panic button as Detective Superintendent Tom Evans responded to the emergency call.
