# Summary

This video traces the history and cultural evolution of the sparkle emoji, from its creation in 1997 by Japanese designer Shigetaka Kurita for NTT DoCoMo's i-mode mobile internet service, through its adoption by Unicode in 2010, to its recent appropriation by AI companies as a marketing symbol. Originally designed as a visual affix in the Japanese manga-influenced visual language to convey cuteness, glamour, and beauty (kakkoii), the sparkle emoji took on completely different meanings when exported to Western audiences who lacked this cultural context. Without understanding its original purpose, Western users interpreted it through iconicity—viewing it as literal shininess or glitter—and used it decoratively online. As internet culture evolved toward sarcasm and irony, particularly among Gen Z, the emoji's meaning became flexible and context-dependent, allowing it to signify emphasis, drama, and excitement. The video explores how the emoji was subsequently "stolen" by AI companies around 2023, who used it to mark nearly every new feature, regardless of whether the feature actually involved artificial intelligence, transforming an ancient symbol of beauty into a corporate marketing tool for vaguely-defined AI products.
