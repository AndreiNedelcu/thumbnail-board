# Summary

This documentary examines the CIA's global network of secret prisons, known as "black sites," established after 9/11 to capture and interrogate suspected terrorists outside the legal system. Following George W. Bush's authorization, the CIA developed facilities across 54 countries—including Thailand, Poland, Romania, Lithuania, Afghanistan, and others—to detain and torture prisoners using "enhanced interrogation techniques" while avoiding international law and domestic legal oversight. The program involved 119 prisoners transferred through clandestine flight routes, with at least nine confirmed black sites operating at various times. The video details specific cases like Abu Zubaida and exposes how the CIA fabricated legal justifications through government lawyers and falsified flight records to conceal operations. Using extensive research, declassified documents, and Senate investigations, the analysis reveals that torture proved ineffective at gathering actionable intelligence, with officials often retroactively claiming success. The program ultimately damaged American credibility, contaminated evidence against major terrorism suspects (preventing trials), and ironically accomplished Al-Qaeda's strategic goal of exposing U.S. hypocrisy regarding human rights. The documentary targets viewers interested in post-9/11 foreign policy, government accountability, and investigative journalism exploring institutional failure and ethical violations.
