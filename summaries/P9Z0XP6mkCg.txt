In 2012, two British university students created one of the internet's most enduring hoaxes by adding a fictional inventor to Wikipedia: Alan McMasters, who supposedly invented the electric toaster in 1865. The prank began as a classroom joke but escalated dramatically when the fabricated article—complete with a doctored photograph—spread across mainstream media outlets including the BBC and Washington Times. Schools created memorial days honoring McMasters, museums credited him in exhibits, cooking shows dedicated dishes to him, and the Bank of England even nominated him for inclusion on the 50-pound note. The misinformation persisted for a decade through circular reporting, where journalists cited the fake Wikipedia article without verification, then those articles were used as sources to validate the original hoax. The video examines how a obviously manipulated image and absurdly detailed false biography—including a woman killed by his toaster and the invention of London Underground lighting—fooled volunteer Wikipedia editors and the public. It illustrates the dangers of misinformation online and emphasizes the importance of fact-checking, while also exploring how data brokers exploit personal information. The hoax was finally exposed in 2022 when a teenager noticed the suspicious photograph and triggered its removal, revealing lessons about information verification and institutional blind spots.
