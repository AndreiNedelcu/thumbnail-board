GitHub Copilot has transitioned to a token-based pricing model on June 1st, replacing traditional subscriptions with per-use charges that have sparked widespread user backlash. The new system charges users based on token consumption, with the Pro Plus plan ($39/month) and Max plan ($100/month) offering limited monthly credits that can deplete rapidly depending on model usage. The creator demonstrates the issue firsthand, exhausting their monthly credit allowance within five hours using Claude Opus 4.8, spending approximately $50 in a single day after just forty prompts. Screenshots from Twitter and Reddit showcase similar complaints from other users—some consuming fifty percent of monthly credits on single tasks, others canceling subscriptions entirely. GitHub has temporarily halted new sign-ups to manage the transition and introduced a "flex allotment" feature providing temporary bonus credits to cushion the impact, though this appears to be a temporary goodwill measure. The video targets developers and AI tool users concerned about subscription costs, highlighting how GitHub Copilot's value proposition has deteriorated significantly compared to its previous pricing structure, with speculation that other subscription services may follow suit with similar token-based models.
