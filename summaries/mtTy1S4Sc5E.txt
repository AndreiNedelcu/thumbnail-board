# Summary

This video analyzes the psychological descent of Franklin Saint from the TV series "Snowfall," examining how a man who accumulated 73 million dollars through drug dealing deteriorated into a morally bankrupt character after losing everything to his CIA handler. The analysis explores key psychological concepts including loss aversion, desensitization to violence, narcissism, and revenge psychology to explain Franklin's spiral. The video traces how Franklin's greed and pride overrode his relationships with Jerome (his uncle and father figure), his mother Cissy, his girlfriend Veronique, and his best friend Leon—all of whom attempted to pull him back from the brink. Through detailed scene breakdowns, the creator demonstrates how Franklin's obsession with reclaiming his stolen fortune and exacting revenge consumed him, leading him to betray family, become desensitized to murdering innocents, and ultimately lose everyone who loved him. The video positions Leon as a contrasting character whose redemption arc opposes Franklin's corruption, targeting viewers interested in character analysis, psychological examination of television narratives, and discussions of how trauma and materialism corrupt moral character.
