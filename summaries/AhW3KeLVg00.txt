# Summary

The creator attempts to scroll to the absolute bottom of YouTube Shorts while analyzing the recommendation algorithm's behavior on a blank slate account using incognito mode and cleared history. Beyond simply reaching the end, the video documents what content YouTube serves to an unknown user and whether any of the creator's own videos appear in the feed within the first hundred videos. Throughout the journey, the creator observes recurring patterns: extensive family content, relationship and couple videos, pranks, preadolescent demographics, and demographic-specific recommendations that gradually evolve. The video functions as a meta-analysis of YouTube's algorithmic suggestion system, revealing how the platform tailors content to inferred user profiles based on viewing patterns. The creator discovers that YouTube Shorts heavily targets younger audiences, particularly teenage girls and family-oriented content creators, while unexpectedly avoiding content aligned with the creator's actual niche. This exploration targets viewers curious about algorithmic mechanics, YouTube's recommendation infrastructure, content discovery processes, and internet culture analysis.
