# Summary

This documentary traces Tim Cook's transformation of Apple from a struggling company into the world's most successful corporation, tracing his career from IBM through Compaq before joining Apple in 1998. After Steve Jobs' return and the company's near-bankruptcy, Cook proved invaluable as Chief Operating Officer, revolutionizing Apple's supply chain and operations through disciplined logistics and strategic partnerships. When Jobs died in 2011, he personally selected Cook as CEO—a controversial choice given Cook's contrasting reserved, data-driven personality versus Jobs' charismatic visionary approach. Initially dismissed as an uninspiring "accountant," Cook faced skepticism that innovation had ended with Jobs. However, the 2014 iPhone 6 launch marked Apple's turning point, demonstrating Cook's distinct philosophy: understanding global customer desires rather than imposing a singular vision. This event introduced the iPhone 6 Plus (contradicting Jobs' one-handed design doctrine), Apple Pay, and the Apple Watch—products that established Apple's dominance in services and wearables. The documentary argues that Cook's greatest strength lay not in creating products but in executing flawlessly, maintaining Apple's ecosystem, and expanding its market reach globally, ultimately proving that Apple's future extended far beyond Jobs' direct involvement.
