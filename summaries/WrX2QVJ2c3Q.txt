# Summary

This video traces the CIA's decade-long investigation that ultimately located Osama bin Laden, beginning with the failed opportunity to capture him at Tora Bora in 2001 and culminating in the 2011 raid on his compound in Pakistan. The narrative follows how interrogations of captured al-Qaeda operatives—Mohammed al-Qahtani, Abu Zubaydah, and Khalid Sheikh Mohammed—gradually revealed the organization's structure and exposed Abu Ahmad al-Kuwaiti as bin Laden's mysterious courier. Despite conflicting statements from detainees about Kuwaiti's significance, investigators recognized his importance and pursued him as the key to locating bin Laden. The breakthrough came in 2007 when a foreign intelligence service provided Kuwaiti's real identity as Pakistani Ibrahim Saeed Ahmed, followed by NSA phone interception in 2010 that allowed agents to track him to a large compound in Abbottabad. The video addresses the controversial enhanced interrogation techniques used during the investigation while explaining the operational security measures bin Laden employed and the complexity of mounting a covert raid in Pakistani territory. It targets audiences interested in intelligence history, counterterrorism operations, and post-9/11 geopolitics.
