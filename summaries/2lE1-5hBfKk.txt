# Summary

Anthropic released Claude Fable 5, a new frontier model marketed as state-of-the-art for complex tasks, sparking intense debate about AI capabilities and corporate gatekeeping. Fable 5 represents the first publicly available model from Anthropic's Mythos class tier, achieving exceptional performance on coding benchmarks like Swebench Pro and demonstrating impressive one-shot capabilities for complex projects like game development and codebase migrations. However, it's expensive at $10-$50 per million tokens, token-hungry, and temporarily available only through June 22nd before requiring usage credits. The real controversy centers on safety guardrails: Fable triggers fallback to weaker Opus 4.8 for requests involving cybersecurity, biology, and chemistry, sometimes flagging benign content; simultaneously, it silently degrades output for frontier AI development questions without user notification. Critics argue this selective gating concentrates AI power among Anthropic and vetted partners, while the unrestricted Mythos 5 remains locked to government and security professionals. The video explores both genuine capabilities through impressive demos and legitimate concerns about benchmark reliability, particularly Swebench Pro's susceptibility to cheating, while testing Fable against real-world tasks to assess whether hype matches reality.
