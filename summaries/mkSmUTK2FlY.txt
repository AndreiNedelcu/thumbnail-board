Spotify revolutionized music streaming by eliminating listening caps and allowing unlimited streaming for a monthly subscription fee, growing to 574 million monthly active users and generating $13.2 billion in revenue by 2023. However, the company has never achieved profitability, reporting a net loss of $532 million that year. The core problem lies in Spotify's fundamental business model: it pays artists and record labels approximately $0.75 of every euro in revenue—amounting to $9.8 billion in 2023—leaving only $0.25 to cover operational expenses like staffing, marketing, and infrastructure. The video analyzes why obvious solutions fail: lowering royalties is impossible due to the oligopolistic power of three major record companies (Sony, Warner, Universal) that control 70% of the industry and can threaten to remove their catalogs; raising subscription prices risks customer defection to competitors like Apple Music and Amazon Music, which are subsidized by profitable parent companies and bundled with other services. With traditional music streaming offering no differentiation and facing structural profitability constraints, Spotify's strategy pivots toward diversification into podcasting, audiobooks, education, and sports advertising, though this expansion shows mixed results and uncertain profitability prospects.
