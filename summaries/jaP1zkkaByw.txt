A keyboard builder documents the meticulous three-year customization process of a premium mechanical keyboard designed for tech reviewer MKBHD, implementing five major modifications to achieve optimal sound and functionality. The upgrades include lubing and filming custom switches—specifically upgraded Cherry MX Brown variants—for smoothness and reduced noise, replacing stock stabilizers with higher-quality TX AP stabilizers to eliminate rattling, applying tape between the aluminum case layers to prevent hollow resonance, adding strategic foam dampening for refined acoustics, and sourcing custom keycaps with Mac OS compatibility. The builder selected the Kyron Q1 Pro as the base platform because it uniquely offers wireless connectivity, backlighting, arrow keys, and native Mac OS media controls—essential requirements for the intended user. Additional personalization includes gloss black accents matching MKBHD's aesthetic and remapping a customizable knob to control volume and brightness since ideal Mac-compatible keycaps proved unavailable. The video targets mechanical keyboard enthusiasts and DIY tech customizers interested in deep-dive build documentation, component sourcing strategies, and the intricate modifications that transform baseline keyboards into high-performance instruments tailored to specific user needs.
