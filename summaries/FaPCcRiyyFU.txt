# Summary

This video offers a framework for evaluating whether to stay in or leave a relationship by reframing the decision around self-respect rather than blame. The presenter argues that relationships should increase your "stats"—health, wealth, happiness, career goals, relationships with others, and social standing—and that the real issue isn't whether a partner is inherently "toxic," but whether the relationship makes you respect yourself more or less. Key concepts include the "three Fs cycle" (fight, have sex, forget), the distinction between preferences and expectations, and the idea that people train their partners through tolerance and reinforcement. The presenter emphasizes that most relationship problems stem from lack of clear communication about expectations and that individuals must take responsibility for their choices rather than blame their partner. The audience appears to be people in troubled relationships seeking guidance on whether to stay or leave, as well as those entering new relationships wanting to avoid similar patterns. The core message is that maintaining self-respect should be the primary metric for relationship decisions.
