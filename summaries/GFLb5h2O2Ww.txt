This video explores tuberculosis, a bacterial infection caused by Mycobacterium tuberculosis that has killed over a billion people in the last 200 years—more than all wars and natural disasters combined. The speaker explains why TB, nicknamed "the white death," remains humanity's deadliest infectious disease despite being curable with modern antibiotics. The video details TB's remarkable evolutionary adaptation: its thick waxy cell coat protects it from immune system attacks, it hijacks macrophage cells to replicate extremely slowly, and it forms granulomas that shield bacteria from both immunity and medication. The presentation distinguishes between latent TB, which silently infects roughly one in four humans globally, and active TB, which spreads through respiratory droplets and causes severe lung damage. The video addresses why TB persists as a global health crisis despite available treatments—poor distribution of antibiotics, development of drug-resistant strains, lack of profit incentives for new drug research, and insufficient public awareness. The target audience appears to be general educational viewers interested in science, public health, and global issues, presented through an accessible yet detailed explanation of microbiology and epidemiology.
