# Summary

This video explores three business models that monetize free, publicly available information and are ubiquitous yet often overlooked. The first example is Oasis, an app generating hundreds of thousands in annual revenue by aggregating free water quality data from government sources, making it accessible and adding independent testing services through a $45 annual subscription. The second is labor law poster companies making millions annually by mailing intimidating compliance notices and selling posters that are actually available for free on government websites—they succeed by solving the convenience problem and reducing friction. The third business discussed is barcodes, invented decades before finding practical application in retail through the combination of laser scanning technology and grocery stores' inventory management challenges. The broader point emphasizes that lucrative business opportunities are hidden in plain sight everywhere; success often comes not from revolutionary ideas but from aggregating free data, simplifying access, reducing friction, or solving compliance headaches. The video targets aspiring entrepreneurs and side-hustlers by demonstrating that million-dollar businesses can emerge from identifying small inefficiencies in everyday systems and providing marginally better solutions to widespread problems.
