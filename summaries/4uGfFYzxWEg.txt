This video examines the 2001 "Who Wants to Be a Millionaire" scandal involving Major Charles Ingram, who won £1 million under suspicious circumstances, focusing on whether he cheated or was falsely convicted. The analysis dissects the evidence against Ingram—primarily 192 recorded coughs in the studio suspiciously timed with correct answers—alongside counter-arguments from skeptics and Ingram's supporters who argue the case represents trial by media rather than solid forensic proof. The video also explores a parallel storyline about the Consortium, an underground organization that systematically exploited the show from 2002-2007 through sophisticated schemes involving rapid phone dialing, call rerouting, and fake friends to help contestants win £4.2 million (26% of all winnings that period) without facing legal consequences. Through statistical analysis, eyewitness accounts, and examination of both the Ingram trial and the Consortium's operations, the video presents a nuanced investigation targeting viewers interested in true crime, game show history, fraud cases, and the intersection of media sensationalism and justice, questioning whether the conviction was warranted or a miscarriage of justice.
