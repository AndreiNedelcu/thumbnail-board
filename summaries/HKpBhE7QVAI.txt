This project demonstrates a functioning volumetric display built around a spinning LED matrix that creates 3D images through persistence of vision. The creator solved the typical engineering challenges of slip rings and power delivery by rotating all components together, including the motor and battery, eliminating complex mechanical connections. The display uses a CD drive motor to spin an LED array at the top of a compact, tealight-sized structure, with supporting electronics mounted below. The prototype successfully renders test content including wireframe cubes, pre-rendered fluid simulations, and flame effects visible from multiple angles. The device achieves volumetric display by illuminating LEDs at precise moments during rotation to build up 3D voxel structures. Future improvements focus on increasing LED count for higher resolution, better centering of LEDs on the rotation axis to eliminate double illumination of pixels, and mechanical refinements like adding weights for stability and a protective transparent cover. While the current prototype is admittedly hastily constructed, it proves the concept's viability and would appeal to makers, electronics enthusiasts, and researchers interested in display technology and creative engineering solutions.
