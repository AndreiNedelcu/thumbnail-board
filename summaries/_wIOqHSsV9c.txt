# Video Summary

This video examines how the United States has orchestrated successful coups to remove foreign leaders throughout history, focusing on cases involving direct U.S. government involvement and concrete evidence of American participation. The analysis begins with Hawaii's 1893 overthrow of Queen Liliuokalani by American sugar interests, then traces the pattern through the Spanish-American War era's manipulation of independence movements in Cuba, Puerto Rico, and the Philippines. The video details U.S. interventions in Nicaragua, Honduras, and explains how the CIA's establishment revolutionized coup methodology during the Cold War. Key examples include the 1953 Iran coup removing Mohammad Mosaddegh to protect British oil interests, the 1954 Guatemala operation defending American banana companies, the 1960 Congo coup against Patrice Lumumba amid Cold War anxiety, and 1963 Vietnam's assassination of President Ngo Dinh Diem. Throughout these cases, the U.S. employs consistent tactics: propaganda, bribery, military support, and staged uprisings—often justifying interventions through anticommunist rhetoric while actually protecting corporate and geopolitical interests. The video argues that powerful nations circumvent democratic processes and international law when pursuing their strategic objectives.
