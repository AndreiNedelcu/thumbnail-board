# Summary

Y Combinator partners discuss the current state of AI startups, emphasizing that approximately 50% of their recent batch companies work with large language models—not because YC favors AI, but because ambitious founders recognize it as a genuine opportunity for building billion-dollar companies. The conversation covers key distinctions between viable AI businesses and "tarpits" (attractive-sounding ideas that ultimately fail), with "boring" ideas like workflow automation and government contract processing emerging as surprisingly valuable. They highlight that successful AI applications focus on concrete problem-solving—automating repetitive information processing tasks—rather than chat-based interfaces that require users to adapt their behavior. The partners address challenges including AI co-pilot saturation, data privacy concerns driving specialized security tools like Prompt Armor, and the viability of fine-tuned open-source models for specific domains. They emphasize that cost alone isn't sustainable competitive advantage as model prices decline, and that customization for private datasets and domain-specific applications hold more promise. The discussion targets aspiring founders, revealing that early-stage AI startups are positioning themselves to capitalize on a "once-in-a-lifetime opportunity" where no one yet has years of language model experience, creating an unusually level playing field.
