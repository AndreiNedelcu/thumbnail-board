# Summary

This documentary examines how a Balkan drug cartel systematically infiltrated global shipping infrastructure, particularly targeting Mediterranean Shipping Company (MSC), the world's largest shipping enterprise, to smuggle cocaine from South America to Europe. The investigation centers on the June 2019 seizure of 20 tons of cocaine aboard the MSC Guyana in Philadelphia—the largest drug bust in U.S. history—worth over one billion dollars. The film reveals how trafficking networks recruited sailors, especially from Montenegro where MSC employs over a third of available seafarers, and exploited the company's refrigerated container systems and expansion of the Panama Canal route. Authorities discovered that cocaine was loaded onto ships in open ocean using sophisticated coordination by senior crew members like the chief mate, then distributed through major European ports like Antwerp and Rotterdam where only 1.5% of containers are scanned. The documentary investigates MSC's responsibility despite not directly smuggling drugs, explores the tension between global trade efficiency and security, and details the resulting 600-million-dollar fine and ongoing civil forfeiture case that could reshape shipping industry accountability. The film targets audiences interested in international crime, globalization's vulnerabilities, and corporate responsibility in supply chains.
